Exchange & Transfers: how it works in Kazakhstan — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Exchange & Transfers» in Kazakhstan — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Currency exchange at Kazakhstan airports for rubles to tenge is generally unfavorable. It's recommended to exchange only a small amount for immediate transport needs and conduct the majority of currency exchange in the city for better rates. Check kurs.kz for current rates. Alternatively, services like Yandex.Taxi may accept direct payments from Russian bank cards.
  • For currency exchange in Kazakhstan, it is generally recommended to bring US dollars, as they often provide a better exchange rate than Russian rubles. Exchanging currency in the city is usually more advantageous than at the airport. Small denominations of USD/EUR may be difficult to find in Astana.
  • Yandex.Taxi in Kazakhstan can accept payments directly from Russian bank cards, eliminating the immediate need for currency exchange upon arrival for transportation services.
  • For non-residents of Kazakhstan (specifically Russian citizens) seeking to use local banks (BCK, Freedom Finance, Kaspi) for SWIFT transfers to Europe, there is no inherent restriction based on citizenship. However, actual success often depends on internal compliance policies. It is recommended to test the system by transferring a small amount first to check for potential blocks or transaction limits before relying on the account for major expenses like residence permit proof of funds.
  • To withdraw rubles in Kazakhstan, consider these options: 1. **Cash withdrawal from Mir cards:** Possible, but the exchange rate may not be favorable. 2. **VTB Kazakhstan ATMs:** May offer better rates than general ATMs. 3. **Exchanging rubles for local currency:** Exchanging physical rubles often yields a better rate than direct card withdrawals. 4. **Bank transfers:** Direct card-to-card transfers from Russia may be problematic. Services like 'Zolotaya Korona' might be a viable alternative for fund transfers.
  • Freedom Finance (Kazakhstan) SWIFT transfers face strict verification. Users may be required to provide extensive documentation for source of funds (salary statements, property sale contracts, inter-account transaction proofs) and family relations (marriage certificates) for both sender and spouse. There are reported issues with manual input fields for document numbers. Freedom Finance's official policy states SWIFT to TRY is available (except for Russia, Belarus, sanctioned entities), with commissions from 50,000 to 450,000 KZT. Payments to Russia/Belarus are prohibited. All transfers are subject to individual verification, and additional documents may be requested. For Russian Federation residents, CNY transfers are recommended.
  • To transfer funds from Raiffeisen Bank (Russia) to Bank CenterCredit (BCC) in Kazakhstan, it is likely necessary to modify the payment details in the transfer order, specifically the beneficiary account information, while ensuring other details remain correct. Further clarification with both banks might be needed for specific instructions.
  • Yandex Go functions in Kazakhstan with Russian bank cards linked to the user's account. This allows for seamless payments in Kazakhstan while being debited in Russian rubles. It is an efficient solution for travelers arriving at airports without local currency.
  • Freedom Finance Kazakhstan cards can be renewed for a long term (e.g., until age 31). While initial card issuance might be restricted to Kazakhstan, renewals are possible remotely, potentially even in Russia. The mobile application is user-friendly, comparable to popular Russian banking apps, and facilitates easy transfers from Russian bank accounts.
  • After a Kaspi.kz card expires, the Kaspi.kz application should continue to function and display account balances correctly. However, online payments may no longer be possible. Funds can be withdrawn in person or transferred between accounts.
  • For favorable currency exchange rates (Russian Rubles to Kazakh Tenge), it is generally recommended to exchange the main sum of money in large cities of Kazakhstan such as Astana or Almaty. While it's possible to exchange a small amount in Russia for immediate travel needs, the rates are typically less favorable. Exchange rates can be checked online by searching for currency exchange offices in the respective cities.
  • When exchanging currency in Kazakhstan, it is generally recommended to bring US dollars, as they often offer a more favorable exchange rate compared to Russian rubles. Airport exchange rates are typically unfavorable. For transportation, services like Yandex.Taxi may accept direct payments from Russian bank cards.
