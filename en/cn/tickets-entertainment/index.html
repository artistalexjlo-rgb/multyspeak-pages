Tickets & Entertainment in China: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Tickets & Entertainment» in China — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • In cases of flight delays causing missed connections, particularly in transit hubs like Shanghai, airline ground staff (often Chinese carriers) may proactively rebook passengers onto the next available flight and assist with luggage transfer, as experienced with a 2-hour delay from Seoul. This indicates a system in place for managing disrupted transit itineraries, but ample layover time is always recommended.
  • Transit at Shanghai Pudong Airport (PVG) often requires inter-terminal transfer. Travelers should use the internal airport train rather than walking. Transit security can be thorough, and it is advised to remove power banks immediately. If booked on a single ticket, the airline is responsible for rebooking in case of delays.
  • Free transit hotels provided by Chinese airlines for long layovers are often located far from both the airport and the city center. Travelers wanting to explore the city should consider booking accommodations independently near the city center, as transit time between airports and downtown can exceed 1-2 hours.
  • Transit hotel eligibility for China Eastern Airlines depends on specific ticket fare categories and is not guaranteed for all long-duration connections. Passengers should check their eligibility via the airline's official app or website after booking. It is highly recommended to email the airline in advance or pre-register for transit services rather than relying on airport desk availability.
  • China Southern Airlines offers free transit hotel stays for long layovers in Guangzhou. Book the hotel via the airline's website in advance. Upon arrival, proceed to Exit 50 to locate the airline desk for transit hotel assistance. Passengers can also pre-select special meal types (seafood, low-calorie, etc.) which are typically served first.
  • China Eastern Airlines may offer compensation for significant flight delays, even on flights with technical stops (e.g., Xi'an to Sydney via Nanjing). In a reported case, a delay from 7 PM to 1 AM resulted in a 160 AUD compensation. Passengers experiencing long delays may be granted free lounge access upon request. Compensation can be complex if a passenger is not in the airline's loyalty system, sometimes requiring assistance from other passengers or representatives to process the payout (e.g., transferred to a third-party account and then converted).
  • When booking multi-leg flights with transit in China, use unified bookings under a single PNR. If a third-party site reports 'partial cancellation' or synchronization issues, immediately contact the airline directly using the PNR to verify the status in the airline's own system, bypassing the aggregator's support if unresponsive.
  • At Beijing Daxing International Airport (PKX), transit passengers connecting between international flights (e.g., China Eastern/Southern and S7) can perform transfer check-in and luggage re-registration directly inside the airside transit zone at the transfer desks (located on the right side of the transfer area). Exiting to the public landside area or passing through Chinese border control is not required, even if automated booking alerts claim "self-transfer required".
  • Online check-in for Air China from Russia often fails via standard methods. Users must pass identity verification in the app by uploading passport data. Even with successful online check-in, physical check-in at the airport counter is usually required for baggage drop or final boarding pass printing.
  • In an airline PNR (Passenger Name Record), segments must be flown in the sequence they are booked. Missing one flight segment (no-show) triggers an automatic cancellation of all subsequent flights in the same reservation. This policy applies strictly to international flights and is not negotiable.
  • For China Eastern Airlines, the last flight segment (e.g., Shanghai-Moscow) often uses a different ticket number. If the segment is missing on the airline's website, try adding '1' to the last digit of the ticket number. Online check-in is typically only available 24 hours prior. Always confirm if baggage is checked through to the final destination to manage liquid restrictions.
  • Travel advice for China transit: 1. Always prioritize single-ticket bookings for protection against delays. 2. Follow 'International Transfer' signage, not exit signs. 3. Use offline translators for communication. 4. If a connection is missed on a single booking, the airline is responsible for rebooking. 5. Travel insurance is recommended to cover incidental delays.
  • Travelers with layovers in Beijing, specifically at Capital International Airport (PEK), may have the opportunity to take tours to the Great Wall. Information suggests there is a designated meeting point for these tours, operating on a first-come, first-served basis.
