Documents & Visa: how it works in Russia — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Russia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • To manage Russian military conscription for individuals under 18 residing abroad: 1. **Timing:** Address conscription status before the 18th birthday to avoid future entry issues into Russia. 2. **De-registration:** Initiate de-registration from Russian residence (apartment) by potentially using a foreign address. 3. **Finnish Documentation:** Obtain proof of residency in Finland, such as a certificate from DVV (Digital and Population Data Services Agency). 4. **Russian Military Commissariat:** De-register with the relevant Russian military commissariat. Process can be lengthy. 5. **Legal Counsel:** Consider consulting Russian legal specialists for complex cases, though it might be deemed unnecessary if the individual has minimal ties to Russia and strong foreign residency. 6. **Local Military Commissariat:** Be prepared for specific requirements and potential follow-ups at the local military commissariat in Russia.
  • The Honorary Consulate of Brazil in St. Petersburg does not process CPF (Cadastro de Pessoas Físicas) applications for individuals. Russian citizens needing a CPF must apply and retrieve it at the Brazilian Embassy in Moscow. This policy was confirmed as of summer 2025.
  • Brazilian citizens in Moscow can renew their passports following a specific procedure. Applications and forms can be completed in advance. However, scheduling an appointment for the new passport is only possible once the current passport reaches its one-year mark. The in-consulate processing time is approximately 40 minutes. It is recommended to prepare documents early but adhere to the scheduling timeline based on the current passport's anniversary.
  • When departing from Russia on an international flight (e.g., to Bali), an onward or return ticket to any third country is mandatory. Airlines (e.g., at Sheremetyevo Airport) strictly enforce this requirement at check-in, and failure to present one may result in denied boarding. While immigration in some destination or transit countries might not always check this, it is a prerequisite for entry into many countries and often required during the eVisa/VOA application process for Indonesia.
  • Visa application slots for Bulgaria at VFS Global centers in Russia (e.g., St. Petersburg, Moscow) are highly competitive and disappear very quickly. Slots can appear suddenly, sometimes lasting only a few minutes (e.g., observed from 18:58 to 19:04). Users often rely on automated bots to detect slot availability. Common issues include login errors (e.g., 403201), which can prevent manual booking within the short window. Slots are typically released on Mondays, and can be for both single and multiple applicants.
  • Requirements for unemployed individuals or housewives applying for a Bulgarian visa from Russia can vary. Some applicants, identifying as housewives without formal employment history, have successfully obtained visas without being required to submit a work record. Conversely, other reports suggest that applicants without current employment may be asked to provide an electronic work record (электронная трудовая) confirming their non-employment status, along with proof of property ownership or other financial ties. Applicants should be prepared for potential requests for additional documentation to demonstrate financial stability and intent to return.
  • An alternative, less formal method for a Russian citizen to change their surname in Russian documents after a foreign marriage (e.g., in Finland) is to apply for a name change based on 'personal desire' at a Multifunctional Centre (MFC) in Russia. This approach bypasses the need for an apostilled and translated foreign marriage certificate, saving time and cost. The MFC will issue a certificate of name change, which can then be used to update the Russian internal passport and subsequently the Russian foreign passport. This method treats the name change as an independent administrative action rather than a direct consequence of a foreign marriage.
  • German visa processing times in Russia, particularly for applications submitted via regional VisaMetric centers (e.g., Novosibirsk), can be significantly longer than initial estimates. While 2 weeks is sometimes quoted, actual times can extend to 18-22 days or even up to 45 working days during peak season (summer). This is influenced by holidays and the time required for document transshipment from regional centers to the German Embassy/Consulate in Moscow. The Embassy's internal review might take 5 days, but the overall process from application to passport collection can be much longer due to logistics.
  • For a Russian citizen who married in Finland and changed their surname, the official process to update Russian documents involves several steps. The Finnish marriage certificate must first be apostilled in Finland and then officially translated into Russian, with the translation notarized. With these documents, the individual travels to Russia to change their internal Russian passport. Subsequently, they can apply for a new Russian foreign passport at the Russian Consulate in Finland, presenting the updated internal passport and other required documents.
  • When booking visa appointments via VFS Global, common errors like 'session expired' are often due to high traffic or server load. Using Incognito mode, clearing browser cache, or switching devices/networks can resolve access issues. Slots often become available early in the morning (around 06:00 local time) due to cancellations by other applicants.
  • Brazilian citizens entering Russia visa-free via airports like DME (Domodedovo) should be prepared to present their passport, a return ticket, and proof of accommodation (e.g., hotel booking or an invitation letter) at border control. They may also be asked about the purpose of their visit. After clearing customs, travelers can proceed to the city via metro (where available from the airport).
  • Property owners can obtain an EGRN extract for visa purposes free of charge by generating it via the 'Gosuslugi' portal and requesting a printed copy with a stamp at the MFC, rather than paying for a new request at the counter.

  • To secure a Finnish visa application slot in Moscow, be aware that slots are released monthly between the 20th and 24th for the following month. These slots are taken very quickly. Excessive activity on the booking system may lead to a temporary ban. An alternative approach for specific cases, such as visiting close relatives, could be to directly contact the Finnish Embassy to inquire about appointment availability, though this method's current feasibility may vary.

  • Russian citizens residing abroad should notify the authorities about their residency status via the Gosuslugi portal. Specifically, military registration should be updated or suspended to avoid drafting notices while living abroad for extended periods. Notification of dual citizenship or foreign residency is typically managed through the Ministry of Internal Affairs (MVD) or via digital government services.
  • Booking Schengen visa slots from Russia (e.g., St. Petersburg) is highly challenging. The process is characterized by technical difficulties (e.g., account blocks, errors on the final application page) and quick depletion of available slots, often appearing as false availability. Furthermore, appointments, including those for VIP services, are subject to cancellation or rescheduling due to directives to reduce applicant numbers. Applicants are typically informed via email about cancellations and advised to contact the consulate or visa center via email to request earlier rebooking, especially if travel plans are already fixed. Submitting screenshots of failed booking attempts is unlikely to secure a slot.
  • For frequent travelers, especially Russian citizens, obtaining a second 10-year biometric passport is highly beneficial. When applying for a Schengen or other visa, the primary passport is held at the consulate for the duration of processing (which can be 1-3 months). A second passport allows individuals to travel to visa-free countries, go on business trips, or apply for other visas simultaneously, avoiding travel restrictions during visa processing periods. Russian citizens are legally permitted to hold two valid 10-year foreign passports concurrently.
  • Users attempting to secure visa application slots by frequently refreshing the booking page may experience account blocking due to 'suspicious activity'. The system might interpret rapid, repeated access as bot-like behavior. Resolution typically involves waiting for a period (e.g., 2-24 hours) or contacting customer support. Some sources suggest changing the IP address might help, but it's not always effective. Accounts blocked for this reason are usually unblocked automatically after some time.
  • Visa application processing times at Russian Visa Centers (e.g., for Bulgaria) vary significantly between regular and priority (VIP) halls. In the **Regular Hall**, despite having all documents prepared, applicants can experience long wait times, often 3 hours or more. This includes delays in being called, queues for fingerprinting, and payment processing. In the **Priority (VIP) Hall**, wait times are significantly reduced. For a family (e.g., 3 adults, 2 children), the entire process can take about 1.5 hours, with minimal delays for being called and no queues for additional services like photos, fingerprints, or payment. Documents are checked efficiently.
  • The process for obtaining documents through the embassy in Moscow involves several steps: application submission, review, notification for scheduling, and finally, an appointment. The total time frame can extend to approximately 4 business days. Applicants need to submit a request, wait for approval, and then schedule an appointment. It may not be feasible to complete the process within a short visit of 1.5 days. It is advisable to apply in advance and schedule an appointment for a date when the applicant will be available in Moscow.
  • For Russian applicants, the processing time for a Bulgarian C-visa currently averages 5-6 weeks, with some cases extending to 40-45 working days or even longer (up to 1.5 months). The maximum processing period can be up to 45 working days.
