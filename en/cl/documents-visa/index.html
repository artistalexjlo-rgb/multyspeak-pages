Documents & Visa: how it works in Chile — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Chile — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Police record certificate validity for Chilean residency applications: The certificate must be no older than 60 days at the exact moment of document submission to the migration office.
  • As of June 2026, there are no active legislative changes or new enforcement trends regarding the revocation or denial of citizenship for children born to tourist parents in Chile. The status quo remains stable.
  • Chile does not restrict the export of wine in personal luggage on departure. However, travelers must comply with the import limits of their destination country (e.g., Russia has a duty-free limit of 3 liters of alcohol per person).
  • Chilean birthright citizenship (jus soli) has become more restrictive for transient visitors. Current interpretation suggests that children born to parents holding only tourist status do not receive immediate citizenship/residency privileges. Typically, at least one parent must hold a residency status (other than tourist) for the child to acquire status, or the child must wait until age 18 to apply for citizenship.
  • Upon arrival at Santiago de Chile airport, immigration officials typically ask for the name of the hotel where the traveler will be staying and the duration of their visit. They do not usually ask for detailed travel plans or proof of onward travel for tourist entries, based on a specific group experience from Turkey.
  • To legalize documents or obtain a stamp at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Santiago, Chile, an appointment is not required. Visit the office located at Augustinas 580 on weekdays before 14:00. Instead of joining the main queue of migrants, approach the building from a different side where there are typically fewer people and a Ministry employee. Present your consular certificate with a certified translation and explain your need for legalization/stamp. You will be invited inside, and the process is usually completed within approximately 15 minutes. Ensure your certificate is consular and includes a certified translation.
  • When entering Chile, immigration officers typically ask about the purpose of your visit. A common and acceptable answer for tourists is 'Vacation' or 'Tourism'. This straightforward response helps facilitate the entry process.
  • For a foreigner marrying a Chilean citizen in Chile, the foreign spouse does not need a certificate of no marriage. The Chilean spouse only needs their birth certificate, which can be obtained online. Required documents for the ceremony include both spouses' passports, the foreigner's PDI slip from the airport, two witnesses, and an official translator if the foreigner does not speak Spanish.
  • In Chile, foreign nationals can enter as tourists and get married. After the marriage is registered, the spouse can apply for a Temporary Residence Permit (Residencia Temporal) based on family reunification (vinculo familiar). It is generally not necessary to marry abroad beforehand. The process for a minor child is separate; usually, after the parent receives residency, they apply for residency for the child as a dependent.
  • Marriage registration in Chile at Registro Civil: 1. Small towns often have no online queue and allow walk-in appointments. 2. Large cities typically require online booking well in advance. 3. Documentation: Passport and witnesses are primary; apostilles on marriage-related documents are often not required for the ceremony itself. 4. Criminal record checks for residency are handled via the Russian Consulate in Santiago and legalized at the Chilean Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
  • To apply for temporary residence in Chile based on family reunification (e.g., after marrying a Chilean citizen), the following documents are typically required: a valid passport, a certificate of no criminal record (ensure it's within its validity period, usually 60 days from translation/legalization), a passport-sized photo, and the marriage certificate. A fee will be required prior to the issuance of the residence permit.
  • To legalize documents for SERMIG in Chile: 1. Ensure the translation is notarized or consular-certified. 2. Legalize the document (with the attached translation) at the Chilean Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MINREL). 3. Services are available on weekdays before 14:00. Consular services can often provide certified translations upon request to streamline the process.
  • For a child who is a citizen of Chile, mandatory consent from the parent is required for exit. If traveling as a tourist, consent from the accompanying parent is generally sufficient. Some airlines might request consent from the non-traveling parent, but successful past travel without it indicates it may not be an issue. Chile itself usually has lenient policies regarding this.
  • When flying to Santiago, Chile (specifically via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines), passengers may or may not be asked for proof of onward travel or return tickets. While some individuals pass without checks, others (e.g., specific nationality groups) might be required to present such documentation. It indicates inconsistent enforcement of airline or immigration policies regarding proof of exit.

  • To apply for a new Russian international passport at the Russian Embassy in Santiago, Chile, you need to submit an application, your old passport, your Russian internal passport, and a payment receipt from Banco de Chile. The process can take several months, with reported waiting times ranging from 1.5 to 6 months, though some applicants received their passports in about 5 weeks. Check the embassy's official website for the most up-to-date list of required documents and procedures.
  • As of May 4th, 2026, online appointments are mandatory for Chilean immigration/consular services. Walk-in visits without prior booking are no longer an option.
  • The Certificate of No Criminal Record required for Chilean visa/residence is valid for 60 days. This period is often counted from the date of translation/legalization, not the issuance date. Ensure the original certificate and its translation are bound and stamped as a single document, preferably legalized at the consulate, to avoid issues.
  • Chilean exit regulations for minors are strict. If a minor (under 18) travels alone from Chile, parental consent must comply with local Chilean regulations, not just the country of citizenship. Airlines often mandate an 'unaccompanied minor' service (menor no acompañado) for adolescents, which must be arranged during ticket purchase. Standard notarized documents from other countries may not be recognized by Chilean immigration officers.
  • Entry requirements for Easter Island: 1. Mandatory online form completion listing an authorized hotel (private stays are restricted). 2. Return ticket is mandatory. 3. PDI (PDI document provided upon arrival in Chile) must be kept and presented at departure. Flights from Santiago to Easter Island take approximately 4.5 hours. Local weather has low temperature variance (day/night), but intense UV radiation.
  • Proving income for Chilean residency via cryptocurrency trading (Binance, etc.) is difficult due to strict documentation requirements. Proving income via rental property earnings is a verified and successful pathway for obtaining residency.
  • Access to Easter Island (Rapa Nui) is regulated. Travelers must present a return ticket, proof of accommodation (reservation at a registered tourism provider or a letter of invitation from a resident), and complete the FUI (Single Entry Form) prior to boarding. Strict environmental regulations apply.
  • Residency application for Chile requires submitting original documents to the Chilean consulate in the applicant's home country (e.g., Moscow). The process can take approximately one year and may involve international travel to secure required documentation.
  • The Tarjeta de Turismo (PDI) issued upon entry to Chile is often required in physical paper form at land border crossings (e.g., Chile-Bolivia, Patagonia borders). While airports may have digital records, it is highly recommended to keep the physical paper receipt issued at the border/airport entry to ensure smooth immigration processing upon departure.
  • To obtain a Chilean temporary residence permit (visado de residencia) as a pregnant woman, one can apply for a humanitarian visa. The process and timeline can vary. It is advisable to consult official Chilean immigration websites or contact the relevant authorities for the most up-to-date requirements and processing times.
  • To legalize and find work in Chile as a foreigner, knowledge of Spanish is highly recommended. Limited options exist without it, primarily for specialists or those employed by international companies. Without Spanish proficiency, securing legal status and employment is significantly more challenging.
  • To obtain Chilean citizenship through marriage, applicants generally need to reside in the country for two continuous years. Key documents required for family reunification/marriage visa applications include a foreign passport, a certificate of no criminal record (valid for 2 months, requiring certification by the Chilean Ministry of Foreign Affairs), a marriage certificate, and the birth certificate of the Chilean spouse. Chile recognizes two types of marital unions: 'union civil' (civil union) and 'matrimonio' (traditional marriage). 'Union civil' can accelerate the process for permanent residency (PMJ) but does not accelerate citizenship acquisition. 'Matrimonio' theoretically allows for citizenship, but practically it primarily accelerates temporary residency and permanent residency. Full citizenship applications typically proceed at a standard pace, often taking over 1.5 years even with legal assistance. For 'matrimonio', a witness is required, and a translator if neither party speaks Spanish. It is advisable to prepare and apostille necessary documents from the country of origin in advance.
  • The Chilean Temporary Residence Visa (VNT) application process for foreigners, especially when applying from abroad, can be lengthy. Initial applications are often made through the SERMIG portal outside Chile, with processing times ranging from 4-6 months to potentially 1-1.5 years. After approval, applicants can enter Chile to finalize the process, including obtaining a national ID card (cédula de identidad). During the waiting period, individuals may stay as tourists, with possibilities for extension, but careful management of visa durations and potential 'visa runs' (exiting and re-entering Chile) is necessary. Neither having a Chilean child nor marriage necessarily expedites the VNT process. Obtaining the cédula de identidad after VNT approval typically takes 1 week to 1 month.
  • Chilean citizenship application process is significantly delayed. Residents with 2 years of continuous residency and ties to Chilean citizens may apply earlier. However, current wait times are estimated at an average of 10 years due to a backlog of approximately 300,000 cases from a previous administration and ongoing issues with revoked citizenships. Approval rates have drastically decreased (e.g., 21 cases in May, 11 in April). There are no guarantees of approval, even for those applying via faster tracks like marriage.
  • Kazakhstan citizens can enter Brazil visa-free. Visa requirements for Chile depend on nationality; citizens of Kazakhstan require a visa to enter Chile. All flights to Easter Island (IPC) must originate from Santiago (SCL).
  • When a child enters Chile as a tourist, and both parents have residency permits (whether temporary or permanent), the process for applying for residency for the child ('reunification') can be complex. If parents are temporary residents, specific internal application procedures may apply. Applying as two principal applicants (titulares) with only passports and criminal records might be simpler than applying with one principal and dependents, which requires additional documents like declarations and apostilled work contracts, potentially posing challenges for apostilling.
  • Temporary residence (VNJ) in Chile based on pregnancy can be applied for and processed from within the country, even if the applicant entered on a tourist status.
  • Exit regulations for minors from Chile traveling with one parent are generally lenient, and border control officers often do not strictly enforce the requirement for the second parent's authorization for tourists. However, carrying a notarized, translated (English or Spanish) power of attorney is highly recommended for security. Consular offices may assist in printing documents verified in the Russian notary database.
  • When applying for Chilean Residencia Temporal (Temporary Residency), particularly for family reunification, and submitting a criminal record certificate from a foreign country (e.g., Russia): 1. Ensure the certificate is apostilled in the country of origin. 2. It must be translated into Spanish. 3. It is crucial to send both the original apostilled certificate and its Spanish translation. 4. Critically, the Spanish translation should ideally be certified by a Chilean notary, who typically adds a QR code to each page, to be recognized as valid by Chilean immigration authorities.
  • To register a marriage in Chile, the applicant needs either a valid passport or a Chilean Cédula (ID card). No additional specific documents were reported as mandatory for the initial process.
  • When entering Chile, border control may assign varied purposes of visit on immigration cards. While a group typically has 'vacaciones' (vacation) listed, individual entries might show 'otros' (others) without further inquiry, as observed in Patagonia. This suggests a degree of flexibility or internal classification by immigration officers that doesn't necessarily impact the visitor's entry if all other requirements are met.
  • The processing time for a Chilean 'Rentista' (financial independence) visa can vary significantly, ranging from approximately 3 months to up to a year. The specific duration depends on the application's complexity and current processing loads.
