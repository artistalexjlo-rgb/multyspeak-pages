  • For local taxi services in Mauritius, the 'Dodo Taxi' application is recommended. Taxis operate normally for short distances, and cash payments are widely accepted. There are many official taxi companies available.
  • Driving in Mauritius requires adapting to left-hand traffic (right-hand drive vehicles). Local roads are generally well-maintained. For taxi services, avoid street-hail prices which can be inflated by 100%; use apps like Dodogo for transparent pricing, noting that some may require cash payment. Car rental is highly recommended for flexibility over fixed resort stays.
  • Mauritius hotel notes: Dinarobin Beachcomber has seasonal pool heating (typically active in winter, may be off in October). Le Morne area is recommended for snorkeling and turtle sightings. Coral shoes are essential due to debris and rocks on the seabed. Driving on the island is manageable, though south roads have serpentine sections.
  • Russian citizens can rent a car in Mauritius without a credit card. Payment options include cash or Union Pay cards. It is possible to arrange a rental without a deposit with the rental company. Payment for the rental is typically made at the time of vehicle handover.
  • When renting a car in Mauritius, verify the fuel level before accepting the vehicle, as some providers may attempt to deliver cars with near-empty tanks. Advance booking is recommended.
  • Standard national driver's licenses are sufficient for renting a car in Mauritius; an International Driving Permit (IDP) is not strictly required.
  • When booking airline tickets, consider the difference in refund and change policies between direct airline bookings and online travel aggregators (OTAs). Night driving is highly discouraged as roads are poorly lit and pedestrians frequently walk on the road surface. Ensure you factor in fuel costs, as it is never included in car rental rates. Plan for sunset around 17:00.

  • Driving in Mauritius involves left-hand traffic. Most drivers adapt within 1-2 hours. An automatic transmission is recommended to simplify driving. Road conditions vary: southern roads can be narrow and winding (serpentines) with typical speeds of 40-60 km/h, while the M1/M2 highway (airport to north) is a multi-lane expressway with a 110 km/h limit, but can experience heavy morning traffic and congestion in urban areas. Maintain awareness, especially during right turns where oncoming traffic will be on the 'new' right side.
