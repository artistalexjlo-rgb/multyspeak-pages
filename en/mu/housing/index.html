Housing in Mauritius: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Housing» in Mauritius — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Recommended areas for families with children in Mauritius: Mont Choisy and Pereybere for better swimming conditions; Flic-en-Flac is often noted for coral debris. High-end family-friendly resorts include La Pirogue and Dinarobin.
  • September is generally a good month to visit Mauritius, marking the transition from winter to spring. Weather is typically pleasant with lower humidity and less rainfall. The north of the island generally experiences more sunshine and calmer seas, making it a popular choice for accommodation and activities. The south offers more dramatic landscapes and is less developed. Consider your preferred activities and atmosphere when choosing between the north and south.
  • For flights from Moscow to Mauritius via Dubai with Emirates (e.g., departing Moscow at 16:25 with a 1h 40m transit for the 02:30 flight to Mauritius): if passengers miss the connection due to delays, Emirates policy is to rebook them onto the next available morning flight and provide a hotel voucher for transit stay.
  • Beachcomber resorts (Dinarobin and Paradis) in Mauritius share common facilities. Guests can use pools, restaurants, and beaches of both properties. Dinarobin is quieter/private, Paradis has better entry to the sea and more entertainment. Official website is currently offering a 40% discount until June 4.
  • For travel to Mauritius in May, the western and northern coasts are recommended for accommodation. These regions are sheltered from strong winds, experience less drastic tides, and offer ideal sunset views compared to other parts of the island.
  • For a family vacation with adults, Maldives offers excellent beaches, snorkeling, fishing, and shark diving, but lacks significant attractions. Mauritius provides richer nature and sightseeing opportunities. Seychelles is suggested as a potential alternative.
  • Weather on Mauritius can be highly variable. One day can have strong winds and waves, while the next can be completely calm, especially on the west coast. Northern areas like Mont Choisy are suggested for calmer conditions.
  • Mauritius hotel notes: Dinarobin Beachcomber has seasonal pool heating (typically active in winter, may be off in October). Le Morne area is recommended for snorkeling and turtle sightings. Coral shoes are essential due to debris and rocks on the seabed. Driving on the island is manageable, though south roads have serpentine sections.
  • For Mauritius, staying on the west coast is logistically optimal. Key attractions: whale watching tours depart from Flic-en-Flac; Casela Nature Parks (safari) is located near Cascavelle.
  • Mauritius beaches often have shallow lagoons due to the protective coral reef surrounding the island. High-depth swimming areas are limited. Recommended locations for better swimming conditions include Trou aux Biches (wide sandy beach) and Paradis (Le Morne peninsula). Coral shoes are strongly recommended due to potential sea urchins and coral debris. Public beach access is allowed even at hotels; visitors can place towels near the water line, but should avoid private hotel loungers and infrastructure.
  • When booking hotels in Mauritius, platforms like Booking.com are reliable. For Riu hotels (e.g., Riu Palace, Riu Turquesa) on Le Morne, payment can be processed in advance (weeks before check-in via card like Visa) or upon arrival. Riu Palace is an adults-only section, while Riu Turquesa accommodates families. No issues with check-in have been reported.
  • On Booking.com, it's possible to find apartments/hotels in Mauritius that do not require prepayment. On Airbnb, users can directly contact owners to arrange on-site payment.
  • When booking airline tickets, consider the difference in refund and change policies between direct airline bookings and online travel aggregators (OTAs). Emirates, while sometimes slower with refunds (up to 30 days), offers direct payment options like office payment and bank transfers, and generally handles changes smoothly. OTAs like KupiBilet and Trip.com have shown mixed results: KupiBilet took 7 days for flight refunds and 30 for seat upgrades, while Trip.com managed to reschedule a non-refundable hotel booking. Direct booking with airlines is often recommended for smoother issue resolution. Russian Mir cards may not be accepted for direct purchases with some airlines like Emirates.
  • Consider the value of an all-inclusive package on Mauritius. If you plan to eat at local cafes and consume alcohol purchased outside the resort, a half-board or breakfast-only option might be more cost-effective. Evaluate personal preferences for dining and relaxation.
  • When booking accommodation in Mauritius, consider properties like Sugar Beach Mauritius. Proximity to other facilities (e.g., Pirogue) can be a factor in room selection (e.g., Block Q). Hotel maps, especially if outdated online, can be crucial for planning. Established relationships with travel agents (e.g., 25 years) can facilitate booking processes.
  • A food recommendation for Mauritius: TERACA, located near KFC, offers a wide variety of breakfast options. The food is described as very tasty, portions are large, and prices are affordable.
  • Mauritius weather is highly localized; short rain showers are common but rarely last all day. Moving to a different beach usually guarantees sunny weather. Average temperatures in August range from 25°C to 29°C. Water temperature is generally warmer on the south coast compared to the north.
  • Hotel reviews for Mauritius mention Tamarin Golf and Spa, located on Flic-en-Flac beach (western coast), about a 15-minute walk or 25-minute drive to Tamarin beach; it's considered expensive. Lux Belle Mare and Dinarobin received positive feedback for food and service. Some beaches may be shallow near the shore but suitable beyond breakwaters. Beachcomber hotels offer complimentary access to paddleboards, canoes, and pedal boats.
  • For hotel bookings in Mauritius, Booking.com is generally preferred for reliability and inventory size. For 4-5* hotels, comparing prices with specialized tour operators (e.g., Maldives Bonus, Maldiviana, Art Tour) is recommended, as they may offer better rates through block bookings and provide operator support in case of flight disruptions.
  • To book the giraffe breakfast experience at Casela, Mauritius, check their official website for activity listings. If direct online booking is unavailable for the breakfast, contact them via their listed email address to inquire about reservation procedures and availability. Other activities like quad biking and animal feeding are also offered.
  • Travelers considering Ethiopian Airlines for flights to Mauritius, particularly from Moscow, have expressed concerns about potential overbooking based on reviews. While some report uneventful and on-time flights, others note that the aircraft may be less luxurious than those of Emirates. It's advisable to research current reviews and consider the trade-offs between cost and service level.
  • For child-friendly beaches and hotels in Mauritius in June, consider the east coast, specifically Lux Belle Mar, known for shallow, clear water. However, the east coast can be windy, making it feel cool after exiting the water. The west coast, near Dinarobin, may have cooler water in June, and coral shoes are recommended. Key factors to consider are water clarity, depth, wind conditions, and temperature.
  • La Pirogue and Sugar Beach on Mauritius are interconnected resorts sharing a beach and facilities. Guests of one can often use the infrastructure of the other. La Pirogue consists of bungalows (often reported as dated but charming), while Sugar Beach features colonial-style multi-story buildings. Common local wildlife like tenrecs (resembling hedgehogs/small rodents) are frequently mistaken for rats by tourists, but they are harmless. Both properties are located on the Flic-en-Flac beach.
  • When traveling to Mauritius with children, consider accessibility and transportation. Google Maps may not be universally functional; alternative mapping services or reliance on friends' devices might be necessary. While food options may not always be within walking distance, renting a car is a viable solution, but careful area selection is advised. Large villas can be very expensive, potentially requiring budget adjustments or alternative accommodation choices.
  • At Hotel Nirvana in Mauritius, some parts of the beach are reported to be unsuitable for swimming. The hotel also reportedly lacks extensive greenery, which might be a concern during peak season.
  • When booking flights for Mauritius, particularly with codeshare options like Air Mauritius operating on Emirates planes, be aware of potential overbooking scenarios. In case of overbooking, passengers holding tickets directly purchased from the operating airline (Emirates in this case) are typically prioritized for boarding. The risk of overbooking is generally low with major carriers like Emirates, but it's a factor to consider if there's a significant price difference. Customer support for some airlines, like Air Mauritius, may be less robust, potentially causing issues with refunds or rebookings if disruptions occur. For significant savings (e.g., 100,000 units), the risk might be worth considering, but for smaller differences (10-15 units), it may not be advisable. Always check the refund and support policies of the ticketing agent and airline.
  • Travel tips for Mauritius: July/August is low season (fewer crowds, lower rates, cooler temperatures). Recommended West Coast hotels: Hilton, Sugar Beach, La Pirogue. North Coast: Maritim, Beachcomber Trou aux Biches. Driving in Mauritius: left-hand traffic, right-hand drive cars, high-quality roads. Island size allows for 7-10 days to cover major sights. Nearby reefs protect the coastline, generally making snorkeling safe near shores.
  • At Hotel Nirvana in Mauritius, second-floor rooms advertised with a garden view actually face a technical parking area. Guests should confirm room view preferences when booking.
  • Mauritius offers honeymoon packages and villa bookings with hotel gifts valid up to 9 months post-wedding. While comparable to Seychelles in terms of celebratory options, the 'honeymoon' period and specific offers might differ. Annual celebrations are recommended regardless of destination.
  • In Mauritius, inquire about the cultural norms regarding tipping hotel staff (housekeeping, waiters). While tipping is common in many tourist destinations, it's important to verify if it's considered customary or potentially offensive in the local culture to avoid misunderstandings.
  • A guest at Tamarin Golf Boutique Hotel (4-star) in Tamarin, Mauritius, encountered a wolf snake (non-venomous, approx. half-meter long) in their room. Hotel staff advise keeping balconies closed as snakes can enter. The hotel provided a new room swiftly.
  • Hotel Nirvana in Mauritius features highly renovated rooms with high-quality fixtures and excellent cleanliness. The room interiors are well-regarded by guests.
  • All-inclusive packages at luxury Mauritian hotels can cost approximately 360 EUR per day for two. It is recommended to compare upgrade costs directly with the hotel and through the tour operator, as the latter can be up to 20% cheaper.
  • At Dinarobin Hotel, breakfast includes fresh juices from a dispenser and a variety of fruits, notably papaya. Options for children include porridge, butter, cheese, boiled eggs, and fruits, ensuring they are satisfied until lunch. Lunch options at the beachfront restaurant include burgers and other treats, with complimentary ice cream. Special requests for fruits like papaya are often accommodated.
  • August in Bel Mare, Mauritius, can be windy and cool. Beaches are present but infrastructure is limited, with mainly villages and few restaurants. Consider other parts of the island for more amenities.
  • At LUX* Le Morne Resort 5* in Mauritius, a dress code applies for dinner. Guests are not permitted to wear shorts in the dining areas. Long trousers (e.g., jeans, chinos, or other smart casual pants) are required for men. Casual summer dresses or smart trousers are suitable for women. This rule ensures comfort and adherence to the hotel's ambiance.
  • When booking hotels in the Maldives, consider that tour operators often include a more comprehensive package (hotel, taxes, transfer, insurance, green tax) compared to direct booking sites like Booking.com, which may only show the room rate. Researching operator packages can sometimes yield better overall value.
  • Dining options on Mauritius vary significantly by location. Many resorts in isolated areas (e.g., North/West) make self-catering difficult. Half Board (HB) or Full Board (FB) is often recommended to avoid logistical issues with finding restaurants. If booking a villa, verify surrounding infrastructure via Google Maps before committing to a self-catering plan.
  • When selecting hotels on Mauritius, consider factors like location (calm bays vs. open coast), wind and wave conditions, proximity to amenities, and service quality. Some highly-rated options like Sugar Beach have received negative reviews regarding beach quality and staff. Travelers seeking specific ambiance, such as quiet bays and adult-only environments, might consider hotels like Le Cardinal or Sands Suites Resort and Spa, but should research thoroughly as opinions vary.
  • When selecting a swimming spot in Mauritius, areas described as 'to the right from the hotel' might offer significantly warmer water. These locations feature varied environments, including areas with rocks and algae suitable for observing marine life, and clear sandy areas. Wind conditions are generally favorable and do not impede swimming.
  • Guests celebrating honeymoons (even up to 9 months after the wedding) at Mauritius hotels can receive special perks like complimentary champagne, snacks, celebratory dinners, and wine bar vouchers. These gifts can be valued at approximately 15,000 INR. Hotel furniture style might be perceived as dated by some but is often intentional. Potential privacy concerns regarding people walking in front of rooms are minimal due to low foot traffic outside of meal times. Breakfast variety can be limited, but options are generally plentiful.

Seychelles is perceived as offering a more tranquil vacation experience. Many travelers combine trips to both islands, flying between them. For those seeking a comprehensive experience, Mauritius is recommended for its extensive sightseeing opportunities beyond just beaches, while Seychelles might appeal to those desiring a more relaxed atmosphere.

  • The weather in Mauritius is generally pleasant for travel during shoulder seasons like October-November and March-April. Late September offers warm evenings and hot days, with comfortable ocean temperatures. The northern and western coasts tend to be warmer and less windy than the east and south.
  • Electrical sockets in Mauritius are Type G (three rectangular pins in a triangle pattern), same as in the UK. Travelers should bring a universal adapter or a Type G adapter if their devices use different plug types (e.g., European Type C or Dubai Type D).
  • For a stay in Le Morne, Mauritius, recommended hotels include Dinarobin and Paradise. These are part of the same hotel network, offering shared facilities and beaches. They are known for having calm, flat beaches with shallow entry into the water, suitable for swimming even in less than ideal conditions.
  • Le Morne area on Mauritius lacks restaurants within walking distance of hotels like Dinarobin. Nearby options include La Gaulette village (limited) or Flic en Flac (approx. 45 min taxi ride). All-inclusive or half-board meal plans are recommended due to excellent hotel cuisine. Shopping offers good value for quality items. Try the lamb baguettes sold at cafes in large chains (around 400 MUR).
  • On Mauritius, public beaches extend to the water's edge. While hotels may restrict access to their loungers and facilities for non-guests, one can typically access the water from public beaches. Purchasing a 'day pass' provides access to hotel amenities like pools and bars. If questioned by hotel security on public beach areas, citing local law and the option to call the police can resolve the situation.

Seat selection might be limited on partner-operated segments, and booking directly with the operating airline or contacting the primary carrier for assistance may be options.
