Activities & Entertainment in Mauritius: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Activities & Entertainment» in Mauritius — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Key attractions in Mauritius include waterfalls, noted for their beauty and play of colors under the sun. The capital, Port Louis, is a popular destination. Casela Park is an option, though access might be restricted on certain days due to private bookings. Visitors are encouraged to explore beyond beaches to experience the island's diverse landscapes. Excursions to the eastern islands and safaris are also recommended.
  • Snorkeling trips at Trou aux Biches can be arranged directly with local boat operators on the beach for significantly lower prices than booking via international tour aggregators. Rates are negotiable; standard excursions often include dolphin watching and reef snorkeling.
  • Big game fishing trips in Mauritius, like a 5-hour excursion from Le Morne, can cost around 27,000 Mauritian Rupees for the entire boat. This cost can be shared among up to 4 people.
  • Whale watching in Mauritius offers a memorable experience but comes with challenges. The boat journey can be rough due to strong waves. Participants need to be strong swimmers, able to quickly enter and exit the boat. For those prone to seasickness, it may be difficult. Booking a private tour is suggested as it offers a better chance to see and swim with whales/dolphins due to fewer participants on board.
  • Mauritius hotel notes: Dinarobin Beachcomber has seasonal pool heating (typically active in winter, may be off in October). Le Morne area is recommended for snorkeling and turtle sightings. Coral shoes are essential due to debris and rocks on the seabed. Driving on the island is manageable, though south roads have serpentine sections.
  • Casela Park in Mauritius is a highly recommended attraction. Visitors can engage in activities such as eco-rider tours and giraffe feeding. It's possible to interact with animals like zebras and giraffes (e.g., hand-feeding).
  • Mauritius has no venomous land creatures. While non-venomous snakes (like 'couleuvre', a type of grass snake) and spiders are present, they are not dangerous. Commonly mistaken for ferrets, mongooses are also native to the island.
  • Whale watching tours in Mauritius can be a fantastic experience. Look for operators who prioritize safety, such as ensuring propellers are not engaged when people are in the water. Consider the duration of the tour and potential for seasickness. Some tours offer complimentary refreshments. Booking with reputable operators like 'Ks and Br' or similar is recommended based on user feedback.
  • Swimming with whales can be a concern due to fear of depths. While Mauritius offers shallow beaches without whales, encountering whales elsewhere is generally considered safe as they are large, non-aggressive animals. Rip currents are not typically an issue. Using a standard mask (not full-face) is recommended for better mobility. The decision involves weighing the desire for this experience against personal fears.
  • Swimming with whales (sperm whales) in Mauritius is officially prohibited, only observation from a boat is allowed. Despite this, many tours facilitate swimming. Fines for tourists are approximately 1000 MUR, while boat owners/captains face fines up to 50,000 MUR and potential license revocation. Some guides offer to proceed with swimming if tourists agree to cover potential fines. The probability of encountering whales is about 90%; if not seen, some tours offer alternatives (e.g., dolphins) or a discount. Video recording is an additional service. Prices depend on the guide (Russian-speaking guides might be more expensive) and whether it's a private or group tour.
  • Casela Park (Mauritius) giraffe feeding: Days off are Tuesday and Thursday. Available hours: 10:30–11:30 and 13:30–15:00. Cost: 500 MUR per person, tickets are purchased at the souvenir shop.
  • Operators offering significantly lower prices for whale swimming tours in Mauritius may engage in price dumping and overcrowd boats beyond safe capacity, leading to increased safety risks. While cheaper alternatives are scarce, some reputable operators prioritize safety and will cancel tours due to adverse weather conditions (e.g., high waves). Advise users to prioritize safety over cost when selecting a tour.
  • In Casela Park, Mauritius, various activities are available. An 'ecorider' for two, including park entrance, costs 10,250 Mauritian Rupees (MUR) and starts at 9:15 AM, offering a less crowded experience. Feeding giraffes costs 1,000 MUR for two, with tickets purchasable at the souvenir kiosk on-site. Feeding sessions are reportedly scheduled only on Tuesdays and Thursdays. The 'breakfast with giraffes' experience requires advance booking and can be fully booked weeks ahead; one user mentioned the next available slot was May 17th. To guarantee a spot for breakfast with giraffes, direct email communication with the park is recommended. Feeding giraffes without breakfast can be purchased on-site. The cost for feeding giraffes is 500 MUR per person. Some visitors find the breakfast experience worthwhile for its exclusivity and atmosphere, while others deem it less valuable than the feeding activity alone, suggesting 10-15 minutes for feeding and photos is sufficient. The park offers an 'ecorider' experience and one roller coaster descent is included with the ticket. The total time spent on these activities can be around 4 hours. For walking with lions, advance booking is recommended, though on-site purchase might be possible.
  • Mauritius has a significant population of stray dogs and hotel-resident cats. It is advised not to feed cats by hand to avoid accidental bites. The island also has a large population of fruit bats (flying foxes).
  • Whale watching tours in Mauritius are available; individual private guides for small groups (approx. 5 people) are recommended over large tour operators for a better experience.
  • Mauritian rum is a topic of discussion. Some find it has a distinct aftertaste. Recommended brands include La Bourdonnais, Lasy Dodo, and Chamarel, with a preference for aged varieties. Visiting local distilleries for tastings is advised to find personal preferences. Gift sets with miniature bottles are available for sampling.
  • Casela Nature Parks on Mauritius does not accept UnionPay cards (including RSHB) or Mir. Only Visa, Mastercard, or cash are accepted. If paying with cash Euros, change is returned in Mauritian Rupees (MUR). Booking tickets in advance is not mandatory; they can be bought on arrival. The basic entrance ticket includes the safari shuttle and access to the lions area.
  • La Pirogue and Sugar Beach on Mauritius are interconnected resorts sharing a beach and facilities. Guests of one can often use the infrastructure of the other. La Pirogue consists of bungalows (often reported as dated but charming), while Sugar Beach features colonial-style multi-story buildings. Common local wildlife like tenrecs (resembling hedgehogs/small rodents) are frequently mistaken for rats by tourists, but they are harmless. Both properties are located on the Flic-en-Flac beach.
  • Whale watching in Mauritius: Swimming tours typically cost between 4500-6000 MUR per person for group tours, with private tours ranging significantly higher. Key logistics: bring two towels, anti-seasickness medication, and consider starting after 9:00 AM to avoid peak boat congestion. Always verify safety protocols with guides before entering the water, as boat motor accidents have been reported.
  • At Casela Park on Mauritius, purchasing tickets for activities like feeding giraffes can involve long queues. Cash payments may not always be accepted for ticket purchases, and inquiries have been made about specific bank card (RSB) acceptance. Safari experiences are available within the park and can be booked separately.
  • The beach at Riu Palace in Mauritius (Le Morne area) can be affected by strong winds and waves, making swimming difficult. Guests recommend bringing water shoes. Many guests opt to drive to other beaches for better swimming conditions, such as the public Le Morne beach, which is considered much better than the hotel's private beach.
  • Sea turtles have been observed on the beach opposite the Dinarobin hotel in Mauritius. This location is suggested for those looking to find sea turtles.
  • Beware of tour booking scams at Flic en Flac beach (specifically an individual named Jay, who issues receipts, takes deposits around 50 EUR, and cancels tours without refunding). When booking whale-watching excursions, prioritize smaller group sizes for safety reasons and ensure boat engines are completely cut off before entering the water.
  • Whale watching on Mauritius involves small boats and rough sea conditions. Participants should be prepared for cold wind and significant motion. Safety for children depends on their swimming ability and comfort with open water. Note: There is an ongoing ethical debate regarding human interaction with whales in the region.
  • June is considered the winter season in Mauritius. Air and water temperatures average 23-24°C. While weather is generally good for sightseeing, visitors should expect wind and occasional storms.
  • Many hotels in Mauritius offer water sports and activities. Beachcomber hotels provide complimentary access to paddleboards, canoes, and pedal boats. Other resorts like Lux Belle Mare also offer various water-based activities. Pricing and availability can vary, so it's advisable to check with the specific hotel.
  • Whale watching excursion on Mauritius was highly recommended by a tourist. The experience with a Russian-speaking local guide was described as unforgettable, with strong emotions evoked by seeing whales in their natural habitat. The guide was also specifically praised.
  • When visiting Casela Park in Mauritius, it is advisable to book certain add-on activities, like 'Breakfast with Giraffes,' in advance, especially during peak season. For most other activities, same-day booking upon arrival is often feasible, particularly if visiting early (e.g., 10 AM). All bookings can be made and paid for online via the official Casela Park website. A suggested strategy for park navigation is to start with the farthest attractions (e.g., giraffe encounter, safari) to optimize the visit.
  • Whale watching in Mauritius carries significant safety risks. Key facts: 1. Always confirm who is captaining the boat, as the same guide may work with different captains. 2. Safety during water entry is primarily the tourist's responsibility; watch out for propellers and boat engine status. 3. Aggressive boat maneuvering to get close to whales can be dangerous. 4. Always maintain situational awareness before jumping into the water.
  • If a foreign credit card is rejected on the Casela Park website, visitors can pay by cash at the entrance ticket counter for general entry and most activities. Giraffe breakfast must be booked and paid for in advance. Alternatively, email the park directly to request payment instructions.
  • Discussion on buying snorkeling gear (masks and fins) for Mauritius. Users are debating whether to buy their own to avoid hygiene issues or poor rental quality. Recommendations sought for masks and fins, including experiences with full-face masks for snorkeling with whales. Price vs. quality is a consideration, with Tusa mentioned as a premium brand and cheaper alternatives sought. Tips on preventing mask fogging (saliva, anti-fog solutions, soap) and proper mask fitting are shared.
  • Renting a car is essential for exploring Mauritius; pick it up and drop it off at the airport. International driving permits are highly recommended, even if local Russian licenses with Latin characters might sometimes be accepted. Ensure your car has a fire extinguisher with a valid expiration date, as this is frequently checked by police during road checks. International permits simplify potential interactions with authorities.
  • Surfing schools are available at Tamarin and Le Morne in Mauritius. These schools typically offer reef surfing, with transport to the surf spots provided by boat.
  • For a 12-day trip to Mauritius for two people in November, including activities like whale watching and visiting a safari park, budget considerations should include flights, accommodation, food, transportation, and activity costs. While specific costs vary, researching current prices for these components will help establish a realistic budget. Whale watching tours can range from $50-$100 USD per person, and safari park entrance fees may be similar. Accommodation and dining expenses will depend on chosen level of luxury.
  • Deep sea fishing in Mauritius: Average charter cost is around 18,000 MUR for 9 hours. Success rates are unpredictable; large marine life like whales can interfere with catches. Shared charters are recommended to minimize financial risk. Marlin fishing is high-stakes; catches below 250kg are sometimes considered 'non-trophy' by local sports fishing standards.
  • A guest stayed at Riu Turquoise - All Inclusive in Mauritius from May 14-24. The hotel and food were considered good. The hotel beach was very windy, but a 15-minute walk to the right led to a better beach. The hotel's location is convenient for Casela Park, seaplane departures, and whale watching excursions. Overall, the guest had a positive impression.
  • The probability of seeing whales on Mauritius is high, but their behavior is unpredictable. They may swim, play, or dive. While sightings are common, their activity levels can vary, sometimes involving brief surfacing before diving again. Despite this, whale watching is described as a very rewarding experience.
  • For convenient access to popular excursions in Mauritius, such as waterfalls (e.g., Tamarind Falls), safari (e.g., Casela Park), and whale watching, it is recommended to stay on the west or southwest coast. The area spanning from Flic en Flac to Le Morne is suggested to minimize travel time to these attractions.
  • To book the giraffe feeding experience at Casela Park, Mauritius, contact them via email in advance. Turtles are in a designated area, and feeding goats is also possible. Giraffe availability might vary due to their 'day off' schedule. Arrive at park opening for a less crowded experience. Swimming with turtles might be possible in certain spots.
  • The user describes a vacation experience on Mauritius, including travel details, accommodation, activities, and costs. Key information points include: - Flights: From Uzbekistan via Dubai with Emirates. - Accommodation: RIU Palace, with good food and entertainment. - Transportation: Rental car available for ~$35/day, international driving permit accepted. - Activities: - Helicopter tour: ~$290 for 25 minutes, booked 3 weeks in advance via official email. - Le Morne mountain hike: Access restricted to guided tours ($20), due to a past incident. - Whale watching tour: "Privat" tour mentioned positively. - National Park visit: Tickets purchased on-site, no queues. - Visits to waterfalls and colored sands. - General impression: The island is beautiful, with a variety of activities leading to an active vacation rather than just beach relaxation.
  • Seaplanes in Mauritius are preferred for waterfall tours as they fly at a lower altitude than helicopters, allowing for better visibility (e.g., seeing sea turtles). Seaplanes offer a private experience (only the pilot and the passenger), whereas helicopters are more suitable for groups of 4+. Seaplane tours operate in 15-20 minute intervals.
  • A private boat for whale watching in Mauritius can be shared to reduce costs. A trip from Trou aux Biches beach costs 20,000 Mauritian Rupees for the entire boat. Payment is typically upon arrival, without prepayment. Contacts for such services can often be found directly on the beach.
  • Swimming with whales in Mauritius is officially prohibited, with potential fines for both organizers and tourists. While boat observation is generally permitted, direct swimming or diving with whales is illegal. There are reports of tourists and operators being caught and fined. Engage in such activities at your own risk, and be aware of potential safety hazards from unscrupulous operators.
  • Whale watching or helicopter tours on Mauritius are highly weather-dependent. Cancellations due to wind are common. Service providers may cite 'technical issues' for rebooking delays. Always verify local wind forecasts via platforms like windy.com and maintain flexible travel dates.
