Shopping: how it works in Mauritius — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Shopping» in Mauritius — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • To buy luggage near Astroea Hotel in Mauritius without a car, consider Bo'Valon Mall or large local supermarkets for cheaper options. For higher quality luggage, a trip to larger malls like Bagatelle Mall or Tribeca would be necessary, implying transportation will be required as these are not within walking distance.
  • Heets sticks are generally not available for purchase locally in Mauritius. Travelers are advised to bring their own supply from their departure country.
  • UnionPay cards work in Mauritius for cash withdrawals and retail payments. Standard Russian mobile roaming often blocks messenger services; purchasing a local SIM card is recommended for reliable connectivity.
  • A cashmere factory store in Mauritius, though often located outside tourist areas, might offer services such as free hotel delivery for unavailable items and purchase discounts.
  • Jiva is recommended as a place for good coffee in Mauritius, specifically for options like flat white or cold brew, according to local feedback.
  • High-quality silk and cashmere scarves are available in specialized shops in Port Louis and other textile stores. Linen clothing, such as men's shirts, can be found, with prices potentially lower in general stores compared to hotel shops. South African goods may also be available at discounted prices.
  • Good quality coffee (beans or ground) can be purchased at a big grocery supermarket within Cascavelle Mall for approximately 300 MUR. The same coffee might be sold at Port Louis market for a significantly higher price, around 800 MUR. It is advisable to compare prices at different locations.
  • Mauritian craft beer is highly recommended. Available in major retail chains, there are approximately 7-8 distinct varieties, all described as delicious and unique. This beer is considered among the best sampled in recent years.
  • Advise travelers that Super U supermarkets in Pereybere and Grand Baie, Mauritius, are good options for buying souvenirs. Mention that these stores often carry a variety of local products and gifts.
  • Mauritian rum is a topic of discussion. Some find it has a distinct aftertaste. Recommended brands include La Bourdonnais, Lasy Dodo, and Chamarel, with a preference for aged varieties. Visiting local distilleries for tastings is advised to find personal preferences. Gift sets with miniature bottles are available for sampling.
  • In Mauritius, cashmere shawls can be found in hotels and some shops for around 40-60 euros. Information is sought on the authenticity of these cashmere products and advice on identifying counterfeits.
  • For grocery shopping in Flic-en-Flac, Mauritius, the main supermarkets are Jumbo and Carrefour. For fresh fruits and vegetables, local advice is to buy them from vendors located at the roundabout featuring the Dodo bird statue.
  • Souvenir recommendations from Mauritius include: Labourdonnais marmalade, New Grove rum liqueurs (vanilla, coffee, caramel flavors are good for gifts, up to 10 bottles per person allowed), spices, tamarind jam, linen clothing (especially men's shirts), and high-quality silk and cashmere scarves. Prices for souvenirs are often lower on excursions (e.g., near Chamarel waterfalls) compared to Port Louis, except for clothing. Recommended rum brands include Lasy Dodo, Chamarel, and La Bourdonnais, with a preference for aged varieties. Avoid rum shaped like a Dodo head, described as low quality.
  • Transporting vacuum-packed smoked marlin from Mauritius is safe for international travel. It is recommended to purchase store-bought, factory-sealed vacuum packages kept in refrigerators. A thermal bag is optional but not strictly necessary for successful transport.
  • Mauritius airport duty-free offers competitive pricing on cosmetics and alcohol, often 15-20% cheaper than Istanbul. Souvenirs are limited to small outlets near gates. It is highly recommended to buy local food and tea at supermarkets rather than the airport. There is a broad selection of French wines available.
  • In Grand Baie, Mauritius, for dinner, a 5-star hotel restaurant like Royal Palm Beachcomber Luxury is recommended. Such locations often offer beach day activities, swimming, and snorkeling opportunities near corals with marine life.
  • Aeroflot typically runs annual ticket sales for Seychelles/Mauritius routes in the third week of November. Prices hover around 50,000 RUB for a round trip including luggage. Recommendations: Monitor the official Aeroflot Telegram channel for announcements and use historical data (patterns from previous years) to anticipate booking windows.
  • Drones are allowed on Mauritius. Supermarket prices are comparable to Moscow, with higher quality products. Taxis are expensive; car rental is recommended (approx. €200 for 10 days). Bringing your own quality snorkeling mask is advisable, as good ones cost €150-€200 locally. Alcoholic beverage prices are similar to Russia.
  • At Mauritius airport duty-free, payments can be made using Mauritian Rupees (MUR), even if prices are displayed in Euros. The cashier will convert the amount at the point of sale. In cafes near the gates, Rupees are directly accepted.
  • Cashmere on Mauritius is available at prices comparable to Falconeri, but the quality is reported to be average. Coffee can be purchased at the 'U' supermarket chain.
  • Dining options on Mauritius vary significantly by location. Many resorts in isolated areas (e.g., North/West) make self-catering difficult. Half Board (HB) or Full Board (FB) is often recommended to avoid logistical issues with finding restaurants. If booking a villa, verify surrounding infrastructure via Google Maps before committing to a self-catering plan.
  • When purchasing goods like Dubai chocolate on Mauritius, be aware of the transaction costs. A purchase of one item cost 2300 units of local currency (likely Mauritian Rupees), which is equivalent to 60 AED (United Arab Emirates Dirham).
  • Beer Garden in Mauritius (potentially in Flic en Flac area) offers 8 types of beer, including non-alcoholic and gluten-free options, along with snacks and chef-prepared appetizers for a daytime meal.
  • Electronic cigarettes can be carried to Mauritius, but detection is possible during baggage screening upon arrival. While some travelers have had them pass through undetected in a backpack, airport security may selectively screen luggage. E-cigarettes are also available for purchase on the island, with prices around 3500 MUR for a 50,000 puff capacity device. Various options exist, and availability should be confirmed locally.
  • In Mauritian souvenir shops, merchants often claim they only accept cash to avoid processing fees. If you insist on paying by card and start walking away, they will usually produce a hidden POS terminal to complete the transaction.
  • Beyond cashmere, shops near Grand Baie in Mauritius also offer textiles like high-quality Egyptian cotton polo shirts and blends of silk and cotton.
  • Electrical sockets in Mauritius are Type G (three rectangular pins in a triangle pattern), same as in the UK. Travelers should bring a universal adapter or a Type G adapter if their devices use different plug types (e.g., European Type C or Dubai Type D).
  • Shopping for vanilla pods in Mauritius: Buying directly at hotels is significantly overpriced (e.g., 500 MUR for 3 pods). Better deals are found at major supermarkets like Jumbo, Winner's, or Super U.
  • Le Morne area on Mauritius lacks restaurants within walking distance of hotels like Dinarobin. Nearby options include La Gaulette village (limited) or Flic en Flac (approx. 45 min taxi ride). All-inclusive or half-board meal plans are recommended due to excellent hotel cuisine. Shopping offers good value for quality items. Try the lamb baguettes sold at cafes in large chains (around 400 MUR).
  • Evenings in Mauritius, including July, can be cool, so packing a sweatshirt or light jacket is advisable. Daytime temperatures are generally warm.
