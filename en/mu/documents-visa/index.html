Documents & Visa in Mauritius: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Mauritius — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Importing IQOS and Heets into Mauritius: travelers report no issues with customs scanning. Devices and sticks in carry-on or checked luggage pass through scanners without extra inspection or confiscation by customs officers.
  • For tourists entering Mauritius with children, the required documents are the child's passport and a standard English translation of the birth certificate (notarization is not required for regular tourist trips). Mauritian authorities do not require parental consent from the non-accompanying parent, and Russian border control only requires it if an official travel restriction has been registered.
  • Le Morne Brabant in Mauritius is officially declared closed due to a past fatality (a foreigner fell from the summit 1-2 years ago). This closure is often perceived as a liability waiver by authorities. Despite the official closure, access is unofficially available, and local guides may still lead groups to the summit. Tourists frequently bypass the official closure signs.
  • While not always strictly enforced, travel insurance may be a required document for entry into Mauritius at the discretion of immigration officers. It is advisable to carry proof of insurance to avoid potential entry refusal.
  • Inform users that the import of IQOS and other vaping devices is prohibited in Mauritius. Penalties for non-compliance may include confiscation of the device and significant fines, potentially ranging from 500 MUR to 13,000 MUR. Advise travelers to leave such devices at home to avoid issues with customs.
  • A traveler planning a trip to Mauritius via Dubai is seeking information on current regulations regarding drones. They had their drone temporarily confiscated and stored by Dubai authorities during a previous trip to the Seychelles upon departure, although it was allowed on arrival and during transit. They wish to know the current procedures for carrying drones through Dubai and upon arrival in Mauritius.
  • Importing electronic cigarettes (e.g., IQOS) to Mauritius: E-cigarettes are restricted. Users report successfully passing airport scanners by placing devices inside a bag under a laptop or other electronics. Security at the airport may not require removing gadgets from bags during the screening process.
  • Travelers entering Mauritius should be prepared to present confirmed return flight tickets and hotel reservations. Practice varies; while some tourists pass with verbal confirmation, immigration officials frequently verify hard copies or digital proof of return travel and accommodation.
  • Transporting vacuum-packed smoked marlin from Mauritius is safe for international travel. It is recommended to purchase store-bought, factory-sealed vacuum packages kept in refrigerators. A thermal bag is optional but not strictly necessary for successful transport.
  • Mauritius arrival procedure: The digital health/arrival form can be filled out online up to 14 days before arrival. The QR code is generated immediately after submission. While it is not always strictly checked at the boarding gate, it is frequently requested upon entry.
  • Inform users about the electronic entry form requirement for Mauritius. Clarify the official submission window (e.g., not earlier than 72 hours before arrival, though the system might allow up to 2 weeks). Provide the official website link for the form. Emphasize that having the QR code ready is important, as it is often checked thoroughly at immigration, despite some travelers reporting not being asked for it.
  • Detailed information on Mauritius drone regulations (Civil Aviation (Unmanned Aircraft System) Regulations 2024, effective Jan 1, 2025). Key points: registration takes 3+ months, permits required for private land, flying over public areas/beaches/parks is forbidden. Penalties include fines up to 1 million rupees or 12 months imprisonment. However, anecdotal evidence suggests users with drones under 250g have flown without issues, especially when not flying from Russia and not declaring the drone upon entry (e.g., Dubai transit). User experiences vary; some flew without incident, others are cautious. Advise users to check the latest official regulations and consider the risks.
  • Travelers report easy border entry in Mauritius (no visa/hotel confirmation check). For car rentals without airport counters, contact the provider via the number in the voucher. Avoid navigating inland national parks at night due to serpentine roads; coastal roads are safer. Mauritius gets dark by 18:00, so plan arrivals accordingly.
  • The E-visa for Mauritius requires an accommodation email. Travelers report that border checks can be minimal, with some only being asked about their country of origin. It is advised to have all documentation ready, even if checks are sometimes superficial.
  • Company for seaplane tours on Mauritius: Lagoon Flight. Approximate cost: 350 EUR for two people. Location is near Le Morne. Electronic cigarettes are generally not confiscated at airport security scanners if carried in pockets, as they are mostly plastic, but smuggling them remains a regulatory risk.
  • Taking a drone to Mauritius for landscape photography might be complicated due to local regulations, potentially requiring flight permits. Some airlines, like Emirates, may also have restrictions on drone transportation. It's advisable to check all rules and regulations in advance to avoid issues upon arrival.
  • Mauritius immigration authorities may strictly require proof of an onward flight ticket. Travelers failing to produce one at check-in or arrival may be denied boarding or entry, or be forced to purchase a ticket on the spot. This practice is enforced inconsistently ('random').
  • The digital health declaration (Mauritius All-in-One Travel Form) generating a QR code is mandatory for entry into Mauritius. Even if immigration officers do not explicitly ask for a printout, the QR code is checked digitally in their system. Failing to complete it beforehand will result in delays at border control.
  • Travelers have reported successfully carrying electronic cigarettes (iQOS/vapes) in hand luggage (backpacks, handbags) through Mauritius customs without confiscation. It is advised to keep them accessible for X-ray screening if requested.
  • Electronic cigarettes can be carried to Mauritius, but detection is possible during baggage screening upon arrival. While some travelers have had them pass through undetected in a backpack, airport security may selectively screen luggage. E-cigarettes are also available for purchase on the island, with prices around 3500 MUR for a 50,000 puff capacity device. Various options exist, and availability should be confirmed locally.
  • Travelers to Mauritius must complete the 'All-in-One Travel Digital Form' online at least 72 hours before departure to receive a QR code for immigration. This digital form replaces traditional paper immigration/health cards. While official requirements suggest proof of funds (approx. $100 USD/day), it is rarely checked, but recommended to carry.
  • Exporting shells from Mauritius can be subject to regulations and potential fines. While some vendors may claim legality, it is crucial to obtain a receipt (check) as proof of purchase, especially for imported shells. Failure to do so may result in confiscation or fines. Research local regulations and consider purchasing from reputable sources or online marketplaces to avoid issues.
  • For travelers flying to Mauritius with children (with only one parent), check airline policies regarding notarized consent. Emirates may flag the requirement during booking or check-in. Always carry a notarized document translated into English to avoid boarding issues.
  • For traveling with a child to Mauritius, it is advisable to have a notarized English translation of the birth certificate, although requirements can vary. Some travelers report it being requested by home country immigration rather than Mauritian authorities. It's best to have it prepared, especially if specifically advised to do so.
  • Upon arrival in Mauritius, immigration checks typically focus on accommodation details (especially if not booked through a hotel) and return tickets. Medical insurance is often not requested.
  • When traveling to Mauritius, even if you have onward travel arrangements (e.g., to Seychelles) and potentially no transit visa, filling out the arrival form is advisable. Immigration officers can assist with baggage re-transfer and facilitate entry if you clearly explain your situation. Direct transfer processes are generally manageable without prior specific transit permissions.
  • Evenings in Mauritius, including July, can be cool, so packing a sweatshirt or light jacket is advisable. Daytime temperatures are generally warm.
