Trip Preparation in South Korea: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Trip Preparation» in South Korea — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Jeju Island rainy season runs from late June through summer. Weather is highly variable. Wind-proof gear is recommended over umbrellas. Official beaches may have closed facilities (showers, lifeguards) off-season or during bad weather. Be aware of rip currents and tidal patterns.
  • Everland tips: 1) Install the official app for real-time queue data, show schedules, and maps. 2) It is a hilly park; comfortable footwear is mandatory. 3) Popular attractions include Pandas, Safari World, T-Express (wood coaster), and Amazon Express. 4) Weekdays are highly recommended to avoid crowds.
  • Services are available for applying for K-ETA (4500 RUB, including pre-border preparation) and various South Korean visas (tourist, business, guest, student, ethnic from 8000 RUB). These services offer assistance with complex cases, accurate border crossing advice, personalized itineraries, and practical travel tips.
  • During summer, Seoul experiences hot and humid weather, often around 30°C. Air conditioning in public transport (metro, KTX) and shopping malls can be quite strong, leading to significant temperature differences. Travelers are advised to wear light, breathable clothing (e.g., loose cotton pants, shorts, thin long-sleeve shirts for sun protection). It's recommended to carry a light jacket or long-sleeved top for cooler indoor environments or after rain. Sun protection is crucial; carry or purchase high-SPF sunscreen, and consider hats or umbrellas. Locals often use thin arm covers available in convenience stores (CU, 7/11) for sun protection.
  • To apply for a K-ETA (Korea Electronic Travel Authorization), ensure all personal data, travel dates, accommodation details, and stated purpose of visit (e.g., tourism) are accurate and consistent. Rejected applications typically cite failure to meet assessment criteria, allowing for up to three re-applications within six months. For minors under 17, K-ETA is not required, but an arrival card may be necessary. Always have supporting documents ready, such as proof of funds (cash is preferred, approximately $100 USD per day), return flight tickets, and a booked hotel or accommodation. A well-defined travel itinerary is also recommended, even if not always requested.
  • Services are available for K-ETA applications (4500 RUB) and various visa types (tourist, business, student, ethnic visas starting from 8000 RUB) for South Korea. These services offer assistance with document preparation, application submission, and guidance for complex cases, including re-applications after denial and entry after previous refusals. They also provide recommendations for border crossing, travel itineraries, and practical travel tips.
  • Assistance is available for various South Korean visa types, including student and marriage visas. Services include K-ETA applications, tourist visas (for various nationalities and complex cases), business/guest visas, student visas, and ethnic Korean visas (F-4). Services aim to increase chances of successful entry, provide border crossing guidance, and assist with itinerary planning. Contact is typically made via pinned messages or provided links.

  • South Korea's August is characterized by intense heat, high humidity, and the rainy season. UV-protective umbrellas are highly recommended. Early September is generally more pleasant for outdoor activities, even though beach infrastructure (loungers/umbrellas) is typically removed after late August.
  • Dress codes in South Korea are generally informal and liberal; tourists can wear open shoulders, mini-skirts, or casual clothes without judgment. However, sun protection is advised due to strong UV rays, and light layers are recommended for indoor air conditioning.
  • Korean immigration officers may request proof of funds (cash is preferred over bank statements), confirmed return travel, and a detailed itinerary. An ideal tourist itinerary should list 2-3 logical sightseeing spots per day per region. It is advised to keep documents organized but not to present them proactively unless requested to avoid appearing suspicious.
  • When entering South Korea, having a well-prepared and detailed travel plan is crucial, especially for short stays. The plan should include at least three locations per day, logically distributed by area to avoid unnecessary travel. While not always requested by immigration officers, having it readily available can significantly improve your chances of a smooth entry, particularly if questioned about your intentions.
  • For navigating Seoul's metro system, the recommended applications are Naver Maps and Kakao Maps. Both provide comprehensive route planning, including public transport schedules. Kakao Maps is generally sufficient for most travel needs, while Naver Maps is also a highly functional alternative.
  • To ensure smooth entry into South Korea, travelers must prepare the following: sufficient cash (approximately $100 USD per day, even with foreign cards), return tickets to their country of citizenship or residency, a confirmed hotel or accommodation booking, and a detailed itinerary (at least three locations per day, logically planned). A valid K-ETA or visa is mandatory. Crucially, all information provided in the K-ETA application must be accurate and consistent with actual travel plans; misrepresentation, such as stating 'tourism' when visiting friends, can lead to entry refusal. Travelers may also be asked about their declared income and employment.
  • For early December in South Korea (5th-10th), warm clothing is essential. Recommendations include a long jacket rated for -30°C and warm, high boots or winter sneakers, as the wind can make it feel significantly colder than the actual temperature. The difference in weather between Seoul and Busan for a day trip in December should also be considered.
  • Daegu experiences extremely high temperatures during the summer due to its location in a basin. Temperatures can be very intense, making it uncomfortable for tourists during the peak summer season. It is advisable to avoid visiting Daegu in the summer if sensitive to heat.
  • Late July to early August in Jeju Island, South Korea, falls within the summer monsoon season. While rainfall is common, there are typically periods of sunshine interspersed with rainy days. Travelers can expect warm and humid conditions. Checking weather forecasts closer to the travel date is advisable for more accurate predictions.
  • Late July and August in Seoul are characterized by high temperatures and humidity, often referred to as the peak summer heat. While the intensity of the rainy season may decrease towards the end of July, the heat and humidity persist. Visitors sensitive to extreme heat may find this period uncomfortable. The heat typically begins to subside in late September.
  • August is generally considered a non-touristic time in South Korea due to intense heat and humidity, especially after rains. Outdoor activities can be exhausting, with temperatures and humidity making it feel like a 'bath.' Many locals cope by staying indoors with air conditioning. The natural scenery is also less appealing compared to spring and autumn. This period is best avoided for typical sightseeing unless one is prepared for the extreme weather.
  • Payment via Kakao Taxi in Busan can sometimes be problematic with foreign cards like Wowpass. While Kakao Taxi is a convenient service, it is advisable to have cash (approximately 100,000 KRW) as a backup payment method, as foreign card acceptance may be inconsistent. Locate nearby Wowpass ATMs in advance if relying on card payments.
  • Seoul in mid-July typically experiences hot and humid weather. While the peak rainy season may be subsiding, intermittent rainfall is still possible. Temperatures can be high, often exceeding 30°C (86°F), with significant humidity. Travelers should prepare for warm conditions and potential rain showers.
  • The weather in South Korea during July and August is characterized by intense heat and humidity, often exacerbated by rain. Outdoor activities can be very uncomfortable, feeling like a 'bath.' Many locals and residents seek refuge in air-conditioned indoor spaces. The natural scenery is less appealing than in spring or autumn. Traveling to coastal areas like Donghae or Sokcho might be an option, but the sea can be rough, limiting swimming. Indoor attractions are a more comfortable alternative during this period.
  • Comprehensive visa services for South Korea are offered, including K-ETA (4500 RUB), Tourist Visas (from 8000 RUB), Business/Guest Visas (from 8000 RUB), Student Visas (from 8000 RUB), and Ethnic Visas (F-4, from 8000 RUB).   • When preparing a travel plan for South Korean immigration, it is advisable to have a detailed itinerary that includes specific places of interest, their addresses, operating hours, and transportation methods (e.g., subway lines, bus numbers). While officers may not ask for every minute detail, having this information readily available and being able to answer basic questions about your planned activities and logistics can facilitate a smoother immigration process. Familiarity with key addresses and general routes is recommended, rather than rote memorization of every detail.
