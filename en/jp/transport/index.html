Transport: how it works in Japan — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Transport» in Japan — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Inquire about websites or platforms for purchasing train tickets from Matsumoto to Osaka, as Trip.com does not seem to offer this route directly.
  • ICOCA/Suica cards have a maximum balance limit of 20,000 JPY. Cards can be recharged multiple times after the balance is spent. They are widely accepted in convenience stores (konbini) and chain restaurants, serving as a supplement to cash for tourists without international bank cards.
  • Traveling to Japan during 'Silver Week' (a series of holidays in September) results in significant crowds and high demand for accommodation and transportation. It is essential to book hotels, Shinkansen tickets, and popular attractions well in advance, potentially months ahead, to secure availability and avoid inflated prices. Consider adjusting travel dates to avoid this peak period.
  • Japan travel planning: Purchase Shinkansen and bus tickets (e.g., to Kawaguchiko) in advance, especially during peak season. While some tickets can be bought on the spot, booking ahead is recommended. Japan Rail Pass can be cost-effective for extensive travel. For transit in Shanghai, confirm terminal information and complete arrival cards in advance.
  • A reserved Shinkansen ticket can be used for travel in non-reserved cars on the same day, either earlier or later than the original reservation, provided such cars are available on the train. This offers flexibility for travelers whose plans may change.
  • If a Suica or Pasmo card is rejected at a bus or station gate, it is often due to an 'incomplete trip' error (e.g., failure to tap out at the previous destination). This can only be resolved by station staff at a railway gate. In Kyoto, buses require tapping the card upon exit, not entry, due to fixed pricing.
  • When purchasing train tickets in Japan, if ticket counters offer limited availability or inconvenient times, travelers should also check self-service ticket machines. In one instance, earlier tickets were available through a machine when the counter only offered later departure times.
  • Choosing between Hiroshima and Onomichi for a 3-night stay during a cycling trip (including Miyajima and Hiroshima): Onomichi offers more scenic cycling potential but has less convenient Shinkansen access. Hiroshima provides better logistical connections. Verify if Onomichi is covered by the chosen Kansai Pass.
  • Navigating public transport in Japan is made easier with the use of Google Maps for route planning, which provides station information, exit numbers, travel times, and fares. The ICOCA card is a versatile travel pass that can be used on subways, commuter trains, and buses in Osaka, Kyoto, and Tokyo.
  • Suica (IC) cards are interoperable and function across Japan, including major regions like Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto, and Kamakura. They can be used for transit and reloaded at most major stations.
  • Google Maps is highly accurate for public transport in Japan, including Shinkansen and local buses. It provides precise routing and timing. For those using JR lines or seeking specific IC card designs, physical cards can be purchased at station machines (e.g., Icoca, Hayakaken, Suica, Sugoca). Note that tourist-specific IC cards are valid for 28 days.
  • First-time travelers to Japan can benefit from advice on customs, currency exchange, transportation, and shopping. Key recommendations include: no liquid restrictions in carry-on, utilizing currency exchange offices like Ninja Exchange for favorable rates, using Google Maps and ICOCA cards for public transport, and choosing appropriate stores for specific purchases (e.g., Don Quijote for sweets, drugstores for cosmetics). Be aware of potential luggage inspections in transit countries.
  • For domestic flights in Japan, consider budget airlines like Jetstar for more affordable options compared to full-service carriers like ANA. Prices can range significantly (e.g., 25,000 JPY for a round trip for two on Jetstar versus 63,000 JPY on ANA, depending on dates and promotions). Use flight aggregators like Trip.com and Google Flights to compare prices. Be aware of luggage allowances and additional fees with low-cost carriers.
  • For the Shinkansen route from Kyoto to Tokyo, choose seat 'E' (left side) for the best views of Mount Fuji. If seeking a warmer carriage, look for designated cars with reduced air conditioning; these are often marked on the platform signage. Be aware that Japanese trains are fixed-direction, so seats are manually rotated at the end of the line by staff.
  • Fishing rods in hard tubes exceeding standard dimensions must be checked as oversized/fragile baggage. Passengers should proceed to the specialized check-in counter at the airport for oversized items.
  • If you exit a station at a stop different from the one designated on your paper ticket, use the 'Fare Adjustment' (Norikoshi) machines located inside the ticket gates before exiting. Alternatively, pay the difference to the station staff. IC cards automatically handle fare adjustments.
  • For booking bus and train tickets in the Japanese Alps, consider using Japanbusonline and Trip.com. Specific routes like Osaka-Takayama and Takayama-Shirakawa-go are available. Note that direct buses from Takayama to Kamikochi are not available; a transfer in Hirayu is required. Trip.com also offers combined routes, such as Osaka-Takayama with a stop in Shirakawa-go.
  • Rental cars should include an ETC card for toll roads. Parking is largely paid, but free spots exist near tourist attractions (temples, parks) and grocery stores; check signage carefully. Japanese speed limits are strict; excessive speeding (20-30+ km/h over) can lead to license revocation even for tourists. Verify compatibility of driver's license conventions (Geneva vs. Vienna) before rental.
  • Domestic flights are available within Japan for travel to islands like Okinawa, as there is no Shinkansen connection. Booking platforms such as Trip.com can be used to find and purchase tickets for these internal flights. It's important to understand the booking process for domestic air travel in Japan.
  • Physical IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) become unusable once migrated to an Apple Wallet/Digital Wallet. Digital cards can be topped up at station machines equipped with contactless pads or at convenience stores like 7-Eleven. Apps like 'CardReader' can help check balances if official top-up methods are unavailable.
  • 'No transfer' (без пересадок) on Japanese train routes means you remain on the same physical train even if the line or train number changes during the journey. You do not need to disembark and re-board.
  • For adding Japanese transit cards (Suica/Pasmo/ICOCA) to Apple Wallet, users often encounter payment issues. Recommended troubleshooting steps: 1. Try a different bank card. 2. Use a VPN if located in restricted regions like Belarus/Russia. 3. If Suica fails, switch to PASMO. Transit cards are widely accepted for small retail payments (conbini, vending machines, etc.) in addition to transit.
  • Russian-issued cards (specifically UnionPay) have limited acceptance in Japan; they work for cash withdrawals at 7-11 ATMs and some major retail stores but are unreliable for general contactless payments. Russian travel aggregators (Ostrovok, Trip.com) accept Russian-issued cards for hotel and transport bookings.
  • Japan Travel Tips: Shinkansen tickets between major cities cost around 7,000 JPY. Local transport (metro, buses) averages 1,000 JPY/day. Capsule hotels are the cheapest accommodation (1,500-2,000 JPY). Book transport and accommodation in advance. eSIMs can be purchased via Trip.com with clear activation instructions. Souvenir katanas can be transported in checked luggage with proper documentation and handling by airport customs.
  • If a piece of luggage exceeds the airline's weight limit (e.g., 30 kg instead of 23 kg), options include: 1. Repacking: Distribute the excess weight into other available luggage or a newly purchased bag. 2. Paying for Overweight Baggage: Inquire at the airline counter or reception about fees for overweight items. The cost can vary significantly. It's generally advisable to adhere to standard weight limits to avoid unexpected charges.
  • Planning a trip to Japan involves itinerary creation and budgeting. Travelers often build their own routes using resources like Google Maps, Trip.com, and Visit Japan. Costs can vary significantly based on flight and accommodation choices. It's important to prioritize destinations and activities to maximize the travel experience within the available time and budget.
  • If JR Kansai-Hiroshima Pass is unavailable online, it can be purchased in person at authorized travel agencies like NTA TiS Osaka (Umeda station) by presenting a passport. This avoids digital redemption issues.
  • When a physical Suica/Pasmo card cannot be topped up at station vending machines after being added to a mobile wallet (e.g., Apple Wallet), it can often be topped up using cash at Lawson convenience store counters, which feature contactless payment pads.
  • Russian driving licenses (including International Driving Permits/МВУ issued in Russia) are not valid in Japan. To legally drive, rent a car, or participate in street-karting, one must hold a permit issued by a country that adheres to the 1949 Geneva Convention (e.g., Kazakhstan, Georgia).
  • Airline baggage allowance rules often specify weight limits per checked item, not just total allowance. If a single bag exceeds the limit (e.g., 23 kg), excess charges may apply even if the total baggage weight for the passenger is within the overall limit. It is advisable to distribute weight evenly or use an additional bag if available and within total allowances to avoid potential fees or repacking.
  • When booking Shinkansen tickets via third-party services like Trip.com, use the provided QR code at station kiosks to print physical tickets. For large luggage, passengers must book seats with extra space, as these are limited and mandatory for bulky items.
  • Tokyo public transport is notoriously crowded and indifferent, while Okinawa exhibits more relaxed social etiquette. In Japan, elderly or disabled passengers often carry specific pins or badges to alert others that they require a priority seat.
  • Traveling to Japan during New Year (Oshogatsu): Expect fewer tourist crowds compared to peak seasons. Celebrations are primarily family-oriented. For festive atmosphere, visit areas like Shibuya or Omotesando. Consider Kyushu for hot springs and scenery; book transport and accommodation in advance due to potential limited availability. Enjoy clear skies for Fuji views.
  • Shinkansen tickets do not have major 40% early-bird discounts. Standard prices apply for high-speed trains like Nozomi. Small savings (approx. 10%) can be found in 'kinken shops' (ticket resale shops) in Japan. JR Pass or standard ticket purchases are recommended for convenience.
  • Shinkansen luggage rules: Luggage up to 160cm (total dimensions) does not require a reservation. Oversized luggage (160-250cm) requires a seat reservation with extra space. Measurements are standard for JR lines.
  • Key travel insights from Japan: Currency exchange is best at dedicated exchange offices like Ninja Exchange in Shinjuku for optimal rates. For shopping, Don Quijote is good for sweets and alcohol, Tom's for cosmetics, and Ginza Karen for luggage. Google Maps is highly recommended for navigation. Notably, there are no restrictions on liquids in carry-on baggage at Japanese airports, allowing for the transport of items like alcohol. Tax-free shopping procedures and purchasing electronics are also mentioned.
  • Carry-on liquid restrictions for flights to China: Liquids, aerosols, and gels (LAGs) in hand luggage must be in containers of 100 ml or less. All such containers must be packed in a single transparent, resealable plastic bag of a maximum capacity of 1 liter. This rule applies to items like toners and perfumes. Larger containers (over 100 ml) are generally not allowed in hand luggage.
  • Power bank travel requirements in Japan: ensure the device has clear capacity labeling and (for international transit through China) a CCC mark. Stick to standard 5,000–20,000 mAh capacities to avoid security inspection delays.
  • In Japan, tax-free purchases are categorized into general goods (clothing, electronics) and consumable goods (cosmetics, food, drugs). Consumables are often sealed in clear anti-tamper bags which technically should not be opened until leaving the country. General goods do not require such sealing. Customs checks on these bags at departure are rare for tourists unless quantities are suspicious (bulk/resale). Most travelers open packages for luggage optimization without consequences.
  • IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) on Android devices are typically restricted to hardware manufactured for the Japanese market. Devices brought from outside Japan often fail to support digital wallet IC integration. Android users without Japanese hardware should rely on physical IC cards. Physical cards can be recharged with cash at station ticket machines or at major convenience stores (Lawson, 7-Eleven, FamilyMart).
  • For Shinkansen travel, medium-sized luggage (size M) fits comfortably on overhead racks. Oversized luggage requires specific reserved seats with designated luggage space, which should be selected during the booking process on platforms like Trip.com, Klook, or at station counters.
  • Domestic flights are a viable option for travel between Japanese cities, including journeys to Okinawa. Platforms like Trip.com can be used for booking tickets. Unlike mainland travel, Shinkansen services do not extend to Okinawa, making flights the primary mode of transport to the island.
  • Eating and drinking on Shinkansen (bullet trains) in Japan is not only acceptable but common. Bento boxes and beverages, including alcoholic options like beer, sake, and imported wines, are readily available for purchase at stations and on the train. While bringing your own alcohol is generally fine, purchasing it onboard is a convenient option.
  • Multi-day Japan rail passes typically expire at midnight on the last day of validity, not exactly 24/48/72 hours after activation. If you board a train before midnight, you can usually complete your journey. However, it's best to check the specific terms of your pass, as some may have different rules or require reservations for certain trains.
  • Tickets for Shinkansen (bullet trains) purchased without a reserved seat are typically valid for any train on the specified route and date, in non-reserved cars. You are not restricted to a specific departure time. However, if the ticket includes a reserved seat, it is valid only for that specific train and time.
