Mail & Shipping: how it works in Japan — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Mail & Shipping» in Japan — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • When transporting a MacBook from Japan to Russia via China, consider the following: 1. Personal Use: Carrying the device without its original box and without activating it may help present it as personal use to customs. 2. Tax-Free Purchases: Tax-free refunds are often processed at export. Keep receipts and the item itself for inspection. For electronics like laptops, inquire about specific customs regulations for both export from Japan and import into Russia and China. Some retailers may offer tax-free options, but it's crucial to understand the implications for customs declarations.
  • Travelers transiting through Chinese airports after departing Japan should be aware of potential luggage inspections. One traveler reported their checked bag being opened and a power bank for a portable fan being removed, with a note left behind. It is advisable to pack such items in carry-on luggage for easier inspection and to avoid potential confiscation. Customs and immigration checks in Japan were minimal, with no questions asked about receipts, goods, or cash carried.
  • Japan Travel Tips: Shinkansen tickets between major cities cost around 7,000 JPY. Local transport (metro, buses) averages 1,000 JPY/day. Capsule hotels are the cheapest accommodation (1,500-2,000 JPY). Book transport and accommodation in advance. eSIMs can be purchased via Trip.com with clear activation instructions. Souvenir katanas can be transported in checked luggage with proper documentation and handling by airport customs.
  • Transporting a large appliance like a microwave oven from Japan to Russia via checked baggage is feasible, provided it adheres to airline size and weight limits. Customs clearance is generally not an issue for personal items with a receipt. However, the primary concern is voltage compatibility. Japanese appliances often operate on 100-110V, requiring a step-down voltage transformer (100V to 220V) for use in Russia (220V). These transformers can be bulky and add to the shipping cost and complexity.
  • Bringing alcohol, such as sake, from Japan to Russia with a layover in China is generally possible if packed securely in checked luggage. Multiple travelers have reported successfully transporting bottles of alcohol without issues. Ensure items are well-packaged to prevent breakage during transit.
  • Inquire about the availability of a service to send luggage from a hotel to another location via Trip.com or if this service needs to be arranged directly with the hotel.
  • Direct mail service from Japan to Russia is suspended. A workaround involves using 'Belarus' as the destination country on the label while keeping the actual Russian address and postal code; the Belarusian postal service then routes it to Russia. Success is not guaranteed.
  • When exporting fishing reels from Japan to Russia, be aware of potential customs issues. Russian customs may consider more than two identical items as a commercial batch, potentially requiring tax payment. While items for personal use are generally allowed, exceeding certain quantity or value limits could lead to additional charges or scrutiny.
  • Bringing plants from Japan is restricted. Transporting plants with soil is prohibited. If transported without soil, the plant may not survive the journey. Additionally, a specific certificate is required for plant import. Research the exact certificate requirements before attempting to transport plants.
  • Importing manga and anime merchandise (especially adult-themed content) into China: Distribute the items across multiple checked bags to avoid suspicion. While customs may not always inspect personal belongings, large quantities or specific genres might attract scrutiny. Having proof of personal use can be helpful.
  • Luggage forwarding service (Takuhaibin) is available in most Japanese hotels. Guests can send luggage from one hotel to another hotel or address. Costs vary by size and distance (approx. 2000-4000 JPY per suitcase). It is possible to ship from convenience stores (combini) as well, but front desk service is more convenient for travelers. It usually takes 1-2 days for delivery.
  • Transporting a large appliance, such as a Panasonic NE-BS9E microwave oven (dimensions 56x54x43 cm in packaging), internationally is generally permissible as checked baggage, provided it meets size and weight restrictions. However, verification of the appliance's voltage compatibility (100-240V) is crucial for its use in the destination country. If the appliance only supports 100-120V, a voltage converter (transformer) will be necessary, which can be bulky. Customs declarations might be required, and retaining the purchase receipt is advisable.
  • Nama chocolate (Royce) contains high fat/cream content. It requires refrigeration or thermal insulation (cooler bag with ice packs) during transit. It is best purchased at duty-free in airports, where specialized cooling packaging is provided.
  • When transporting electronics like a CD player purchased in Japan, especially a used item without original packaging, it's recommended to pack it securely in checked luggage. Protective measures like wrapping the device in clothing or soft items can help prevent damage during transit. Be aware that new, high-end audio equipment in Japan can be very expensive.
  • Exporting a souvenir katana from Japan: Ensure you have official documentation from the store proving it is a souvenir. Declare it to customs at the Japanese airport, who will assist with proper packaging for checked baggage (oversized luggage). Avoid carrying it in hand luggage. Shipping via mail to Russia is not recommended due to potential issues and high costs. Transporting it as checked baggage with proper declaration is the safest method.
  • When importing items from Japan, personal use items like a Dollfie Dream doll are generally not an issue, even if their value exceeds 40,000 JPY. However, importing large quantities of items, especially those related to anime or manga, may raise questions about intent for resale and could lead to customs scrutiny or confiscation.
  • When using luggage forwarding services in Japan to hotels, inquire directly with the hotel about their policy on luggage storage duration if you plan to arrive a few days after your luggage. While hotels often hold luggage, confirm specific timeframes to avoid issues.
  • Electronics with batteries must be carried in hand luggage for safety reasons. Standard retail stores (off stores) typically do not offer tax-free refunds for these items. Customers should verify tax-free eligibility and procedures with the retailer, especially at large electronics stores, as specific policies may apply.
  • Sending packages from Japan to Russia is currently very difficult. Standard postal services are not available between the two countries due to Japan's geopolitical alignment. While some individuals might facilitate sending postcards via Belarus, the reliability is low. The primary obstacle is the lack of direct postal or courier services. Some indirect methods involving transits through other countries might exist but are complex and unreliable.
