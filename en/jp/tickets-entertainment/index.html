  • To view Mount Fuji, consider the following: 1. Visibility: Check local weather forecasts and visibility reports, as Fuji is often obscured by clouds. Some travelers use specific indexes or online resources to track visibility. 2. Locations: Popular viewpoints include Hakone, the Fuji Five Lakes region (Kawaguchiko), and certain high points in Tokyo. 3. Timing: Early mornings and clear, crisp days often offer the best chances. Traveling during drier seasons (late autumn to early spring) can also increase visibility.
  • For a short stay in Osaka (1.5 days), visitors can explore the aquarium and sample local cuisine such as takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, and kitsune udon. Day trips to nearby cities like Kobe or Himeji are possible, but allocating more time for Osaka itself is recommended to fully experience the city and its attractions, including Osaka Castle.
  • Traveling to Japan during 'Silver Week' (a series of holidays in September) results in significant crowds and high demand for accommodation and transportation. It is essential to book hotels, Shinkansen tickets, and popular attractions well in advance, potentially months ahead, to secure availability and avoid inflated prices. Consider adjusting travel dates to avoid this peak period.
  • Japan is widely considered one of the safest countries globally, contrary to some misconceptions fueled by media portrayals. While incidents like sexual harassment ('chikan') or assault can occur, they are rare, and Japan's overall safety record for solo female travelers is exceptionally high compared to many other destinations. Reassurances can include the availability of the Russian embassy contact, potential connections with Russian-speaking residents via language exchange apps, and emphasizing Japan's low crime rates. Fear-mongering narratives are generally unfounded.
  • Japanese souvenir stamps (eki stamp) require separate notebooks from Goshuin (temple seal books). Goshuin are considered sacred and are for temple visits only. For train station/sightseeing stamps, any high-quality notebook works. Umbrellas in communal stands at cafes or shops are often shared, but taking one is a local convention, not a formal right for tourists.
  • Japanese single-entry visas typically become valid on the date of issuance or the specific start date indicated. Entering Japan before this date is impossible. If a flight segment is missed in a single booking (e.g., Beijing-Tokyo), airlines often automatically cancel subsequent segments (no-show policy). Always verify visa validity dates against flight schedules before booking.
  • When visiting Mount Fuji, consider various viewpoints beyond Kawaguchiko, such as Fujinomiya or Enoshima Island, which also offer scenic views. Be prepared for potential cloud cover obscuring the mountain. An overnight stay might increase the chances of clear views, and checking weather forecasts is crucial for planning.

  • For visitors to Japan, Kyoto is recommended for historical sites and atmosphere, best visited early morning. Osaka is preferred for nightlife, modern shopping, and as a central hub for day trips to Nara, Kyoto, and Kobe.

  • Ghibli Museum tickets go on sale on the 10th of every month for the following month. International payment cards are required. Tickets sell out almost instantly. Third-party platforms like Klook offer tour packages, but availability is limited and expensive.
  • The Izu Peninsula is a recommended alternative to the overcrowded Kawaguchiko area. Key attractions include the Jogasaki Coast (lava formations), Mount Omuro, and various hot springs (onsen). It is accessible from Tokyo and offers a more relaxed atmosphere while still providing views of Mt. Fuji.
  • Planning a trip to Japan involves itinerary creation and budgeting. Travelers often build their own routes using resources like Google Maps, Trip.com, and Visit Japan. Costs can vary significantly based on flight and accommodation choices.   • Traveling to Japan during New Year (Oshogatsu): Expect fewer tourist crowds compared to peak seasons. Celebrations are primarily family-oriented. For festive atmosphere, visit areas like Shibuya or Omotesando. Consider Kyushu for hot springs and scenery; book transport and accommodation in advance due to potential limited availability. Enjoy clear skies for Fuji views.

Chuo City is closer to the river, but Roppongi provides a more central base for general sightseeing and day trips.
