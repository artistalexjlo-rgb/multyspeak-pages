Documents & Visa in Georgia: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Georgia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • For vehicle entry into Georgia from Russia, if the mother is the owner and the son is driving (with the mother in the car), a power of attorney is typically required. Vehicles equipped with LPG systems may face scrutiny, so ensure all documentation and modifications are compliant. Vehicle insurance can be purchased upon arrival at the border.
  • For entry into Georgia with pets (cats/dogs), the following are required: 1) Microchip, 2) Valid vaccination certificate (vaccinations must be performed AFTER microchipping to be valid), 3) International veterinary passport, 4) Official health certificate issued by a state veterinary clinic within 2 days of departure.
  • Medical insurance is officially required for entry into Georgia as of 2026. While border control checks are inconsistent (some travelers report no verification), medical services for foreigners are expensive. A standard travel insurance policy costs approximately 1500 RUB per week.
  • Bringing a homeopathic medicine kit to Batumi, Georgia, is generally not an issue. Customs officials are unlikely to question or confiscate such items, as they are perceived as inert substances. The primary concern would be if the kit appears suspicious, but standard homeopathic preparations are usually allowed without difficulty. The user can proceed with bringing their kit.
  • Visa run strategy: One-day round trips (e.g., Tbilisi to Yerevan or Batumi to Sarpi/Turkey) are accepted practices for border renewal. Short stays are sufficient for entry/exit requirements.
  • Medical insurance is mandatory for entry into Georgia. It is checked at the border crossing points, including Lars. Failure to present valid insurance may result in a fine of approximately 300 GEL.
  • When crossing the land border into Georgia with valuable items like an engagement ring worth €6000, it is advisable to check the customs regulations regarding declaration. While wearing the item might seem like a way to avoid inspection, it's best to be informed about official procedures to prevent potential issues. Consult the official Georgian customs website or contact them directly for accurate information on declaration thresholds and requirements.
  • When processing multiple documents at the Georgian Embassy (e.g., georgia.mid), where each document involves two individuals, determine the number of service tokens required. If each document requires a separate token, and you are processing three documents with two individuals per document, you may need to obtain multiple tokens. It's best to clarify the token system with embassy staff upon arrival or check their official website for guidance on appointment booking and service allocation.
  • When entering Georgia, amounts equivalent to 10,000 USD or more must be declared to customs. Amounts below this threshold do not require mandatory declaration.
  • For visa runs in Georgia, travel insurance is required, typically with a minimum coverage of 30,000 GEL. While sites like polis.ge offer options, purchasing directly from the insurance provider can be cheaper (around 2 GEL per day). Be aware that visa run participants might be denied claims if they need to use the insurance, as they may not be classified as tourists, but it usually satisfies border control requirements.
  • When entering Georgia by car, if the vehicle is registered to your spouse but you will be driving, a notarized power of attorney from the owner is typically required. This document must explicitly grant permission for international travel (выезд за границу). Without this, you may face issues at the border crossing.
  • When importing Georgian alcohol into Russia, each adult is allowed up to 3 liters without duty. While plastic, factory-sealed bottles are generally accepted, Russian customs law technically requires original factory packaging. Be aware that while most travellers face no issues, adhering strictly to the law regarding packaging is recommended to avoid potential complications.
  • Travelers entering Georgia must have valid medical insurance covering the entire duration of their stay. While insurance checks are reported to be rare upon entry, they are more common upon exit. Failure to present valid insurance upon exit can result in a fine of 300 Georgian Lari (GEL).
  • It is possible to enter Georgia by car, leave the vehicle, and then fly out, including to a third country. To do this legally, submit a written statement to the customs office (Gümrük) indicating the address where the car will be stored. This procedure differs from some other countries, like Turkey.
  • Overstaying your permitted duration of stay in Georgia by two months will likely result in a six-month entry ban. It is crucial to adhere to visa regulations and depart the country or extend your stay legally before the expiration date to avoid such penalties.
  • Importing dogs to Georgia requires a veterinary certificate obtained from Rosselkhoznadzor. Rabies antibody titers are not required. Microchipping is recommended.
  • Traveler driving a car to Georgia asks if medical insurance is mandatory and checked. Confirms car insurance can be obtained post-customs. Requests information on necessary documents if the car isn't registered to them and on criteria for medical insurance, including whether the cheapest option is acceptable.
  • To import a pet (dog) into Georgia by car from Russia, specific documents are required. These include proof of rabies vaccination (administered at least 30 days prior to travel), microchipping, and an international veterinary passport. For car travel, it is advisable to obtain animal exit documents from the airport in advance. Document checks can vary; sometimes they are not performed on entry into Georgia, but may be stricter upon exit. Ensure all documentation is up-to-date, especially for larger breeds or specific breeds like Maltipoo.
  • Customs declaration for cash is free in Georgia. However, when depositing large sums into a bank account, banks may request documentation regarding the origin of funds, which can take 1-2 days for verification. Banks may refuse transactions involving cryptocurrency due to concerns about its origin.
  • Medical insurance is mandatory for travel through Georgia. It can be purchased online from Russian insurance providers like Ingosstrakh, Tinkoff Insurance, or Alfa Insurance. Ensure the policy covers the duration of your stay, whether it's a continuous period or split into segments for entry and exit.
  • Clarification on documents required for car entry into Georgia: if the owner travels with the user, only the STS (vehicle registration) is needed. If the user is traveling alone with a car not registered to them, a notarized power of attorney, translated into English or Georgian, is required.
  • For tourists entering Georgia via land borders: vaccine checks are not performed. Pet travel is generally very simple, and pets may even go unnoticed by border guards. There's a dedicated chat for animal transport, suggesting this is a common topic.
  • When driving a car into Georgia that is not registered in your name (e.g., owned by a parent), it is highly recommended to have a formally issued power of attorney (доверенность). Without this document, you may face issues at the border crossing. Ensure all vehicle registration and personal identification documents are in order to facilitate a smooth entry.
  • Inquire about the general attitude towards Muslims and women wearing headscarves in Georgia. Specifically, ask if there are potential issues at the border when entering the country with religious attire. Mention that Adjara is historically a Muslim region but seek current perspectives.
  • Provide advice regarding the use of toilet facilities at the Larsi border crossing on the Georgian side, warning about potential discomfort due to open cubicles and proximity to male facilities. Suggest using facilities on the Russian side beforehand.
  • Bringing USD to Georgia is more cost-effective than bringing RUB due to unfavorable exchange rates and double conversion issues. Older USD banknotes (e.g., 1996 series) are widely accepted.
  • Travel medical insurance is mandatory for entering Georgia. The document must be in English or Georgian. If issued by a Russian bank (e.g., Alfa Bank), it often comes in English by default. If the policy is in Russian, it must be translated. Policies can sometimes be purchased directly at the border crossing.
