Housing in Belarus: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Housing» in Belarus — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Finding monthly apartment rentals in Bereza, Belarus, can be challenging as many properties are geared towards short-term corporate stays, making daily rates expensive. Long-term rentals may require searching on platforms like Kufar.be, with potential for higher pricing compared to less tourist-frequented areas.
  • To be deregistered from military service in Russia due to relocation to Belarus for more than six months (e.g., to avoid conscription), Russian citizens typically need to provide proof of Belarusian residency, such as a Temporary Residence Permit (TRP/РВП) or a Permanent Residence Permit (PRP/ВНЖ). Standard travel documents like flight tickets or rental agreements are usually insufficient. Additionally, Belarus allows a maximum stay of 90 days per year for non-residents without a TRP or PRP.
  • To find accommodation in Brest without a Belarusian phone number, use platforms like Kufar.by or Sutochno.ru. Be aware that monthly rates on short-term rental sites can be high. Direct communication with landlords via booking platforms is possible, allowing for negotiation outside of the platform's fees. Avoid unofficial agents who may pose a scam risk.
  • Real estate rental payments in Belarus must be conducted in Belarusian rubles (BYN). Contracts can denominate the price in foreign currency equivalents, but actual settlements in USD/EUR are prohibited and subject to administrative fines. US dollar bills of any design are legal tender, though newer series are often preferred for convenience.
  • Budget housing (apartments) can be found in 'agrogorodoks' (small rural towns) for prices significantly lower than in major cities (e.g., 4000-5000 BYN). Online classifieds like Kufar.by often lack these offers; local networking and word-of-mouth are more effective for finding available housing.
  • Kufar is the primary platform for property rentals in Belarus. Registration often requires a local Belarusian SIM card. Non-residents may struggle to register; some users bypass this by registering via social media (VK) or asking locals to provide contact details from listings. Payment is usually processed via local bank transfers as per regulations.
  • To book a room at the 'Express' hotel in Minsk from Russia, users recommend checking platforms like 'Sutochno.ru' or 'Realt.by'. Avito is also mentioned as a potential option for booking accommodations. Direct booking methods are not specified.
  • For affordable initial accommodation in Belarus, consider checking platforms like "Kufar" or "Realt" for listings. These sites often provide a range of options for short-term rentals while you explore the area.
  • For finding rental housing in Grodno, Belarus, besides 'Kufar', users suggest checking 'Avito' and 'Sutochno.ru'. 'Kufar' is described as similar to 'Avito'. 'Flatbook' is also recommended. Some platforms might require a Belarusian phone number for registration, but booking might still be possible.
  • When searching for housing on Kufar.by, be cautious of landlords requesting direct prepayment to a personal card instead of through the platform's secure system. While some landlords may request a small prepayment (e.g., 10-20 BYN) to confirm booking dates, always prioritize using the platform's payment options for security and dispute resolution. If a landlord insists on direct transfer, verify their reviews and consider alternative listings. Kufar.by offers options for booking with Russian cards.
  • Realt.by is a verified and trustworthy platform for real estate rentals in Belarus. However, users should remain cautious as fraudulent activities can occur on any online marketplace. It is advisable to exercise due diligence when booking through the platform.
  • Russian citizens can stay in Belarus for up to 90 days. For longer stays, RVP (temporary residence permit) or VNZh (permanent residence permit) is required, necessitating a work contract, study enrollment, or property ownership. When searching for rentals, initially avoid disclosing Russian citizenship. Offering several months' rent in advance or a higher rate might help secure a property. Ensure the rental agreement is officially registered.
  • Russian bank cards (Mir, etc.) can be used in Belarus for certain services. Sberbank Online allows payments for Belarusian services (e.g., sanatoriums) via bank operators. Mobile top-ups can be managed through EPIP (ERIP) via Paritet Bank or directly through mobile operators' apps from Russia.
  • When renting property in Belarus as a Russian citizen, long-term options are harder to secure. Use local platforms like Kufar or Realt. It is standard practice to register the lease agreement officially at the local settlement center (RSC). Being a mother with a child is often perceived as a 'plus' for reliability.
  • To obtain a Temporary Residence Permit (VNZh) in Belarus through property acquisition, the property must be habitable. Homes designated for demolition typically do not qualify. Furthermore, registering residency based on a contract for a property under construction is usually not permitted, thus hindering VNZh application.
  • In Belarus, some landlords request passport details of all guests before check-in, citing regulations for reporting to migration services. This practice is more common in cities like Grodno and Brest, especially for self-check-in or late arrivals. While some travelers find this unusual compared to other countries, it is considered a legitimate practice by some responsible landlords to comply with regulations. Providing passport information via text is preferred over sending photos. Registration typically occurs within three hours after check-in.
  • It is possible to pay utility bills in Belarus in advance for one to two months using the ERIp system. This is generally preferred over incurring debt.
  • Purchasing property in Belarus as a foreigner is possible, provided the necessary funds are available. Standard procedures are in place to verify the legal integrity and absence of encumbrances related to the property transaction.
  • For finding accommodation in Minsk, use dedicated rental platforms. Be cautious of offers in Telegram chats, as they are often associated with scammers.
  • Purchasing vacant properties in Belarus for a nominal fee (around one basic unit) is a potential pathway to obtaining residency (VNZh). However, the property must be habitable and suitable for year-round living, not a ruin. The process of obtaining VNZh after purchase can still be complex, with potential rejections even for homeowners, sometimes suggesting marriage as an alternative. Resources like the registry of vacant houses ('eri2nca.by') can provide listings.
  • Real estate transactions in Belarus for foreign citizens are legal but complicated by land ownership regulations. Land associated with a private house often needs to be converted to a long-term lease from the state before a sale to a non-citizen can be completed. Sellers may decline such offers to avoid this administrative burden, preferring domestic buyers to streamline the process.
  • Rental prices for studios and one-bedroom apartments in Minsk, Belarus, show significant variation on platforms like Avito. There is a wide range, from approximately 20,000 Russian Rubles to 150,000 Russian Rubles per month. It is recommended to research average market rates and consult local forums or real estate groups for accurate pricing information and potential VNZh-related housing advice.
  • While some landlords in Belarus request passport details prior to check-in for registration purposes, this is not universally practiced. Some renters have experienced situations where no contract was signed, or it was completed upon arrival. The practice of requesting details beforehand is often aimed at saving time during check-in, especially for contactless arrivals. It is not mandatory to send passport photos; providing the data via text is sufficient. The primary registration process typically occurs within three hours post-check-in.
  • For finding long-term rental apartments in Belarus, users recommend 'Kufar.by' (similar to Avito) and 'Realt.by' (comparable to Cian). 'Sutochno.ru' is also mentioned. It is advised to look for verified landlords.
  • For booking accommodations in Belarus, popular platforms include 'Kufar.by' (similar to Avito), 'Realt.by' (similar to Cian), and 'Sutochno.ru'. 'Flatbook' is also mentioned as an option. It is recommended to verify landlord or host reliability.
  • Payments for Belarusian services (utilities, mobile providers like A1) can be made directly via Russian banking apps (e.g., Sberbank) by searching for 'Pay A1 Belarus' or using the 'ERIP' service. Direct transfers between Russian and Belarusian cards are generally not possible.
  • Booking accommodation in Belarus, especially for popular travel dates (e.g., June), requires advance planning. Popular booking platforms like Ostrovok and Yandex.Travel may have limited availability or increased prices during peak seasons. Users seeking last-minute bookings might find options scarce. It is advisable to book several weeks or even months in advance, particularly for specific dates or preferred locations, as availability can diminish quickly due to high demand and potentially lower prices compared to some Russian destinations.
  • Purchasing property in Belarus for residency purposes varies significantly in price. While rumors of houses costing as little as one basic unit (approx. 45 BYN) exist, these are typically dilapidated and unsuitable for VNZh. Habitable properties suitable for residency usually start from $1,000-$2,000 USD and can be considerably more expensive. Both Russians and other nationalities are reported to buy property for residency, car registration, or relocation, with varying degrees of renovation required.
  • Acquiring property in Belarus for the purpose of obtaining a Temporary Residence Permit (VNZh) requires the property to be habitable. Homes slated for demolition are generally not suitable. Likewise, purchasing an apartment in a building under construction may not qualify for VNZh as it typically does not provide the necessary registration for residency.
  • Landlords in Belarus may request photos of Russian citizens' passports for migration registration purposes, particularly after July, due to new regulations. It is advisable to discuss this with the landlord and potentially provide redacted passport photos.
  • Russian citizens seeking long-term apartment rentals and permanent registration in Belarus might face challenges. While it's possible to rent an apartment, obtaining permanent registration ('propiska') can be complex and may require specific conditions or agreements with landlords. Some landlords may be hesitant to provide permanent registration due to administrative complexities or potential landlord taxes. It is advisable to clarify registration possibilities thoroughly with the landlord before signing any rental agreement. Some users suggest that obtaining a VNZH (residence permit) might facilitate the process, but this also involves specific residency requirements.
  • The aquacenter in Grodno is described as small but reasonably priced. Entry to the main pool area with slides is charged hourly, with separate fees for sauna access. Accommodation in apartments typically includes Wi-Fi. The aquacenter is accessible by public transport within the city. Many bus stops feature electronic displays with schedules, aiding navigation.
  • Renting property long-term in Belarus may present challenges if you lack a Belarusian phone number for registration. Platforms like Kufar typically require a local SIM card. Additionally, using Russian platforms like Avito for booking Belarusian properties might be restricted due to their registration policies.
  • For medium-term rentals (1 month) in Belarus, platforms like Sutochno or Ostrovok are common for initial search, but rates can be high. Seasonal price drops occur after the summer peak (starting in September). Negotiating directly with landlords for cash payments upon arrival is a common practice to secure a lower rate compared to online-only bookings.
  • Rental agreements (dogovory nayma) in Belarus, especially for long-term leases, generally require registration. This process typically involves visiting a local registration center (like the RSC in Grodno) with the property owner. One copy of the registered agreement is sent to the tax authorities. The specific registration location might vary by region (e.g., administration office in Brest).
  • Purchasing a property in Belarus can be a basis for obtaining a Temporary Residence Permit (RVP). However, the property must be habitable year-round. If purchasing a dilapidated house, significant investment in repairs will be required, and alternative accommodation might be needed during renovations. Unlike the permanent residence permit (VNZh), RVP offers more flexibility, allowing for renting an apartment while renovating or building.
