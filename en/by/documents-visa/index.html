However, this is a legal loophole that can be closed or monitored at any time; relying on this as a guaranteed method to bypass travel bans for debt or alimony is high-risk.
  • Purchasing a property in Belarus can be a basis for obtaining a Temporary Residence Permit (RVP). However, the property must be habitable year-round. If purchasing a dilapidated house, significant investment in repairs will be required, and alternative accommodation might be needed during renovations. Unlike the permanent residence permit (VNZh), RVP offers more flexibility, allowing for renting an apartment while renovating or building.
  • Citizens of the Russian Federation can apply for a Permanent Residence Permit (VNZH) in Belarus without specific 'reasons' (like property ownership or marriage) due to the Union State agreement. Requirements: valid Russian passport, proof of permanent registration, and a medical certificate (form 1 zdr/u-10) issued in Belarus. Foreign medical documents (like those from Russia) are generally not accepted for the mandatory local health assessment. VNZH procedure often involves: medical exam, biography, and payment of administrative fees (usually 2 base units for permit + ID card).
