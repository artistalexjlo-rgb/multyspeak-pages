Shopping in Belarus: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Shopping» in Belarus — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • The acceptance of Russian MIR payment cards in Belarus can be inconsistent. While some transactions may be successful, widespread issues have been reported. It is advisable for travelers to have alternative payment methods, such as cash or cards from other international payment systems, as MIR card functionality is not guaranteed across all merchants and banks in Belarus.
  • Accessing seller phone numbers on the Kufar platform (a Belarusian classifieds website) typically requires a Belarusian SIM card. It is not possible to view these numbers without one. If you need to contact a seller, you may need to ask a friend or acquaintance with a Belarusian SIM card to assist you in obtaining the phone number.
  • As of the current information, H&M stores are not present in Belarus. Searches for locations in cities like Brest, Grodno, or Minsk will not yield results for this brand.
  • For bulk purchases of Lidskoe beer in Minsk, standard grocery stores will sell individual bottles or packs. There are no dedicated wholesale markets for alcohol like in some other countries. Consider visiting larger retail chains or hypermarkets like Unistore.
  • Kufar is the primary platform for property rentals in Belarus. Registration often requires a local Belarusian SIM card. Non-residents may struggle to register; some users bypass this by registering via social media (VK) or asking locals to provide contact details from listings. Payment is usually processed via local bank transfers as per regulations.
  • Registering for Avito from Belarus can be problematic as the platform often requires a Russian phone number. While workarounds using virtual number services (like 'onlinesim') might exist, they are not always reliable. Using social media accounts (Google, VK) for initial signup may bypass the phone number requirement temporarily, but a verified phone number is often needed for full functionality. Acquiring a Russian SIM card might be a more stable solution.
  • The 'Syty Putnik' hotel complex is located over 40 km from Minsk. Delivery services may be available, but it is advisable to confirm directly with the hotel regarding their specific offerings and proximity for external deliveries.
  • Purchasing and registering a car in Belarus for Russian citizens can be complex. Requirements may include Belarusian residency (VNZh) or a Belarusian intermediary ('prokladka'). Cars imported into Belarus might be recognized as EAEU goods by Russian customs, but preferential recycling fees might not be waived, potentially requiring additional payments. Consult with customs officials for specific procedures and costs.
  • On certain holidays (e.g., graduation days), Belarus prohibits the retail sale of alcohol in supermarkets and retail stores. This restriction typically applies to drinks with an alcohol content of 7% or higher. Bars, restaurants, and cafes selling alcohol by the glass are exempt from these bans.
  • Russian bank cards (Mir, etc.) can be used in Belarus for certain services. Sberbank Online allows payments for Belarusian services (e.g., sanatoriums) via bank operators. Mobile top-ups can be managed through EPIP (ERIP) via Paritet Bank or directly through mobile operators' apps from Russia.
  • Recommended confectionery brands in Belarus include 'Kommunarka'. Popular items to purchase include marshallows (zefir), cranberries in sugar, pastila, and unique flavor combinations like gooseberry candies or spicy pepper halva. Main retail locations include brand shops or the 'Pershy' National Trade House.
  • To visit Belovezhskaya Pushcha, purchase tickets at the entrance ticket office. Transportation to the park needs to be arranged separately; consider group transport options like those found on Kufar if traveling with a large group. For the best experience with animals, visit the enclosures upon opening (9 AM) and bring food. Dining options include the 'Sosny' cafe (affordable and tasty) and the main building's restaurant (pricier). Allocate at least a full day, ideally two, to explore the enclosures, take guided tours, and enjoy activities like cycling.
  • Major electronics retailers in Belarus include '5 element' and 'Elektrosila'. 'DNS' may also be available. Customers advise selecting specific items to avoid upselling of unwanted products.
  • Registering on Avito (a Russian classifieds platform) using a Belarusian phone number might be problematic. Users looking to register with a Belarusian number may need to use a Russian phone number for the initial registration or verification process. It is generally recommended to use a phone number from the country where the service is primarily based or targeted for full functionality and to avoid potential registration issues.
  • July in Belarus typically features variable weather. Daytime temperatures can range widely, from around 15°C to 35°C. It is advisable to pack layers, including a light jacket, as evenings can be cooler and rain is possible. The weather is generally comparable to central Russia during the same month.
  • Prices for brands like Nike, Puma, and Diesel in Belarus are generally comparable to those in Russia. While significant discounts are not always guaranteed, sales periods may offer more favorable pricing.
  • According to user feedback, 'shop.by' offers the lowest prices and the largest selection. '5 element' is also mentioned as a place to find electronics.
  • Duty-Free shops may be available at major border crossings between Belarus and neighboring countries, as well as at Minsk Airport. It is advisable to check specific locations and operating hours in advance.
  • Inquire about local souvenirs from Belarus. Seeking information on bus routes from Grodno to Brest, specifically looking for options suitable for children who get carsick, possibly larger buses without air conditioning.
  • To obtain a better price and a UDD-ZhD (Universal Transport Document for Railway Transport) for train travel from Russia to Belarus, purchase tickets to Smolensk instead of directly to Minsk. Traveling directly to Minsk may require a Schengen visa for Russian citizens. Both Russian and Belarusian railway regulations allow passengers to board at intermediate stations if the ticket is paid for until the final destination. Failure to board at the departure station does not automatically annul the ticket.
  • Electronics prices, particularly for Chinese smartphones like Xiaomi and Huawei, tend to be higher in Belarus compared to Russia. For instance, a Samsung A56 model was observed to be over 5,000 rubles more expensive in Belarus.
  • In Belarus, the sale of alcoholic beverages with an ethyl alcohol content of 7% or higher may be restricted, especially on holidays. This restriction does not apply to drinks with less than 7% alcohol.
  • Kufar is a popular classifieds platform in Belarus. To use Kufar effectively, especially for interacting with sellers or landlords, a local Belarusian phone number is often recommended or required for registration and verification. Russian phone numbers may face limitations, and accounts linked to them might be restricted or blocked. While it might be possible to register with a Russian number initially, obtaining a Belarusian SIM card is often suggested for full functionality. Some users have reported issues with blocked accounts, potentially due to inactivity of the associated SIM card or platform-specific restrictions.
  • If you cannot pick up a Wildberries order in Belarus, simply not showing up can result in a penalty (around 200 BYN per item), especially if you have a low repurchase rate. It is advisable to officially cancel or return the order to avoid these charges. The penalty may not apply if the repurchase rate is high.
  • When traveling to Belarus, Russian citizens can generally use Russian bank cards (including Sberbank, PSB, Tinkoff) for payments. For cash withdrawals, using Belarusian bank ATMs, such as those of Belorusbank, is recommended to avoid high commissions (e.g., Sberbank's 5.5% fee). The 'Mir' card often offers favorable exchange rates, but it's advisable to check specific bank rates. For substantial amounts, exchanging cash at an exchange office might be more cost-effective.
  • To buy good quality sausage and meat in Brest, consider visiting the official store of the Brest Meat Processing Plant or exploring the meat stalls at the Kolkhoz Market.
  • Keep receipts from border gas stations or cafes as potential proof of purchases, especially if crossing borders, as these might be requested by authorities.
  • Using a 'Mir' card for payments in Belarus can be more advantageous than exchanging cash. For example, a transaction of 100 Belarusian rubles might cost around 2100 Russian rubles (a rate of 27.27 per BYN) when paid by card. In contrast, exchanging cash might yield a less favorable rate, such as 3.505 BYN for 100 RUB (a rate of 28.53 RUB per BYN). Therefore, using a 'Mir' card is recommended for potentially better exchange rates.
  • For navigating cities like Vitebsk, Belarus, and finding points of interest such as cafes and attractions, users recommend using map applications that can provide both navigation and information on local amenities. While specific app recommendations vary, the general advice is to use a reliable navigation tool that integrates points of interest.
  • Recommended restaurants in Minsk for a pleasant dining experience include 'Bulbashi' (ul. Gertsena, 4) for its atmosphere, 'Paliushki' (ul. Vitebskaya, 7) in the Rakovskoye Predmestye area, and 'Podvorie'.
  • In Minsk, alcohol sales are generally unrestricted, even on holidays like graduation days. While some regions in Belarus might have sales limitations on certain dates, Minsk typically allows purchases without issue. Check local news for any specific, rare exceptions.
  • Popular items to bring back from Belarus include: magnets, 'sanctioned' goods like Coca-Cola, Sprite, and Fanta (which are in short supply in Russia), and Belarusian sausages.
