Tickets & Entertainment in Indonesia: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Tickets & Entertainment» in Indonesia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • To find group activities for children in Nusa Dua, Bali, such as Thai boxing, judo, football, or drawing, local sports clubs, international schools, and community centers are good starting points. Online searches using terms like 'kids sports Nusa Dua,' 'children's art classes Bali,' or 'martial arts for kids Nusa Dua' can provide specific options and contacts. Enquire in local expat or family chat groups for recommendations.
  • Uluwatu is highly recommended for sunset views, specifically featuring cliffside scenery and surf breaks. Popular venues like 'Single Fin' are frequently cited for optimal viewpoints.
  • Visiting Sekumpul, Hidden, and Fiji waterfalls is highly recommended. A tip for cost savings: stay at a nearby hotel that provides a badge granting access to Sekumpul for IDR 30,000 and Fiji for IDR 20,000, avoiding mandatory guides. Hotel cost was approximately IDR 190,000 per night.
  • The weather in Bali in early November is a transitional period between the dry and wet seasons. Rainfall is possible, as indicated by a report of rain on November 1st of the previous year. The duration and intensity of rain can vary.
  • Gili Air offers excellent snorkeling (sea turtles) and varied infrastructure. Key travel tip: research beaches using Google Maps to check tide impacts, as extreme low tides can make swimming difficult.
  • The Gili Islands differ in atmosphere: Gili Trawangan is known for its lively party scene ('hell of Canggu'). Gili Air offers a slightly calmer vibe with some social activity. Gili Meno is the quietest, suitable for relaxation. Gili Gede is very secluded.
  • Google Maps operational statuses for Bali's natural attractions, such as Kanto Lampo waterfall, are often unreliable due to poor business listing maintenance. However, heavy overnight rain can cause actual, temporary closures due to dangerous currents. To confirm if a waterfall is open, tourists should check the most recent Google Maps reviews filtered by date ('newest') or consult local tour operators before departing.
  • Lake Yeh Malet in Karangasem has officially reopened to visitors after a major clean-up operation that cleared decades of silt and overgrowth. It is safe for swimming and local fishing, and offers clear, scenic views of Mount Agung. It serves as an excellent alternative stopover destination for travelers heading to or from Amed.
  • Tides and currents can vary across different beaches in Bali. While the general pattern of high and low tides is consistent, their intensity can be more noticeable in some locations, such as Nusa Dua, compared to others like Kuta. Seminyak experiences high tide.
  • Hiking Mount Batur is recommended at night for sunrise to avoid extreme heat. Official guides are generally required for the trekking path; booking through tour operators ensures logistics and better photo opportunities.
  • Full-face snorkel masks with integrated valves are discouraged for open-water swimming due to potential CO2 buildup issues, which can lead to fainting and drowning risks. Standard two-piece mask and snorkel sets are recommended for safe snorkeling.
  • Mount Batur sunrise jeep tour: Departure is typically around 3:00 AM. Guides and jeep drivers are readily available at the base parking area (Pura Pasar Agung) without advance booking. Pricing is negotiable.
  • During the peak dry season in August and September, Bali experiences cooler temperatures and cold evening winds driven by winter air masses moving from Australia. Ocean water temperatures also decrease by a few degrees. Travelers planning night-time scooter rides or staying near coastal areas should bring light jackets or hoodies.
  • Pancasila Day is celebrated on June 1st in Indonesia, commemorating the five principles of Indonesia's state ideology: belief in one God, just and civilized humanity, Indonesian unity, democracy, and social justice. Government offices, banks, and some services may be closed, while tourist establishments generally remain open. Official ceremonies and flag-raising events are held.
  • Best beaches for swimming in Nusa Dua and surrounding areas: For calm waters suitable for swimming, consider Mengiat, Geger, Melasti, and Sanur beaches. Amed is also recommended for its tranquil waters.
  • User has arrived in Kuta and is asking for nearby attractions. They are informed that Kuta primarily offers surfing, malls, and cafes, with main attractions located in Uluwatu and Ubud.
  • Sekumpul waterfall is Bali's largest and highly recommended. Nearby Hidden and Fiji waterfalls are also worth visiting. Staying at a local hotel might provide a badge for discounted entry and avoid mandatory guides. Tukad Cepung Waterfall is another beautiful option.
  • Road reconstruction of the key 8km section Sampalan-Toyapakeh on Nusa Penida started in mid-2026 and is scheduled to last for 240 days. Expect heavy traffic delays and poor road conditions on the western side of the island.
  • Late June to early July is generally considered the dry season in Bali, making it a good time for a holiday with favorable weather conditions.
  • Mount Agung hiking: Entrance fees for Besakih Temple include sarong rental. Authorized guides are recommended for safety. Avoid independent guides offering services at the entrance to prevent overcharging. Altitude is high; physical preparation is required. Use specialized 'Bali Trip' or 'Travel Companion' community chats to find groups.
  • Bali weather is tropical and highly localized. While the dry season is reliable for coastal activities, rain can occur unexpectedly, particularly in mountainous regions. Weather forecasts in the tropics are generally only reliable for short-term observations.
  • When visiting Tegalalang Rice Terraces in Bali, the official entrance fee is 50,000 IDR. Visitors must keep the printed entrance receipt. Some cafes and viewpoints along the terraces require proof of entry; showing this receipt prevents double charging.
  • May 14, 2026, is the Ascension of Jesus Christ (Kenaikan Yesus Kristus), a public holiday in Indonesia. Friday, May 15, 2026, is also a holiday. Expect reduced hours or closures for banks, schools, and government offices.
  • Bali experiences a cooler 'dry season' (winter) during June-August, where nighttime and early morning temperatures can feel chilly, especially when riding a scooter. Water temperatures also drop by a few degrees compared to the December rainy season peak.
  • For a beach holiday in the Bukit area of Bali, Nusa Dua is recommended for a fresher, more relaxed vibe. For a younger, more social atmosphere, Uluwatu is suggested, although its beaches are more scenic than ideal for lounging.
  • Wave conditions at Nusa Dua, Bali: While some spots like those near Conrad hotel may have strong waves and murky water, reef spots like Bali Soul, Mengiat, Geger, and Sawangan generally have calmer waters further from shore, suitable for swimming. Check surf forecasts on Surfline or Windy.app for specific conditions.
  • For baby shower decorations in Bali, specifically flowers and balloons, it is recommended to inquire at local party supply stores or florists. Ideas for welcoming a new family member can be sought from local event planners or online resources.
  • Ijen volcano tours often advertise 'blue flames' which are currently not visible due to crater closure and safety restrictions. The climb is approximately 3km, generally manageable, but requires proper physical preparation. Sulfur gas can be intense; masks are recommended but usage depends on wind direction.
  • Nusa Dua is located on the eastern side of the Bukit peninsula, meaning it only offers views of the sunrise. For ocean sunsets, travelers must visit the western side of Bali, such as Uluwatu, Jimbaran, Seminyak, or Canggu.
  • To practice English with native speakers in Bali, join interest-based clubs (e.g., running, padel, diving tours). Engaging in group activities and communicating with locals and expats from various English-speaking countries (Australian, American, British) is effective. Consistent practice is key.
  • The appearance of Jatiluwih rice terraces (lush green vs. muddy puddles) depends on the harvest cycle, not a specific month each year. Rice typically takes around 4 months from planting to harvest. Late March sees freshly planted rice. Early May is described as 'lush green' with unripe rice, making it an ideal time for scenic views. September might be after harvest, showing 'puddles'.
  • Waterboom Bali is a water park located in the heart of Kuta, Bali. It features slides for all levels, including the 'Climax' capsule slide. Online ticket purchase is recommended for avoiding queues and arranging transfers.
  • For DIY silver jewelry making workshops in Bali, visit the village of Celuk, located near Ubud. Celuk is famous as the traditional silver- and gold-smithing center, offering affordable handcrafting classes for tourists.
  • To travel from Bali to Lombok by water, the primary option is the public ferry from Padang Bai. Additionally, various fast boat services also operate between Bali and Lombok, offering quicker but generally more expensive transfers.
  • Key attractions in Ubud include the Monkey Forest, the Artists' Trail, the Reptile/Bird Park, souvenir markets, and nearby waterfalls and rice terraces like Tegalalang. While some attractions like temples are within the center, most others require transportation (taxi or motorbike). Walking is not practical for most sights outside the immediate center.
  • For a quiet vacation with good snorkeling, Gili Air or Gili Meno are recommended over Gili Trawangan, which is known for parties. Nusa Dua does not offer significant snorkeling opportunities. Gili Air provides a more tranquil environment compared to the busier Bali.
  • High UV exposure near the equator can cause significant sunburns in as little as 30 minutes during peak hours (11:00–16:00). Daily use of SPF is essential for skin safety, and outdoor activities are recommended before 10:00 or after 16:00.
  • Recommended swimming beaches in Bali include Nusa Dua (Geger, Mengiat) for calm water. Canggu and Seminyak are primarily for surfing and nightlife. Expect variable beach conditions; swimming at surfing beaches can be hazardous.
  • October in Bali is a transitional period between the dry and wet seasons, characterized by high humidity and potential rainfall. It is advised to check forecasts closer to the travel date as weather patterns can vary annually.
  • When visiting Bali, consider exploring different areas. For beautiful beaches, start or end on the Bukit Peninsula (Nusa Dua). Allocate most of your time to Ubud for its nature and atmosphere. Canggu is recommended for a lively scene, while the northern parts of the island offer tranquility and fewer tourists. Assistance with accommodation and itineraries is available.
  • Traveling to Sumbawa by land and sea (ferry/boat) is generally not feasible within a 24-hour period. It's a time-consuming journey. For ferry and boat schedules, check relevant travel websites or inquire in local transport chat groups.
  • For a quieter experience with good snorkeling, Gili Air is recommended over Gili Trawangan. Gili Air offers better tranquility and a more relaxed atmosphere, although infrastructure might be less developed than on Trawangan. Gili Meno is also an option for seclusion.
  • Beach conditions on the Bukit Peninsula (Bali) are heavily influenced by the lunar cycle. High tide during full moon periods can lead to stronger currents and waves even in lagoon-like areas. For families with children, it is recommended to monitor tide tables, but beach accessibility and conditions change daily regardless of forecasts.
  • A fire dance performance, known as Kecak dance, is held at Uluwatu temple in Bali. It is a traditional Balinese ritual performed at sunset. It is advisable to check for specific timings and ticketing information.
  • Description of animal sightings in Bali: mentions monkeys, monitor lizards, birds, dolphins in Lovina. Notes that monitor lizards can be seen at Bali Zoo and are common. Mentions that Binturong is not found in Bali. User shares an anecdote about a monitor lizard in their pool in Nusa Dua.
  • Recommended beaches for swimming in Bali: Mengiat and Geger (Nusa Dua). Melasti is popular for beach clubs but often has strong currents and large waves, making swimming difficult.
  • Speed boats to the Gili Islands operate from Padang Bai harbor in Bali. While Sanur is also a departure point, Padang Bai is often considered a convenient and cost-effective option. Travel time and cost can vary depending on the chosen operator and sea conditions.
  • For dolphin watching tours in Lovina, depart around 6:40 AM. Guides approach tourists at the beach. The cost is approximately IDR 150,000 per person. Dolphin sightings can vary daily.
  • Bali tidal charts are essential for coastal activities, especially in the Bukit peninsula area. Low tides (typically 1.0m - 1.2m) expose sharp reefs, making swimming dangerous or impossible at spots like Padang-Padang or Bingin. Users should check local tide schedules daily and correlate them with their planned beach time.
  • For a night ascent of Mount Batur, it is highly recommended to book accommodation in the immediate vicinity of the volcano (e.g., around Kintamani) for the night prior to the climb. This proximity significantly reduces pre-dawn travel time from other parts of Bali, allowing for better rest and a more convenient start to the hike. Various accommodation options are available in the area through booking platforms.
  • Mount Bromo is generally accessible for children aged 8+ (jeep access + ~45 min hike + stairs). Mount Ijen is not recommended for children due to the strenuous night ascent, toxic sulfur gas risks, and steep terrain.
  • Lempuyang Temple ('Gates of Heaven') is highly congested with tourist queues. An alternative viewpoint is Lahangan Sweet, located nearby. It offers panoramic views of Mount Agung with significantly fewer crowds and shorter wait times.
  • New arrivals in Ubud are looking for recommendations on local attractions and activities. They find walking difficult due to a lack of sidewalks and prefer personal, non-bot-generated advice.
  • A local fire dance show is available at Uluwatu, near Melasti beach. For specific timings and ticket information, it's recommended to inquire locally or check with local tourist information services.
  • Recommended beaches for swimming on Bali due to calm waters and infrastructure: Mengiat and Geger (Nusa Dua). Safety and water conditions depend on tide schedules rather than seasonal wave patterns.
  • Nusa Dua beaches can experience seasonal debris and seaweed due to ocean currents. Geger and Mengiat beaches are often cleaner; hotels clear their immediate frontage, but conditions vary by day.
