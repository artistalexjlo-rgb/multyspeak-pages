Documents & Visa in Indonesia: real experience from chats

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Indonesia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Indonesia enforces a strict 6-month passport validity rule for entry. Passengers with less than 6 months remaining are denied boarding at the point of origin or refused entry at the Indonesian border. Online visa portals will reject passport data if the expiration date is within this 6-month window.
  • The tourist tax in Bali is currently voluntary and is not actively checked at hotels or upon departure. A QR code is generated upon payment, but it is not a mandatory requirement for entry or accommodation.
  • Discussion regarding the processing time for e-visas to Bali, with some users reporting near-instantaneous approval while others mention it can take up to 48 hours. It's also noted that VOA can be processed quickly, but some users have received it instantly.
  • Passport validity must be at least 6 months at the time of entry into Indonesia. For international transit, especially with separate tickets, travelers must ensure the airline's policy allows boarding, as airlines are strictly liable for transporting passengers with insufficient passport validity for their final destination.
  • Electronic visa processing issues in Indonesia are often resolved by using mobile data instead of Wi-Fi for file uploads. Passport images require high contrast and clarity for automated systems to accept them. Existing valid visas prevent the issuance of a new one of the same type while remaining in the country. Post-COVID regulations mandate an in-person visit to an immigration office for biometrics (photo/fingerprints) even for online-initiated visa extensions.
  • Indonesian immigration often requires proof of onward travel (an exit flight) upon entry. Failure to present this can lead to being denied boarding or denied entry. If plans are flexible, purchase a refundable exit ticket or use services that provide temporary flight reservations. Check the airline requirements before departure.
  • Visa on Arrival (VoA) in Bali costs 500,000 IDR. If paying in USD cash (approx. $35), avoid paying with large bills because the change is often given in IDR at a highly unfavorable exchange rate. Applying for an e-VoA online before arrival is recommended to use the fast-track autogates (available for biometric passports, though families with small children must still use manual desks).
  • If experiencing issues with uploading a passport for a B1 visa application in Indonesia, try different VPNs or contact support. If the online application fails, visas can be obtained and paid for in USD or EUR at the airport. For visa renewals, check the immigration website for eligibility and start the process up to 14 days before expiry. Some online renewals may still require an in-person visit for photo capture.
  • If you encounter errors when applying for an E-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) for Indonesia, try using a VPN with a US server. Ensure your VPN is configured correctly, and if issues persist, consult visa-related chat groups for troubleshooting tips.
  • An onward or return ticket is a legal requirement for entry into Indonesia. While some travelers successfully bypass checks via automated e-gates, airlines frequently deny boarding at departure points without proof of onward travel. Furthermore, immigration officers regularly conduct manual checks, especially for families. Travelers should possess a valid onward flight ticket or a verifiable booking to avoid entry denial.
  • Extending a tourist visa (VO1/e-VOA) in Bali often requires proof of a return or onward flight ticket. It is advisable to have a confirmed return flight; some travelers book refundable flights to neighboring countries (e.g., Kuala Lumpur) as a valid proof for the immigration office.
  • For a family KITAS (spouse is an Indonesian citizen), the process often involves obtaining a SKTT (Surat Keterangan Tempat Tinggal) based on the spouse's KTP (ID card) registration area. If the spouse's KTP is registered in Jakarta, the initial registration may need to happen there, although subsequent residency can be moved to Bali. It is recommended to consult official Dukcapil (Civil Registry) websites or legal professionals for the latest specific document requirements for spouse-sponsored visas.
  • The official allowance for alcohol import into Indonesia is strictly limited to 1 liter per person. Customs officers selectively check baggage, and advanced scanners often detect concealed liquids. If excess alcohol is found, it is confiscated or destroyed. Local or imported wine of decent quality can be purchased on Bali for approximately 150k to 200k IDR per bottle.
  • For entry into Bali, travelers need an e-visa and a completed Customs Declaration (ECD). While immigration officers may not always ask for an outbound ticket, airlines often require proof of return or onward travel upon check-in. If caught without one, travelers sometimes book last-minute onward tickets to neighboring countries (e.g., Malaysia) via platforms like trip.com or use 'onward ticket' services.
  • For extending tourist visas (like B1 or C1) in Bali, applications usually require a passport and supporting documents. If applying through an agency, payments can sometimes be made via Russian cards (SBP/T-Bank) depending on the agency's local payment gateway. Using biometric passports allows entry via e-gates, skipping manual immigration queues. Always carry small cash (IDR) for VOA fees at the airport.
  • Immigration authorities in Indonesia may conduct 'administrative checks' at the airport during departure. Even with valid visas (e.g., 33g), previous visa history or sponsor company issues can trigger entry/exit restrictions. It is advised to verify visa status with professional agents before travel and ensure all past visa closures were handled correctly.
  • As of May 2026, the Bali tourist tax is voluntary. It is not currently enforced or checked upon departure, despite some AI models providing outdated or incorrect information suggesting it's mandatory. Travelers are not required to pay this fee to leave Indonesia.
  • When traveling to Bali, an outbound ticket to any third country (e.g., Thailand, Singapore) is generally sufficient. There are no known recent rule changes or official requirements mandating a return ticket specifically to the traveler's country of origin (e.g., Russia).
  • Online visa extensions for Indonesian e-VoA (C1) cannot be requested earlier than 14 days before expiration. Users must avoid delaying the request until the final 1-2 days, as the immigration portal frequently experiences synchronization glitches, such as missing 'extend' options or processing errors where payment is deducted but status remains unchanged. Overstaying incurs a fine of 1,000,000 IDR per day. If online payment status fails to update, visit the nearest Immigration Office (Kantor Imigrasi) immediately.
  • Indonesian immigration authorities may request a return or onward flight ticket upon entry and during visa extension processes. Digital booking screenshots are often insufficient; officials may demand real, verifiable bookings or instruct the applicant to provide a valid ticket via email within a short timeframe to complete the extension.
  • Visa extension in Indonesia requires proof of onward travel. Immigration officers may perform manual checks. Using 'fake' or temporary booking services is risky and often leads to entry refusal or forced purchase of a real ticket at the border. Always use refundable tickets or legitimate booking services if unsure about travel dates.
  • Visa overstay in Indonesia is fined at 1,000,000 IDR per day. For short overstays (e.g., 1 day), it is often processed at the airport before departure. For longer periods, official extension at the immigration office is required. Be aware of lengthy wait times (up to 6 hours) at immigration offices.
  • Visa extension process in Indonesia (VOA/e-VOA) requires mandatory biometric photo capture at the immigration office within a few days after payment. Failure to appear results in an overstay, charged at 1,000,000 IDR per day. Absence of an email notification from immigration is not a valid excuse.
  • Visa processing (C1/E-visa) in Indonesia is currently experiencing delays due to internal immigration administrative changes. Standard processing times may extend to 10-15+ days. Avoid agents promising same-day or 24-hour service, as these are currently unreliable. Always verify status through the official government e-visa portal.
  • For extending Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA) in Indonesia, the online process requires application and payment (approx. 500,000 IDR) online, followed by a mandatory personal visit to the immigration office (e.g., in Denpasar) within 3 working days for biometrics (photo and fingerprints). Avoid agents claiming 100% remote extension without biometrics.

  • When filling out the electronic arrival card for Indonesia, it is sufficient to list only one address of stay, even if planning to stay at multiple hotels during the visit.
  • Travelers transiting through Singapore on their way to Indonesia generally do not require a separate Singaporean visa if they meet the transit requirements (typically 96-hour transit visa exemption). It is advised to have a printed onward ticket to the final destination and to verify visa requirements with the airline during check-in at the departure airport.
