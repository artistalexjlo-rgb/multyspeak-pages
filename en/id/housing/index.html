Housing in Indonesia: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Housing» in Indonesia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Short-term villa rental scams in Bali are frequent. Red flags include: no formal contract, request for P2P/cash payments, and significantly lower prices (20-40% off) compared to market rates. Use reputable aggregators like Booking.com or Airbnb for initial bookings to ensure financial protection. Verify local property management company credentials before making direct bank transfers.
  • Recommended resources for finding long-term accommodation in Bali: Booking.com, Agoda, Trip.com (for initial short-term stay), Facebook Marketplace, and Rumah123. For larger groups (e.g., 8 people), it is safer to book a hotel first and search for villas locally to avoid scams and ensure the property matches expectations.
  • Rental villas in Bali often feature basic gas stoves with limited heat control, typically only high-intensity settings. Travelers planning to cook are advised to manage expectations regarding temperature control or use larger cookware to distribute heat.
  • The most effective and widely available local mosquito repellent in Bali is 'Soffel'. Travelers recommend the cream version over the spray for longer-lasting protection. For treating rooms indoors, 'Baygon' spray is effective but requires subsequent ventilation to keep children and pets safe.
  • Tipping standards in Bali: Avoid very small denominations (2k-5k IDR). Standard tips in restaurants are appreciated at 10-20k+ IDR, even if a service charge is included. For hotel staff (cleaning teams), 20k+ IDR is a more appropriate gesture than nominal amounts.
  • Rental fraud is prevalent in Bali's informal market. Essential safety protocols: avoid all advance payments before physical inspection, verify ownership documentation, sign contracts locally, and prioritize well-known platforms (e.g., Booking.com) over direct private transfers in messaging apps.
  • A wide children's bunk bed (120x200 cm) is available. It is made of comfortable and spacious wood, features storage drawers, and comes with lacquered finish and included mattresses. The original price and dimensions can be provided.
  • Bali is undergoing a transition in waste management regulations. Businesses (hotels, restaurants, cafes) are now required to manage and sort waste on-site. Unauthorized disposal or failure to comply with local height and zoning regulations for construction projects can result in immediate work stoppage and potential loss of permits.
  • The Indonesian government is implementing a star-rating system for villas and cracking down on unlicensed short-term rental properties. Approximately 1,600 non-compliant properties have been identified for potential removal from major booking platforms. Owners are being encouraged to formalize their business status to ensure market transparency.
  • Accommodation recommendations in Nusa Dua, Bali: Top-tier beachfront options include Mulia, St. Regis, Ayodya, Merusaka, Hilton, Kempinski, Westin, and Laguna. For second-line value options with beach access and shuttle services, consider Novotel Nusa Dua and Courtyard Nusa Dua. Nusa Dua is known for quiet evenings with most venues closing around 23:00.
  • To store clothes in vacuum bags in highly humid environments like Bali, clothes must be thoroughly washed and completely dried (preferably machine-dried). Always insert silica gel desiccant packs inside the vacuum bag to absorb remaining moisture and prevent mold.
  • To optimize accommodation costs on Bali, avoid long-term booking via Booking.com. Use Agoda for better pricing, or Facebook 'bali rent' groups for direct deals. For groceries, download the Grab app and check the 'Mart' section to compare local prices of meat and cheese versus Moscow equivalents; local produce is generally cheaper and better quality.
  • For a 14-day stay in Bali, budget accommodation can be found in guesthouses. Estimated food costs for two adults for 14 days can range from 2.8 million IDR (approximately 100,000 IDR per person per day) if eating local food, or higher if opting for European cuisine. Transportation costs vary, but scooter rental is generally affordable.
  • Arriving at night and planning to find accommodation upon arrival is possible, but be prepared for potential questions from immigration. Having at least a refundable first-night booking is advisable. Avoid suspicious offers for cheap accommodation.
  • Hotels in Indonesia often have age restrictions for solo travelers under 18. Parents can request a 'Guarantor Letter' or 'Parental Consent Form' from the specific property to potentially bypass restrictions, though individual hotel policies vary.
  • When searching for accommodation on Facebook (e.g., in Bali), if direct contact information (phone number) is not provided in the listing description, the primary method of contact is via Facebook Messenger. Response times can vary. Caution is advised regarding requests for deposits or prepayments without clear contact information or prior viewing, as this may indicate a scam. Relying on the platform's messaging system is often the only option.
  • The electrical outlets in Bali are typically Type C and Type F, with a voltage of 230V and a frequency of 50Hz. Russian plugs (Type C) should generally work without an adapter. However, it is always advisable to check your specific plug type.
  • When visiting Bali, consider exploring different areas. For beautiful beaches, start or end on the Bukit Peninsula (Nusa Dua). Allocate most of your time to Ubud for its nature and atmosphere. Canggu is recommended for a lively scene, while the northern parts of the island offer tranquility and fewer tourists. Assistance with accommodation and itineraries is available.
  • Electricity in Bali is prepaid via the PLN token system (token listrik). Tokens can be purchased at any minimart (Alfamart, Indomaret) or via banking apps using the meter's serial number. The rate is typically 2,500 to 3,000 IDR per kWh. Average monthly costs range from 600k–800k IDR for a guesthouse room, 1M IDR for a small house, to 3M–4M IDR for a luxury villa with continuous AC and pool pumps.
  • Travelers in Bali often use informal methods to detect staff access to their rooms, such as placing items in specific spots. While theft is not the norm, standard security practices like using hotel safes for valuables and documents remain essential.
  • Job offer: Assistant needed for one day for private property tasks including garage cleaning and pushing a car into the garage. Payment is 6000 rubles plus a bonus of up to 1200 rubles for lunch.
  • When choosing accommodation on Nusa Penida, it's recommended to stay in an area with at least a couple of restaurants or cafes within walking distance. Since Nusa Penida is relatively small, from any location, it's possible to arrange transportation with a driver to visit the main attractions. Consider your budget and preferred hotel type.
  • For a night ascent of Mount Batur, it is highly recommended to book accommodation in the immediate vicinity of the volcano (e.g., around Kintamani) for the night prior to the climb. This proximity significantly reduces pre-dawn travel time from other parts of Bali, allowing for better rest and a more convenient start to the hike. Various accommodation options are available in the area through booking platforms.
  • Indonesian immigration actively monitors local Telegram channels and social media. Posting public ads to sublet rooms, find roommates to split rent, or rent out personal bikes without a proper work visa (KITAS) is considered illegal commercial activity. This can lead to heavy fines, detention, and deportation. Use private channels for such arrangements or have the official property owner handle the listing.
  • Under Indonesian law, only Indonesian citizens are legally allowed to work as real estate brokers. Foreigners acting as intermediaries for property sales are operating in a legal gray area or illegally. AREBI (Indonesian Real Estate Broker Association) is actively pushing for stricter controls and blocking of platforms facilitating non-resident foreign property sales.
  • Avoid remote prepayment for long-term villa rentals in Indonesia. High-risk practices include bank transfers to unverified individuals. Recommended workflow: book temporary accommodation for arrival, conduct physical site visits to assess noise levels (construction) and actual property conditions, and verify property ownership through a local notary before signing any contracts or paying deposits.
  • Legal assistance is available for construction disputes in Bali. Lawyers can help analyze situations, assess risks, and guide clients through negotiations or legal actions. For consultations, contact TG/WA: 62 817 9677-771.
  • Recommended payment strategies for booking accommodation in Indonesia: 1. Prioritize booking options with 'Pay at property'. 2. Avoid direct online prepayments using Russian-issued cards due to high transaction failure rates. 3. Use reputable exchange services listed in official chat pins, acknowledging potential delays in response time during night hours (WITA time).
  • A booking for Handara Golf & Resort Bali for one night, June 2nd to June 3rd, is available for resale at 800,000 IDR. The original price was 1,000,000 IDR. The booking includes breakfast and a room for two. Confirmation documents can be provided.
  • Legal assistance is available for construction dispute issues in Bali, including broken deadlines, altered terms, documentation problems, and lack of response after payment. Lawyers can analyze the situation, assess risks, and guide through negotiations or legal actions. Contact information: TG & WA: 62 817 9677-771.
