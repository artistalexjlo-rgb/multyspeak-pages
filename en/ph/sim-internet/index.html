SIM & internet in Philippines: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «SIM & internet» in Philippines — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • To purchase and activate a Smart SIM card in Manila (e.g., at a Smart Store in Rockwell, Makati City), the cost is approximately 55 PHP for a card with 10GB for 3 days. Activation requires submitting a passport photo, proof of address (hotel booking is recommended over Google Maps screenshots), and a copy of your return ticket. If the activation website fails to load, connecting to a hidden, unsecured Wi-Fi network named "iphonedemo" (sometimes available on store demo phones) can resolve the issue. Tariff changes are not typically handled in-store but can be done via 7-Eleven or the Smart app.
  • Travelers can purchase eSIM profiles for the Philippines on Trip.com using Russian bank cards. Searching for 'esim philippines' on the platform yields various options, such as a 10-day package with 1 GB of daily high-speed data for approximately 450 RUB.
  • Kaspersky eSIM service offers data plans for travelers visiting the Philippines. A package of 20 GB valid for 30 days is available for approximately 1,370 RUB, providing stable 4G/5G mobile data connectivity.
  • For reliable internet on Boracay, especially for work calls, consider hotels as they often offer faster Wi-Fi. However, the signal strength may be limited to the immediate vicinity of the room. If stable and quiet internet is crucial, explore co-working spaces or cafes known for good connectivity.
  • Avoid purchasing overpriced tourist SIM cards sold at Philippine airports, which are often bundled with unnecessary services and cost between 2500 to 4000 PHP. Standard local rates are much cheaper: a local Smart SIM card with 70 GB of 5G data costs around 1050 PHP. Basic data packages can be bought for 100 PHP (25 GB) with top-ups of 25 GB costing 199 PHP. Alternatively, travelers can purchase affordable eSIMs via platforms like Trip.com beforehand.
  • Skillborn is preparing for release on Google Play and requires beta testers. 12 players are needed to install the app and log in occasionally for 14 days. Testers will receive a bonus, and their progress will be linked to their account. To become a tester, join the tester group, accept the invitation, install Skillborn from Google Play, and link the account by sending '/link' to the developer for a code.
  • For a one-month stay in the Philippines, a Smart SIM card is recommended. Specifically, a 5G plan for 28 days priced around 800 PHP, often with unlimited data, is a viable option.
  • On May 29, 2026, the exchange rate in Manila airport was 61.15 PHP per USD. One old USD banknote was rejected. A Globe SIM card was purchased at the airport. Network performance for Globe SIM varies between iPhone (supporting 5G) and Android (potentially limited to 4G) devices in southern Cebu.
  • For mobile connectivity in the Philippines, it is recommended to purchase a local SIM card upon arrival, ideally at the airport. The SIM card itself is inexpensive (around 60-70 PHP), but requires purchasing data packages separately. Popular providers are Globe and Smart. Packages typically vary in duration (1, 3, 5, 14, or 30 days) and offer sufficient data for most users, excluding heavy streaming. A common price for a SIM with a package for a two-week stay is around 1000 PHP.
  • Comparisons between internet and infrastructure in the Philippines and Vietnam often favor Vietnam. Internet speeds and ping times are generally reported to be worse in the Philippines, especially outside major cities. Power outages and unstable mobile internet are more common in the Philippines. Vietnam, particularly with providers like Vietttela, offers better value with high daily data allowances at low costs, and more stable international connectivity, despite some potential website blocking. Overall, the level of comfort and infrastructure in Vietnam is often perceived as higher than in the Philippines, with comparable amenities costing more in the latter.
  • Burgos Street in Makati, Manila, is known for its nightlife and adult entertainment, often involving transgender individuals. Travelers seeking a different atmosphere might prefer areas closer to Power Plant Mall. Regarding Smart SIM card activation, purchasing a higher-tier plan and paying for several months in advance may result in a smoother, more assisted activation process directly by store staff, potentially bypassing some complexities of self-activation.
  • For better connectivity and 5G access in the Philippines, consider purchasing a Smart SIM card instead of Globe. Ensure you opt for a 5G-compatible SIM.
  • Mobile connectivity in the Philippines is provided by two main carriers: Globe and Smart. Coverage varies significantly by location and island. For reliable service, it is recommended to carry SIM cards for both providers. Physical SIM cards are widely available for purchase throughout the country for those who do not use or support eSIM technology.
  • Registering a local SIM card in the Philippines can be problematic. When filling out the registration form, use your arrival point in the Philippines as the registration address, not your current location. This may resolve registration errors.
  • For reliable internet in the Philippines, purchase a local SIM from 'Smart'. They offer long-term postpaid unlimited data plans (starting at 3 months) for approximately 2,500 PHP/month. Note: These plans require a physical visit to a Smart office to terminate the subscription.
  • For convenient and affordable e-SIM options in the Philippines, Trip.com is recommended. Users report success with e-SIMs purchased there, some of which are multi-country compatible (e.g., China and Philippines).
  • Mobile internet costs vary significantly in the Philippines. Globe offers plans around 8GB/week for approximately 400 PHP/month, which some consider expensive. Smart has a 99 PHP/2-week plan. For reliable remote work, users may opt for premium, unlimited 5G plans, though 5G coverage is not universal and speeds can vary, sometimes being worse than Wi-Fi in non-touristy areas. Prices for premium plans are not explicitly stated but are understood to be higher than basic options.
  • To check your mobile number on a Smart SIM in the Philippines: 1. Send SMS with text 'BAL' to 214. 2. Use the 'Smart App'. 3. On iPhone: Settings -> Phone -> 'My Number'. 4. On Android: Settings -> About Phone -> Status/SIM Status.
