Trip preparation: how it works in Philippines — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Trip preparation» in Philippines — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Bohol is a viable alternative to Cebu for a beach chill vacation, offering similar experiences and activities. Bohol has beaches and an adventure park near the Chocolate Hills with ziplining and buggy rides (though these can be expensive). It is easy to combine trips to both Cebu and Bohol within a two-week period. In early August, the weather is generally favorable. Approximate costs for activities: Kawasan Falls - 600 pesos, buggy rides - around 4500 pesos.
  • The Philippines offers diverse travel experiences, from bustling cities like Davao to beach destinations like Boracay and Palawan. While potentially more expensive and less comfortable than some neighboring countries like Vietnam (which is often cited for better English proficiency and visa run ease), the Philippines provides a wide array of landscapes and activities. Travelers can customize itineraries by exploring different islands and beaches, catering to various preferences from active exploration to relaxation. For those interested in business or networking, Southeast Asia has online communities for connecting with entrepreneurs and professionals.
  • Travel costs in Palawan, Philippines, can be significantly higher than in other regions. Accommodation prices, particularly in popular areas like Boracay and El Nido, can be 3-5 times more expensive. Transportation costs are also substantial; for instance, the journey from Puerto Princesa to Sabang is about 75 km, and from Sabang to El Nido is approximately 400 km. Diving activities can be up to 2.5 times pricier, and food expenses may range from 20-50% higher. Sabang beach might offer fewer crowds, while El Nido can be very crowded. Southern Palawan is noted as a more remote and less developed region.
  • Contrary to some opinions, Boracay offers a wide range of activities beyond simple beach walks and sunsets. Travelers have reported needing more than 7 days to experience all the island has to offer, including sea activities, excursions, visiting various beaches, dining at cafes, exploring the area, attending discotheques, engaging in social interactions, and seeking out adventures.
  • For a two-week trip to the Philippines, a combination of Boracay and Panglao is recommended for first-time visitors. Boracay offers excellent beaches, a long coastline, and diverse dining options, suitable for a week-long stay. Panglao and Bohol also feature beautiful beaches and attractions like the Chocolate Hills, butterfly museums, and tarsiers. July is generally not peak season due to potential rain and storms, with September and October posing higher weather risks. For diving enthusiasts, consider Donsol, Subic Bay, Puerto Galera, Palawan, Malapascua, and Panglao. El Nido is beautiful but can be crowded and expensive for a first visit. Direct flights from Hong Kong may not be available; connecting flights are more likely. When choosing between Manila and Cebu for island hopping, consider Palawan is closer to Manila, while Bohol is closer to Cebu. Consider Siargao for beginner surfing, with options for excursions to nearby islands. Expect varied prices across islands; for example, beachfront hotels in Boracay might cost around $46, while similar accommodation in Palawan or El Nido could be around $180. For a shorter trip, consider combining Palawan and El Nido. For diving, Napaling reef and Balicasag are options in Panglao, with sardines typically found at Panagsama.
  • For parents traveling with young children (ages 5, 8, and 10), there's a conscious effort to balance experiencing local life and culture with safety. While understanding different lifestyles is important for children, nighttime excursions are avoided due to safety concerns. Daytime exploration with explanations is preferred.
  • November in the Philippines is generally considered the transition from shoulder season to peak season. While some travelers experience continuous heavy rains and storms, particularly in late October and early November on islands like Boracay and Palawan, others report excellent weather with no rain during the same period (e.g., from mid-November). Tropical storm season peaks in September and October. Heavy rain can significantly impact beach activities and walks, but less so for diving.
  • In July, Manila can be hot and humid. Palawan experiences daily but short-lived rain showers, which generally do not interfere with tourist activities like swimming and sunbathing.
  • Traveling to Cebu via Hong Kong is a viable option. Hong Kong can be an enjoyable stopover destination, with attractions like Victoria Peak, the Avenue of Stars, and the Mid-Levels escalator. While accommodation in Hong Kong can be expensive, the city has an efficient MTR system for transportation. Further details regarding Cebu itself can be found in other messages within the chat.
  • Weather forecasts in the Philippines often indicate rain, but actual rainfall can be less frequent or severe than predicted. While typhoons bring strong winds and heavy rain, generalized 'rainy season' forecasts may not reflect daily conditions. Locations like Siquijor and Moalboal often experience good weather despite rain predictions.
  • To legally relocate a pet to the Philippines, several steps are required: obtaining an Import permit, ensuring all vaccinations are up to date according to local regulations, selecting and booking appropriate flights, and arranging online support until arrival. These procedures can be complex, and expert assistance is available.
  • When hiring a private driver on Cebu for routes like Moalboal-Oslob-Kawasan, expect prices to vary. For an 8-hour trip covering Moalboal, Kawasan Falls, and Oslob, reported costs range from 3000-4000 PHP per car, not per person. For a trip including Oslob, Kawasan, Tumalog (excluding Osmena Peak), a van for several people cost around 1700 PHP per person. Bargaining is common. Note that some reports mentioned stable gas prices, while others claimed an increase, so current fuel prices might be a negotiation factor.
  • Asia's weather, particularly in the Philippines, can be unpredictable. While peak season may offer sunny weather, it often comes with higher prices and intense heat. Traveling during shoulder seasons or off-peak might offer a balance of weather and cost, but be aware of potential weather disruptions like typhoons or increased seismic activity. The Philippines has diverse climate zones, so local weather can vary significantly.
  • The Cebu-Moalboal-Oslob route is best managed independently using public buses. Key tips: arrive at the whale shark spot in Oslob by 06:00-07:00 to avoid crowds. Canyoneering in Moalboal requires basic swimming skills and physical activity. Local food specialty is 'Lechon'.
  • For first-time visitors to the Philippines planning a month-long trip in October and aiming to visit multiple islands, consider destinations like Bohol and Cebu, often combined for diverse experiences. Boracay is also a popular choice, known for its beaches. October is generally considered the end of the wet season or shoulder season, so weather can be a factor. Prioritize islands known for established tourism infrastructure and activities suitable for varying weather conditions.
  • A challenging day trip itinerary from Lapu-Lapu includes Oslob (for whale sharks, best seen before 9 AM), Moalboal, and waterfalls. To maximize this trip, arrive in Cebu the day before, stay overnight, and start by 3 AM. Swimming with sardines is also a recommended activity. Recommended waterfalls include Tumalog and Mantayupan; Kawasan may be less impressive. This is an exhausting itinerary, best spread over more than one day if time permits.
  • A budget of $3500 for one month in the Philippines (excluding accommodation) is considered sufficient for a comfortable mid-range trip. Costs for food, local transfers, and recreational activities (like diving) can be kept within $1500–$2000 for two weeks, depending on the level of activity. Prices for food and hotels on Palawan can be significantly higher (up to 3x) compared to other tourist hubs like Boracay.
  • August is generally part of the wet season in Boracay, Philippines. While it's possible to have sunny days, expect a higher chance of rain, potential typhoons, and rougher seas. The weather can be unpredictable, and it's advisable to check forecasts closer to the travel date. Some travelers find it less crowded during this period.
  • Manila offers several attractions for a multi-day visit, including exploring Intramuros and Fort Santiago, visiting historical churches and basilicas, and experiencing local transport like jeepneys. A 3-day itinerary can be comfortably filled with these activities.
  • When planning a trip to the Philippines, if starting in Cebu and intending to visit Bohol, consider allocating more time to Cebu and its surroundings like Moalboal. While El Nido and Palawan are highly recommended, they often require dedicated time. Prioritizing nearby islands can optimize the itinerary for travelers with limited time.
  • For an itinerary covering Cebu, Boracay, and Bohol before flying to China, careful sequence planning is advised. For inter-island flights, Trip.com is a suggested platform, known for potentially accepting Russian payment cards. Regarding currency, it is generally better to use local currency (PHP), but USD can be useful for exchange.
  • For a 7-10 day trip to the Philippines, Boracay is recommended for first-timers focusing on beach relaxation, offering activities like snorkeling near Crocodile Island, markets, massages, and other beaches. For a more active trip with diverse nature, start with Cebu for shark swimming, mountain views, turtle and sardine encounters, canyoneering, and waterfalls. From Cebu, travelers can then fly to Palawan for scenic views and water activities, or return to Boracay for more beach time. Some suggest a minimum of two weeks for a Philippine trip due to travel time and acclimatization.
  • The Philippines has four distinct climate zones. Rainfall is localized, and it is common for one island to experience rain while a neighboring island remains sunny. Weather is unpredictable; August-October is the high-risk season for typhoons which can cause continuous rain for extended periods.
  • For a first-time 2-week trip to the Philippines, arriving in Cebu and planning to visit Bohol, it's often suggested to visit Bohol first due to ferry logistics. For a 5-day stay on Bohol/Panglao, travelers seek advice on best areas to stay, essential places to visit, and recommendations for specific beaches and hotels on Panglao.
  • In Port Barton, Philippines, travelers often seek companions for activities such as sea trips to observe turtles. Platforms like the 'Friends — TravelAsk' channel are useful resources for finding other travelers to join group activities, potentially optimizing costs and enhancing the travel experience.
  • On Boracay, algae have been observed in February. In November, visitors reported clear waters and good weather, though conditions can vary year by year. It is generally assumed that October also has no algae, but the weather is unpredictable. January conditions regarding algae are not definitively stated, but there's an open question about their potential presence.
  • Sending a 15-year-old solo to Cebu for language school requires careful consideration of her independence, navigational skills, and the safety of the destination. While some airlines offer unaccompanied minor services, verify age restrictions and procedures. The Philippines can be challenging for first-time solo travelers due to language barriers and potentially complex transportation systems. Researching school-provided airport reception, pre-arranged transport, and familiarizing the student with emergency protocols is crucial. Consider the potential for stress and the student's comfort level with navigating unfamiliar environments independently. Ensure the chosen school environment fosters English use among students from diverse backgrounds.
  • For first-time visitors to the Philippines, Bohol and Cebu are highly recommended, often combined. Boracay is also a favored destination. October is considered shoulder season, with potential for typhoons; late November is generally safer regarding weather. When traveling by ferry, expect potential delays or cancellations, especially on routes involving more exposed waters like Siquijor and Dumaguete. Ferries between Bohol and Cebu are generally more frequent (hourly departures) and less prone to severe weather disruptions compared to routes like Siquijor/Dumaguete via Tagbilaran to Cebu (which might only have 2 departures daily). Always allow significant buffer time (e.g., 24 hours) between ferry arrivals and flight departures.
  • For PADI diving certification in the Philippines, especially for non-English speakers, it's recommended to find a center with a Russian-speaking instructor (e.g., in Panglao). To optimize vacation time, complete the PADI theoretical course online before arrival. After instructor registration on the PADI website, candidates access the online course, complete 300 pages of material, multiple tests, quizzes, and a final exam, requiring a minimum 75% score. The on-site practical portion then typically takes only a couple of days, avoiding 3-4 days (approx. 5 hours per chapter) of theory during the trip.
  • For a short trip (e.g., 8 nights), especially during holidays like New Year, Boracay is often recommended over splitting time between Boracay and Panglao/Bohol. Boracay offers a long beach, diverse food, and activities like diving, sailing, snorkeling, and surfing, which can keep visitors engaged for a week. While Panglao (Bohol) provides inland attractions (hills, volcanoes, tarsiers, Loboc River cruise), travel between islands for a short duration can lead to fatigue. A single destination minimizes travel time and allows better adaptation to a new environment.
  • A couple's travel budget for two weeks in the Philippines, covering Manila, Puerto Princesa, El Nido, and Boracay, including tours (Underground River, El Nido Tour C, private sunset boat on Boracay) and private transfers, amounted to approximately 1700 USD without strict budget adherence. Scooter rental in El Nido was noted to be significantly cheaper than in Boracay.
  • For pet relocation to the Philippines, expert assistance is available covering: obtaining an Import Permit, vaccination recommendations, flight selection and booking, and online support until arrival. This service is designed to streamline the process of moving pets internationally.
  • Diving safety protocols in the Philippines: 1. Always use a personal dive computer; do not rely solely on the guide's timing. 2. AOWD certification is often required for sites with strong currents (e.g., Balicasag). 3. Always perform safety stops as dictated by the computer, regardless of guide instructions. 4. eLearning (PADI) can be completed prior to arrival to save time during the vacation.
  • Malapascua Island is known for its diving, particularly for thresher sharks. While some visitors find 3 days sufficient to explore the island by motorbike, others suggest longer stays are needed. Notable beaches include Paliton Beach and Salagdoong Beach. The experience on Malapascua can vary based on individual expectations.
  • For family-friendly destinations in the Philippines for children aged 8-13 in July, consider Boracay for its amenities or Cebu in a quieter area. Siquijor is recommended for a peaceful environment with a respectful local population and minimal street vendors, offering a tranquil atmosphere outside of weekends. Avoid large cities like Manila.
  • El Nido is considered one of the most beautiful but expensive destinations in the Philippines. Planning advice: allow at least one week for tours (A, B, C, D) and rest days. For less crowded beaches, visit Duli Beach in the north; Las Cabanas is more popular and crowded.
