Documents & Visa in Switzerland: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Switzerland — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Technical tips for accessing Swiss visa appointment portals: 1. Use a computer instead of a mobile device. 2. Switch from Wi-Fi to a mobile hotspot/cellular data connection. 3. If access is blocked, using a Russian VPN server may resolve the connection issue. Note that these sites are prone to session timeouts, crashes during form filling, and temporary account blocks due to excessive activity/refreshing in a short timeframe.
  • For urgent medical visas to Switzerland, having a direct contract and treatment confirmation from a Swiss clinic is essential for expedited processing. Standard appointments via visa centers may be too slow; direct communication with the consulate regarding medical urgency is advised. Beware of scammers offering 'expedited services' via private messages.
  • When applying for a Swiss visa, applicants often debate the necessity of additional documents like proof of real estate ownership or marriage certificates, with anecdotal evidence suggesting varied requirements. Some users report successful visa acquisition without extra documents, while others faced rejections despite seemingly complete applications. The visa application process, especially when relying on an invitation from friends, can be perceived as similar to applying for a tourist visa, with no guarantee of approval. Experiences with visa agents also vary, with some actively discouraging applications due to past client rejections, even with flawless documentation. Appealing rejections has also been mentioned as a difficult process. Documentation requirements can be complex, particularly for those with property in different cities but applying locally. Obtaining a visa for Switzerland can be challenging, with some users sharing specific case details, such as receiving a 12-day single-entry visa for a trip aligned with travel dates, even when sponsored by a spouse with Swiss residency.
  • Recent travel to Russia may impact Swiss visa applications due to sanctions. Several individuals have reported visa rejections for personal visits to Russia prior to applying for a Swiss visa. Official invitations or work-related visits to Russia appear to be less problematic. The situation implies a selective application of sanctions, where personal visits are scrutinized more than official or business-related ones. Travelers are advised to consider postponing non-essential trips to Russia if they intend to apply for a Swiss visa soon. Individuals with dual residency or strong ties to Russia may face particular challenges in navigating these regulations.
  • Invitations for Swiss visas can be prepared in English. For example, an invitation submitted in a single copy and exclusively in English was accepted.
  • Swiss tourist visa processing timeline in Moscow: standard application submitted on May 8, passports ready on May 20. Total duration: 12 calendar days. Result: 1-month duration visa issued for tourist purposes with hotel and flight bookings.
  • Renewing a Russian passport at the consulate in Bern currently faces significant slot shortages. A valid passport is required for travel. Renewing is significantly easier if the applicant possesses a valid internal Russian passport. If slots are unavailable, it is recommended to call the consulate to inquire about cancellations.
  • The visa tracking system on the official Swiss visa application website can be challenging due to CAPTCHA issues. Users should ensure they enter the CAPTCHA characters in all uppercase (capital) letters, as this is a common requirement for successful verification.
  • A German residence permit (Aufenthaltstitel) does not grant the right to work in Switzerland. To work in Switzerland as a non-Swiss citizen, one must secure a job offer from a Swiss employer who is willing to sponsor a work permit. Freelancing requires proof of multiple clients to avoid issues with social security legislation. Foreigners are prioritized after Swiss nationals and EU/EFTA citizens. Networking via LinkedIn and showcasing high-level specialized skills is essential. Obtaining EU citizenship first significantly reduces bureaucratic hurdles and long-term risk of residency permit revocation.
  • Swiss authorities require an original criminal record certificate with an apostille for family reunification visas. Electronic versions from platforms like Gosuslugi are not accepted. The estimated processing time is up to 30 days for the certificate and approximately 5 days for the apostille.
  • Switzerland does issue tourist visas, although the process may have become more stringent recently, with an increase in rejections noted since late last year. Common reasons for refusal include 'unproven purpose and intentions.' While obtaining a tourist visa is possible, applicants should be prepared for thorough documentation checks and potentially longer processing times. It's advisable to have strong evidence of your travel intentions and financial stability.
  • For a Swiss visa application based on an invitation, a printed PDF copy of the invitation letter is generally accepted. A successful applicant confirmed obtaining a visa with a PDF. It is crucial to verify the final text of the invitation against the official requirements on the Embassy of Switzerland's website, particularly regarding the invitee's passport number. Legally, an original document is not strictly required; the key is that the content of the letter aligns with the stated intentions.
  • In Switzerland, the right to family reunification for adult children (over 18 years old) is generally lost. This process is typically only possible if the adult child is financially dependent due to health reasons or is enrolled in an educational program. Previous residency in Switzerland as a minor does not automatically grant reunification rights as an adult. For specific cases, consultation with immigration lawyers is recommended.
  • Visa processing times for Switzerland can vary. In May, applications in Moscow were processed within 8 to 14 days from submission to collection. Be aware that processing times may fluctuate based on the volume of applications and the specific visa center. It is advisable to check the most current processing times with the relevant visa application center.
  • For Swiss guest visas for Russian applicants, a common trend is issuance for specific travel dates, even for regular visitors. However, close relatives (spouses, children, parents, grandchildren) with established travel history to Switzerland have a higher chance of obtaining a multi-entry visa for one year or longer. The invitation letter should explicitly request a multi-year visa, state that the inviting party covers all expenses, and possibly outline planned visits throughout the requested period. While example dates for the immediate trip should be included, the request for maximum duration should be clear.
  • For Swiss visa applications, hotel booking confirmations from standard travel aggregators may be considered insufficient. It is recommended to contact the hotel directly to request an official invoice or a confirmation letter with the hotel's stamp to satisfy consular document requirements.
  • For Swiss visa applications, appointment slots may not be consistently available for all months. Users can reschedule their appointments if a more favorable date appears. It is recommended to download and fill out the application form locally, as online forms might have formatting issues when printed. If encountering problems, contacting the relevant service with your details and a description of the issue can lead to a prompt resolution, sometimes within a few hours.
  • Swiss Schengen visa application requirements for private visits: 1. Both financial proof (savings balance and salary card history) may be collected despite official requirements stating one. 2. A travel itinerary (marshrutyi list) might be accepted instead of purchased flight tickets. 3. Courier delivery of the passport to different cities can be arranged directly at the visa center at the time of submission. 4. Proof of relationship (chat history/photos) is not strictly required for private visits.
  • When a medical visa to Switzerland is denied due to doubts about the applicant's intent to return to their home country (common for young males without strong ties like employment, business, or real estate), an appeal is recommended. The appeal should include the original document package plus additional proof of strong ties to the home country. Processing time for an appeal typically ranges from 2 weeks to 1 month, which may not be feasible for urgent travel. Despite significant financial proof (e.g., 8 million RUB and 120,000 CHF in paid bills), lack of established ties can be a primary refusal reason. Alternatives include requesting a supporting letter from a Swiss medical professional, contacting the EDA (Federal Department of Foreign Affairs) hotline, or seeking assistance from charitable foundations like 'Krug Dobra' (in Russia) or the Red Cross for accompaniment support. Some sources suggest that a visa refusal without an interview may be legally questionable.
  • For Swiss visa applications longer than 90 days (Visa D), the process is handled directly by the consulate, not VFS Global. Obtain necessary work permits from the Swiss employer first. Upon receiving confirmation of permits, contact the consulate for the Visa D application.
  • When traveling to Switzerland for the purpose of marriage, it is advisable to inquire about the necessity of obtaining insurance in advance. Consult relevant authorities or your travel insurance provider for specific requirements.
  • Schengen visa appeals for Switzerland are handled via the embassy or consulate that issued the refusal. Applicants should check the specific instructions on the relevant embassy's website. Appeals often require a formal letter and proof of intent to return. Note: success rates for appeals based on short-term invitations are low.
  • The VFS Global center for Swiss visa applications in Moscow is located at Letnikovskaya street, 10, building 5 (near Paveletskaya metro station).
  • When applying for a Swiss National D-Visa, if online booking systems only offer 'short stay' options, applicants should directly contact the Embassy's visa department via email (e.g., moscow.visa.admin.ch). Request an appointment for document submission, as long-stay visa applications are processed directly by the Embassy's visa section, not always through standard online booking forms.
  • Securing a Swiss guest visa appointment slot in Russia (e.g., St. Petersburg, Moscow) has become challenging, with slots often available only several months in advance. Users might get temporarily blocked for excessive calendar browsing. A strategy for obtaining a desired later slot is to book any earliest available slot and then reschedule it to a preferred future date. Rescheduling to an earlier date is not possible. For urgent early slots, continuous monitoring for cancellations is required.
  • Swiss visa slots are currently scarce. No specialized bots are widely available due to low demand. Applicants report that slots appear randomly, with night monitoring being the most effective strategy. Some applicants successfully secure slots by manually checking VFS Global/TLS sites, often catching cancellations. Documentation (especially hotel confirmations directly from hotels) is critical due to high scrutiny.
  • Passport control at Basel Airport (BSL) is currently experiencing long delays, often exceeding 2 hours, due to the implementation of the Entry/Exit System (EES). Non-EU and EU citizens are often in the same queue. Passengers arriving in Switzerland should follow signs for the 'Swiss' exit and take the bus line 50 to reach the city center. Border agents generally do not request proof of return flights or hotel bookings for every passenger.
  • When booking a visa appointment on the VFS Global website, if slots appear visible but are unselectable, it indicates they have been claimed by other users during the data entry process. The system is prone to lag; persistent checking at different hours is required.
  • To reschedule an existing visa appointment for Switzerland, navigate to the official visa application center website. Look for a specific section or link that states, 'If you have already made an appointment or wish to reschedule, click here.' This link will lead to the functionality allowing you to modify your existing appointment to a new available date and time without requiring you to find new slots from the general booking interface.
  • A financial guarantee for a Swiss visa or proof of sufficient funds for a stay can be demonstrated by presenting a printout of a statement from a Russian bank card.
  • For visa applications involving minors (under 18) in Switzerland, parental presence at the visa center is not always mandatory if a notarized consent form for travel/biometrics is provided. A notarized translation of this consent is typically required.
  • Securing an appointment slot for Swiss visa applications has become challenging. Slots are released sporadically and require frequent monitoring of the visa application center's website. While previously more accessible, current demand and reduced capacity have made obtaining appointments difficult. Be persistent in checking for availability and consider applying well in advance of your planned travel dates.
  • When applying for a Swiss visa, it is often more reliable to download the application form, fill it out locally on a computer, and print it, rather than relying on the online web form, which may be unstable.
  • When submitting bank statements for a visa application to Switzerland, an electronic bank statement with a digital signature, printed in color (or even black and white), from a Russian bank like T-Bank (Tinkoff) is generally accepted. Some visa centers might suggest a wet-sealed document is preferable; T-Bank can provide this via courier within 24 hours upon request. There is no requirement for translation or notarization of these statements. It is permissible to combine statements from multiple banks. Requirements for bank statements for visa applications have reportedly remained stable for approximately 15 years.
  • In Switzerland, extending an existing residence permit (VNM/B permit) is generally considered to be significantly easier and offers higher chances of success compared to applying for an initial permit or seeking family reunification. The legal framework often distinguishes between the right to extend an existing permit and the right to obtain a new one for the first time, favoring extensions.
  • In Swiss real estate, competition is high. While official listings are common, there is a secondary market where brokers (agents) use personal connections with 'regies' to prioritize candidate applications for a fee (usually one month's rent).
  • For Schengen visa applications, visa centers act as intermediaries and often request travel plans beyond the exit date from the Schengen zone. However, if return tickets to the country of origin are not yet available due to long-term booking windows, applicants can provide a cover letter explaining the schedule. Buying refundable tickets is a common 'fail-safe' strategy to satisfy bureaucratic document checklists.
