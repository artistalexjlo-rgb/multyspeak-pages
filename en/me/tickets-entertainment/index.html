Tickets & Entertainment: how it works in Montenegro — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Tickets & Entertainment» in Montenegro — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Podgorica is primarily an administrative center and lacks significant luxury shopping or high-end tourist infrastructure compared to coastal areas. Porto Montenegro in Tivat is considered the primary luxury destination in the country.
  • Access to viewpoints above Kotor is possible by car, with paid parking available at €1.30 per hour (cash payment). Free parking spots can also be found nearby. The road is narrow and serpentine. The cable car operates with a round-trip ticket costing €20 for adults.
  • There is a free hiking trail to the Kotor fortress. To find it, walk along Tabacina street, passing the Kamelija shopping center, until you reach the local water utility building; a trail behind it leads to the fortress.
  • Oyster farms in Boka Bay (near Perast/Risan) are best accessed via the Blue Line bus service from Tivat. Walking from Tivat to the farms is not feasible. The area between Perast and Risan is home to several oyster farms including the popular Milos farm.
  • A 3-hour boat tour in the Bay of Kotor, costing approximately 35 EUR per person, is highly recommended. The itinerary typically includes a visit to Our Lady of the Rocks (without disembarking), a submarine tunnel, Mamula Island, and the Blue Cave with swimming opportunities. This excursion is a popular alternative to other activities like the Kotor cable car.

Official shuttle services from Tivat, Kotor, and Budva can be booked via their website. In case of bad weather, operations may be suspended, leading to delays in evacuation/descent. For the best views of the bay, hike 800m from the 'Monte 1350' bar to the 'Best View of Kotor' lookout.

National Park entrance fee is 5 EUR per person. Payment is cash only. Traveling to Skadar Lake from Virpazar might be slightly cheaper.

This toboggan is a rail-based amusement ride that completes a circular route, descending and then ascending back to the starting point; disembarking mid-route is not possible. There are no established walking trails from the toboggan's upper station. Access to and from the top stations typically requires using the cable car or arranging a taxi/private vehicle due to steep slopes.
