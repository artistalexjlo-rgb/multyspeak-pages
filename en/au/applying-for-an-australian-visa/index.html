Applying for an Australian Visa: how it works in Australia — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Applying for an Australian Visa» in Australia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • For Australian visa applications, check if the reference number type can be changed within the application form, as there might be a dropdown menu for selection.
  • For Australian visa applications, provide a bank statement showing the account balance and transaction history for the last three months. The inclusion of a Russian internal passport number on bank statements is generally not required by Australian immigration. Consider writing a letter in English explaining your ties to your home country and reasons for visiting Australia.
  • For Australian visa applications, strong ties to your home country are crucial. Provide evidence such as rental agreements (even historical ones), property deeds, car ownership, stable employment, or business income. Including documentation of pets is also advisable, as it demonstrates commitment. It's better to over-document than under-document your ties to your home country.
  • For Australian visa applications, photos are not required with the initial submission as they will be taken during the biometric collection. Flight and hotel bookings are also generally not required for the application itself. Focus on providing essential documentation proving ties to your home country and financial stability.
  • For Australian visa applications, if you have a lost passport with previous Schengen visas, you can mention this in your cover letter and provide supporting evidence if available, such as photos from Europe. However, relying solely on old Schengen visas from a lost passport might not be a strong factor in your application, as visa officers may not be convinced by them. It's best to focus on current financial stability and the purpose of your trip to Australia. The application form has a section for 'Evidence of previous travels,' where you might be able to upload relevant documents or notes.
  • Previous immigration and travel history is considered in Australian visa applications. However, the weight given to this factor can vary, and outcomes are not always predictable. While extensive travel history can be beneficial, its absence does not guarantee refusal, and strong travel records do not ensure approval. Applicants should provide all required documentation and may consider a cover letter to explain their intentions.
  • For Australian visa applications, provide a 3-month transaction history statement for your primary bank account and a balance statement for any savings accounts. This demonstrates both regular income and available funds. While optional, including personal items like pet photos is not a requirement.
  • For Australian visa applications, it is not always necessary to have pre-booked hotels and flights. Providing a detailed travel itinerary, even without confirmed reservations, can be sufficient. Omit unnecessary documents such as pet photos unless specifically requested.
  • For Australian visa applications, translated documents are required. While professional translation is ideal, self-translation is often acceptable. If you translate a document yourself, ensure all text, including any on stamps or seals, is translated. It is recommended to indicate that the translation was done by AI and provide your contact details. Combine the translated document with the original. For bank statements, translations are required for the period specified (usually 3 months), and individual transaction translations are necessary.
  • Form 54 (Family Composition) is part of the Australian visa application process. It requires listing family members and can be completed and signed without a witness. Family photos are not required. Ensure all information provided is accurate.
  • For an Australian visa application as a working individual, required documents typically include: employment certificate (stating position, salary, duration), 2-NDFL certificate, bank statement (balance and 6-month transaction history), passport copy, and a daily itinerary. Pre-purchasing flights and hotel bookings is not necessary; approximate details in the itinerary suffice.
  • For freelance developers without a formal employment contract, earning income through an Individual Entrepreneur (IP) and receiving payments to a personal card, Australian visa applications require careful documentation. Standard proof of employment includes contracts and tax records. Alternative documentation may include GitLab activity screenshots and bank statements showing regular income. If formal employment cannot be substantiated, the applicant may be classified as unemployed. Consider submitting a detailed explanation in the 'Other' section of the application.
  • When applying for an Australian visa, documents confirming property ownership should be uploaded in the 'Financial Status' section. Look for 'право собственности' (property ownership) as a document type option.
  • After completing the Australian visa application form, applicants can review all details before uploading supporting documents. It is recommended to provide travel history for the last 10 years, and include significant earlier visas (e.g., US visas) if available, as this can strengthen the application.
  • When applying for an Australian visa as a family (e.g., two adults and a small child traveling together), it is acceptable to submit one common cover letter for the entire family application, as you are traveling as a unit.
  • A valid driver's license (IDL) is not a mandatory document to attach to a travel itinerary for an Australian visa application, even if the itinerary includes car rentals.
  • When applying for an Australian visa with a travel companion, you can indicate this in the 'accompanying person' section of the application form. Alternatively, you can mention it in a cover letter. Each individual must submit their own application and supporting documents.
  • For Australian visa applications, it's advisable to apply as early as possible. Visas are typically issued for one year, with processing times currently ranging from 1.5 to 2 months. Avoid purchasing flight tickets until the visa has been approved and issued.
  • All documents submitted for an Australian visa application must be translated into English. Machine translation tools like Google Translate or GPT can be used for this purpose.
  • For Australian visa applications, consideration begins from the date of payment, not biometrics submission. All submitted documents must be current, not older than one month at the time of payment. Biometrics are typically required to be submitted within two weeks following the payment.
  • Russian domestic passports are not required for Australian visa applications. Discrepancies in name transliteration between bank statements and passports can be resolved by editing the document PDF or by adding an explanation in the cover letter.
  • When assisting a senior friend (e.g., a 67-year-old widow with sufficient funds) in applying for an Australian visa to travel with you (who holds a 3-year visa and has an Australian citizen son): 1. If your friend's itinerary includes meeting your Australian citizen son, it is advisable to mention this in the visa application and cover letter. 2. Regarding indicating her own son as a sponsor: opinions vary. Some suggest it demonstrates strong ties to her home country, while others argue it's unnecessary if she has ample personal funds and could potentially complicate the application. 3. It is strongly recommended to state in the cover letter that she is traveling with you (a friend who has previously visited Australia). Always provide truthful information.
  • For Australian visa applications, demonstrate financial capacity by providing evidence of funds. While a transaction account may have a low balance, supplementary evidence from savings accounts or term deposits is acceptable. Regular income is important, and a revolving balance on a transaction account is generally understood.
  • Australian visa application sequence: 1. Submission of documents/forms, 2. Payment of fees, 3. Biometrics appointment invitation. Successful outcome is communicated via a 'Grant' notification email. Physical visa labels are not issued.
  • For Australian visa applications, ensure consistency in your name across all documents. While minor variations like 'Anastasiia' and 'Anastasia' might be acceptable, the name on your biometric appointment and application form must match your passport exactly. If discrepancies are found, it's crucial to address them before your biometric submission.
  • Required documents for an Australian visa application typically include: employment verification, 3-month bank statements (transaction history and balance), copies of old passports with visas, property deeds (e.g., EGRN extract), travel insurance, a travel itinerary, and a cover letter. Supporting documents like driver's licenses or extensive pet photos are generally not required unless specified.
  • When applying for an Australian visa from Russia, a Thai Digital Nomad Visa (DTV), even if electronic and not physically stamped in your passport, should not be included as supporting documentation to 'strengthen' your case. Australia may view such visas, intended for digital nomads, as indicative of a lack of strong ties to your home country, which can negatively impact your application. Focus on demonstrating clear connections to Russia instead.
  • After submitting your Australian visa application and attending the biometric appointment, it is crucial to click the 'I confirm' button at the bottom of the document list. This action, while not impacting the visa outcome or processing time, serves to confirm completion. If no additional requests for documents are received via email, assume your application is proceeding as normal. For biometric appointments, if the earliest available date seems unusually far in the future, it may indicate a system glitch. Try accessing the booking system later or from a different device, as immediate or next-day appointments are often available.
  • When applying for an Australian visa with multiple valid passports, scan and upload all passports that contain relevant visas or stamps. The electronic visa will be linked to one passport, but providing details of all valid passports is acceptable and may be required in the application form.
  • For Australian visa applications, provide a 3-month bank statement showing transaction history for your primary account. For a savings account, a statement of the balance is sufficient. Regular income is important, and small balances on a transaction account are acceptable if supported by other financial evidence.
  • If you have a low official salary but a significant amount of savings and your spouse has a good income and savings, it's recommended to choose the 'sponsorship' option for your Australian visa application. Submit both your financial documents and your spouse's supporting documents, along with a sponsorship letter. Indicate your relationship as 'de facto partner' if applicable.
