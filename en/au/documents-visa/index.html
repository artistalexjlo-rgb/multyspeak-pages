Documents & Visa: how it works in Australia — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Documents & Visa» in Australia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • After submitting an Australian visa application, the applicant has a 14-day window to book and attend a biometrics appointment. If it is impossible to schedule within this period, notify the Department of Home Affairs.
  • To access ImmiAccount without the mandatory authenticator app: look for the 'I need another option' link in the bottom-left corner of the MFA setup page. This allows users to opt for email-based verification instead of using Google Authenticator or similar apps.
  • After submitting your Australian visa application and paying the fee, you must provide biometrics within 14 days. You will receive a biometric collection referral letter via email or in your online account. Schedule an appointment on the VFS Global website and print the referral letter to avoid additional on-site fees.
  • If an incorrect date of birth is entered on an Australian visa application, contact the Department of Home Affairs. They may provide correction forms. If the error is not rectified, it might be necessary to withdraw the application and resubmit. It is crucial to address such errors promptly.
  • For Australian visa 600 applications: 1. Passport scans: Submit copies of the current passport and any previous passports containing visas. You do not need to scan every blank page; focus on pages with identity info and travel stamps. 2. Unused visas: It is recommended to include copies of visas even if they weren't used for entry, as they demonstrate travel history. 3. Group applications: When applying for family, create a group in the ImmiAccount and list family members as accompanying travelers.
  • When filling out the Australian visa application, if you are traveling with colleagues or individuals whose details were provided in sponsorship letters or other supporting documents, it is advisable to list them in the application form. This ensures consistency and can be particularly relevant if you share a common travel purpose, such as attending a marathon.
  • For Australian visa applications, provide a bank statement showing the account balance and transaction history for the last three months. The inclusion of a Russian internal passport number on bank statements is generally not required by Australian immigration. Consider writing a letter in English explaining your ties to your home country and reasons for visiting Australia.
  • When completing the Australian visa application form, if you are asked 'Did this applicant undergo a medical examination for an Australian visa in the last 12 months?' and you have not had such an examination, select 'No'.
  • For Australian visitor visa (subclass 600) financial evidence: excessive funds can trigger refusal by suggesting an intent to migrate. Recommended amount: enough to cover the trip duration (e.g., 600,000–700,000 RUB for a two-week couple trip). If a spouse has no independent income/transaction history, provide a sponsorship letter rather than forced account activity. Always declare previous visa refusals in the application to maintain transparency.
  • Australian student visa refusal based on GTE criteria. Key reason for denial: educational 'downgrade' (switching from medicine to trade). Officers require a clear, logical career progression path. A strong GTE statement must explicitly explain why the specific education cannot be pursued in the home country and how it aligns with the applicant's long-term career goals.
  • When applying for a family visa (e.g., parent and child), it is highly recommended to submit the adult's application first. The applicant will need the parent's application or Group ID to link the child's visa application, which ensures synchronized processing.
  • For Australian visitor visa applications, remote work is not an automatic ground for refusal if strong ties to the home country and an intent to return can be demonstrated. It is crucial to provide a comprehensive employment certificate from your employer stating your position, salary, start date, and, importantly, approximate dates of leave/vacation and confirmation that you are expected to return to work. This documentation aims to reassure immigration officials of your non-immigrant intent, regardless of the remote nature of your work.
  • When asked for 'significant dates' on an Australian visa application and traveling purely for tourism, state 'No significant dates. I am travelling for tourism purposes only.' Ensure the duration of your travel plan aligns with the declared stay. Previous visa history, even from childhood, must be disclosed; attempts to conceal it are not advised.
  • If a visa agent (even a recommended one) experiences prolonged payment processing issues (e.g., over a week) for a visa application fee, and refuses to provide access to the application portal or accept alternative payment methods, it is a red flag. Applicants should immediately request login credentials for their application account to verify status and payment directly. Report such issues to relevant authorities or community administrators if the agent was recommended.
  • If a passport is renewed after visa submission, the VEVO status may still show the old passport. To update: contact the Department of Home Affairs via email or phone, stating the urgency. Updates generally take 24-48 hours. The visa grant number remains the same; the system verifies entry via the new passport details.
  • Gold Coast, Australia, is a significant film production hub, with Hollywood films frequently shot in the city and on Main Beach. Post-COVID, the city experienced a substantial population increase due to internal migration from Melbourne and Sydney, leading to a doubling of house prices. The area also sees whale migration, offering opportunities for whale watching tours during the season.
  • For a tourist visa application to Australia, indicating hostels instead of hotels in the itinerary is generally not an issue. The focus is on the overall travel plan rather than the specific type of accommodation, as long as it aligns with the purpose of the visit. It is also permissible to mention camping in a tent as part of the accommodation plan.
  • When applying for an Australian visa, especially a Tourist Stream visa (subclass 600), ensure you provide comprehensive documentation to demonstrate your genuine intention to visit Australia temporarily and your ties to your home country. Key documents typically include: proof of employment (payslips, employment contract, leave approval), financial capacity (bank statements showing sufficient funds), property ownership (deeds, titles), vehicle registration, and details of close family members. If you have previously been refused a visa or have migration issues (e.g., visa refusals for other countries like the USA), this must be disclosed. It's crucial to provide accurate and complete information, as misrepresentation can lead to visa refusal.
  • The APEC Business Travel Card (ABTC) does not typically grant visa-free entry to Australia for tourism purposes. Australia generally does not approve tourism-related ABTC applications. Entry is usually restricted to business purposes and requires prior arrangement with Australian authorities. Check with the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs for specific details regarding ABTC applicability for Russian citizens.
  • For Australian Subclass 600 tourist visas, there is no fixed rule regarding the required time to spend outside Australia between visits (unlike the '3 months in, 3 months out' rule often cited for other countries). Frequent short visits (e.g., leaving for 5 days and returning) are generally permissible, provided the cumulative stay does not violate the total visa conditions.
  • Previous immigration and travel history is considered in Australian visa applications. However, the weight given to this factor can vary, and outcomes are not always predictable. While extensive travel history can be beneficial, its absence does not guarantee refusal, and strong travel records do not ensure approval. Applicants should provide all required documentation and may consider a cover letter to explain their intentions.
  • Australian visa application requirements: All documents (2-NDFL, bank statements, ownership papers, birth/marriage certificates) must be translated into English. Electronic signatures on bank statements and tax documents are accepted. For business owners/sole traders (IP) in the visa form, if a 'supervisor' contact is required, the applicant can use their own contact information or a deputy's details.
  • To apply for an Australian visa, understand the biometric submission process. You have a 14-day window to complete biometrics after submitting your application and paying the visa fee. The referral for biometrics is typically issued immediately after payment. Appointment slots are generally available, even on short notice. Therefore, it's advisable to initiate the visa application and payment process first, then schedule your biometrics appointment within the given timeframe, rather than waiting for fixed travel dates.
  • Australian visitor visas (subclass 600) typically allow multiple entries with a maximum stay of 3 months per visit within a 12-month validity period. The visa expiration date indicates the last day to enter the country, not the last day to stay. Repeatedly staying for 3 months and leaving for short periods can lead to suspicion of non-tourist activities and potential future visa denials.
  • If a group application was intended, but one member holds a non-Russian passport (e.g., Estonian), they may need to apply for a different visa subclass (e.g., eVisitor 651 if eligible, or Visitor visa 600) based on their citizenship. Even with dual citizenship, the visa must be obtained based on the passport used for entry into Australia. Check specific eligibility criteria for each visa type.
  • Form 54 (Family Composition) is part of the Australian visa application process. It requires listing family members and can be completed and signed without a witness. Family photos are not required. Ensure all information provided is accurate.
  • If you are on the document upload page of your Australian visa application and realize you need to edit previous sections, use the 'Back' button (usually located on the left side of the screen) to navigate to the desired section. You can then make corrections and proceed through the application steps again.
  • For Australian visa applications, payment of the visa application charge (VAC) typically requires a valid international credit or debit card. Cards issued by certain Belarusian banks may be subject to sanctions or restrictions. Commonly accepted methods include cards from major international networks (Visa, Mastercard) issued by banks in countries like Kazakhstan or through services that facilitate international payments (e.g., 'ПлатиПоМиру'). Always check the official Australian Department of Home Affairs website or consult with a reliable migration agent for the most up-to-date payment options and accepted card types.
  • Australian visa application submitted on April 22nd, began processing on June 4th. Status in the personal account shows 'further assessment', which is a normal status indicating thorough review. Applicants with similar submission dates are receiving responses. Expect a reply within a couple of business days, possibly with a request for additional documents.
  • Russian bank cards, including Union Pay cards issued in Russia, cannot be used to pay for visa application fees on immi.gov.au. This also applies to payments made via the official Union Pay app if it is linked to a Russian card. General Union Pay card acceptance for payments in Australia might also be problematic.
  • To save an Australian visa application at any stage, navigate to a previous page if the 'save' button is not visible on the current one. This ensures progress is not lost.
  • For an Australian tourist visa (subclass 600), financial solvency is crucial. An applicant should provide a bank statement showing the available balance. While some banks' balance certificates may not show the account opening date (e.g., Alfa Bank's 'Available Balance' statement), immigration officers assess the source of funds. If an applicant has no official employment, they must clearly explain the origin of their money and how the trip is financed. Registering as self-employed just before application, while presenting a long history of transactions, may appear suspicious to visa officers as they may infer the funds were borrowed, indicating insufficient financial standing for travel.
  • Australian Visitor Visa (subclass 600) processing time is calculated from the date of the visa fee payment, not the biometrics appointment. Notification of approval or refusal is sent via email. Applicants are permitted to travel internationally while waiting, provided they applied from outside Australia.
  • Once the visa application fee is paid, individual applications cannot be combined. If traveling with companions, it is necessary to either apply as a group before payment or for each individual to submit a separate application. Additional documents can be attached post-payment, but the application itself cannot be merged. To correct errors after submission, use the 'Update details' and 'Notification of incorrect answer(s)' functions within ImmiAccount.
  • Australian passport renewal applications submitted online, despite an advertised 72-hour processing time, can take up to 1.5 months due to human processing. Mass delays have been reported on platforms like Reddit. Checking the website status frequently is generally ineffective. To potentially expedite the process, applicants can send a letter via the feedback form on the official website. A confirmation email is sent upon the successful attachment of the new passport.
  • If your Australian visa application status indicates 'additional supporting information required,' it is essential to check your ImmiAccount for any specific requests. This could be for documents beyond the standard biometric collection. Review all communications and uploaded documents carefully to identify any missing items or discrepancies.
  • After completing the Australian visa application form, applicants can review all details before uploading supporting documents. It is recommended to provide travel history for the last 10 years, and include significant earlier visas (e.g., US visas) if available, as this can strengthen the application.
  • When entering a Russian mobile number in an Australian visa application form, use the international country code '7'. If the form does not allow '7' as the first digit, ensure you are entering it in the correct format (e.g., after a '+' symbol, or in a dedicated country code field), or use '7' and then the 10-digit number without the leading '8' used for domestic calls.
  • It is possible to obtain an Australian visa even with a passport that lacks previous visas from countries like the USA, UK, or Schengen Area. The key factors considered are your ties to your home country, including stable employment, property ownership, and family relationships.
  • Australian visas are primarily electronic. While a physical stamp in the passport is not mandatory, it can be requested from a border control officer at designated points, typically located near automated border gates. Availability may vary.
  • When applying for an Australian visa as a family (e.g., two adults and a small child traveling together), it is acceptable to submit one common cover letter for the entire family application, as you are traveling as a unit.
  • Pregnant travelers are permitted to enter Australia; giving birth in the country does not grant the child automatic citizenship. Airlines typically require a medical certificate for travel after 26-28 weeks of gestation.
  • When filling out Australian visa applications for multiple individuals using a single primary account (e.g., for friends or family), each applicant's individual email address should be used for their respective sections or confirmations. While the main account might be registered under one person's email, subsequent steps, such as email confirmations or receiving visa information, should be directed to the specific email address designated for each applicant. This ensures that each individual receives relevant communications directly.
  • When an Australian visa application asks 'Do you plan to visit Australia again?' and the exact dates are unknown, it is acceptable to state 'Yes' and provide an approximate timeframe, explaining that the specific dates will be determined by future vacation schedules (e.g., 'Yes, I plan to visit in February 2027, subject to my future vacation schedule').
  • If your Australian visa application is refused, you can reapply immediately after addressing the reason for refusal. A common refusal reason (600.211) is insufficient evidence of ties to the home country. Ensure to provide strong evidence of your intention to return, such as employment, property ownership, or rental agreements, in addition to financial documents.
  • For individuals employed without a formal contract, receiving salary payments via an Individual Entrepreneur (IP) to a personal card, and lacking self-employed status, proving employment for Australian visa applications can be challenging. Official employment contracts and tax filings are the standard. Potential documentation includes GitLab screenshots showing consistent project work and bank statements with regular income deposits. Alternatively, explain the situation in the 'Other' section of the application, but be prepared for the applicant to be officially classified as 'unemployed' if formal employment cannot be substantiated.
  • When applying for an Australian visa with a travel companion, you can indicate this in the 'accompanying person' section of the application form. Alternatively, you can mention it in a cover letter. Each individual must submit their own application and supporting documents.
  • If, after submitting biometrics for an Australian visa, the online application status shows a generic 'additional information needed' message, but no specific request has been received via email, it is generally standard system behavior. Applicants should wait for an official email notification requesting specific documents or information before taking further action.
  • The status 'Requesting Visitor visa' typically appears in the 'Messages' section of the Australian visa application portal. To view the status related to biometrics collection, navigate to the 'Biometrics collection' section on the left-hand side of the portal.
  • For Australian tourist visas, formal marriage is not always a strict requirement for sponsorship or demonstrating ties. An unmarried partner remaining in the home country can act as a tie. Sponsorship documentation from a non-relative partner, including a sponsorship letter, employment certificate, and bank statements, can strengthen the application. While official documentation of the relationship may be limited, a cover letter and any available joint photos can be included.
  • For an Australian visa application, showing sufficient funds is crucial. 200,000 RUB is generally considered insufficient for a 20-day trip to Australia (minimum estimated 2500 AUD for 10-14 days, not including flights). A sudden large deposit of funds (e.g., an additional 200,000 RUB) just before applying can raise suspicion, as visa officers prefer to see regular income and consistent financial activity over 3-6 months. If funds are limited, it's advisable to shorten the proposed trip duration in the itinerary (e.g., 12-15 days for a solo traveler, 7 days for a couple). Alternatively, a large sum can be placed into a savings/deposit account with a balance certificate, but it's still recommended to also provide bank statements showing regular transaction history. Some applicants have successfully submitted simplified bank statements showing only 'received/spent/balance' without detailed transactions.
  • When applying for an Australian tourist visa (subclass 600) as a de facto couple, honesty and clear documentation are key. While not strictly required for this visa type, providing evidence of your de facto relationship, such as joint travel history (photos, passport stamps) and a joint cover letter, can be beneficial. Focus on demonstrating your ties to your home country through employment, property (even rental agreements if applicable), and a well-articulated travel plan. Emphasize your intention to return home, which is a crucial factor for visa approval.
  • Visa applications for minors attending Australian language schools are typically processed faster than standard tourist visas. While biometrics may be collected on separate dates, visas are often granted concurrently and within a shorter timeframe.
  • Russian domestic passports are not required for Australian visa applications. Discrepancies in name transliteration between bank statements and passports can be resolved by editing the document PDF or by adding an explanation in the cover letter.
  • Visa application status updates for Australia are found in the 'Messages' section of the application portal. The 'Biometrics collection' section on the left side specifically relates to the status of biometric data submission and processing.
  • A family from Kazakhstan applied for Australian tourist visas (subclass 600) to visit their son studying in Melbourne. The husband, as a sponsor, provided proof of significant financial assets (over 200,000 AUD), real estate, and an existing business. They have another minor child remaining in Kazakhstan. The husband was granted a 1-year multi-entry visa (max 3 months stay) in 7 days. The wife was refused on day 6 (before biometrics) due to the visa officer's doubt about her intention to return to her home country, despite a strong travel history. This case suggests that even substantial financial proof can be misinterpreted as an intent to migrate, leading to refusal for one applicant while the other (sponsor) is approved. Key advice for re-application: strongly emphasize ties to the home country for the refused applicant, including family dependents (minor child staying home), business involvement, long-term commitments, and elderly parents. Ensure precise and consistent information, and review applications filled by third parties thoroughly. Apostille of documents is not required for Australian visa applications.
  • It is illegal to work in Australia on a tourist visa. To find a sponsoring employer, individuals should first consult the Australian Skill Occupation List to determine if their profession (e.g., KIPiA engineer) is in demand and eligible for skilled migration visas. Then, research specific visa subclasses (e.g., skilled visas, employer-sponsored visas) and their requirements. Resources like the T-Journal article (t-j.ru/move-to-australia) can provide a general overview, though specific details should be verified on official government immigration websites. Direct approaches to employers or recruiters after understanding visa pathways are recommended.
  • It is possible to update information and upload additional documents for an Australian visa application after initial submission, up until the application status reaches 'finalizes'. This feature allows applicants to correct errors or provide supplementary information.
  • Australian 600 visa processing times are unpredictable and should never be used as a basis for booking non-refundable travel. Processing typically takes 4-5 weeks from the date of submission, not biometrics. Standard recommendation: wait for the visa grant letter before purchasing flight tickets or paying for courses. Using local visa agents does not guarantee faster processing or success.
  • For Australian visa applications, ensure consistency in your name across all documents. While minor variations like 'Anastasiia' and 'Anastasia' might be acceptable, the name on your biometric appointment and application form must match your passport exactly. If discrepancies are found, it's crucial to address them before your biometric submission.
  • For the Australian Tourist Visa (subclass 600), ensure you provide the necessary documentation. A bank statement covering three months is generally sufficient, and a separate 'balance' statement is not required if the balance is clearly shown. An employment record is not mandatory for unemployed applicants; focus on other supporting documents. The Russian internal passport is not considered a valid form of identification for visa application purposes. If a sponsor is involved, the sponsorship letter should be attached to the application of the sponsored individual.
  • For an Australian tourist visa (subclass 600), the applicant's travel history (or 'empty passport') is less critical than demonstrating strong ties to their home country. Immigration officers prioritize evidence that the applicant intends to return. Key factors include stable employment (which serves as a significant tie), verifiable financial solvency, and family connections. If officially unemployed or with unclear sources of income, the applicant must provide a clear and comprehensive explanation of their financial situation and demonstrate other compelling reasons for their return. Transparency and strong evidence of ties to the home country are paramount to a successful application.
  • When paying the Australian visa application fee with a card that is not yours, ensure that the 'card holder address' field correctly reflects the address associated with the cardholder. Using a local address for the card (e.g., Kazakh address for a Kazakh card) can prevent payment issues.
  • An Australian visa cover letter should primarily focus on strong ties to the applicant's home country and the genuine purpose of the visit, rather than simply listing past travels (which are visible in visa scans; visas from US, UK, Canada, NZ are generally more impactful than Schengen). Key elements to include: details about family remaining in the home country (e.g., elderly parents, minor children, pets), stable employment, property ownership, or business interests. Use the letter to clarify any ambiguities in submitted documents (e.g., absence of leave dates in an employment certificate, reason for choosing budget accommodation). The letter serves to assure the visa officer of the applicant's intent to return.
  • Australian tourist visa application timeline: current average processing time is approximately 4-6 weeks from submission. Key points: 1. Application submission date triggers the review process, not the biometric appointment date. 2. Biometrics must be submitted within 14 days of application. 3. Proof of funds: regular salary deposits are more important than large one-time deposits. 4. Supporting documents: marriage certificates are considered 'supporting ID', not 'primary ID'. 5. Car rental: most international agencies require a credit card with the driver's name embossed on it.
  • For Australian biometrics appointments, applicants should bring: 1. International passport. 2. Russian passport (or a copy if the original is unavailable, some centers can print from Gosuslugi if forgotten). 3. Printed biometrics appointment letter (the first page is often sufficient, but having the full document is recommended). It's advisable to have digital and physical copies of all essential documents. Although not mandatory, a positive attitude is generally helpful.
  • Regarding Australian visa 600 applications: The 'Confirm arrival/attachment' button in the ImmiAccount after submitting biometrics has no technical impact on the application processing time or the officer's assessment. Status changes such as 'Initial assessment' to 'Further assessment' happen automatically; no manual action by the applicant is required or recommended. Processing times vary widely; check standard processing stats rather than relying on UI button clicks.
  • When applying for an Australian visa as a group, applications are often processed independently. There is no guarantee that the second applicant's processing time will be linked to the first, and sometimes the second application may even be processed faster.
  • After submitting your Australian visa application and attending the biometric appointment, it is crucial to click the 'I confirm' button at the bottom of the document list. This action, while not impacting the visa outcome or processing time, serves to confirm completion. If no additional requests for documents are received via email, assume your application is proceeding as normal. For biometric appointments, if the earliest available date seems unusually far in the future, it may indicate a system glitch. Try accessing the booking system later or from a different device, as immediate or next-day appointments are often available.
  • When filling out the Australian visa application, a TRN (Transaction Reference Number) may be requested for a child. Be prepared to provide this information.
  • Visa officers (case managers) for Australian 600 visas prioritize consistent income over sporadic account balances. Key factors: verifiable official income, ties to home country (family, work), and a logical narrative in the cover letter. Avoid 'padding' accounts right before applying. Recommended savings should align with estimated trip duration (approx. 250 AUD per person/day). Form 54 is often used for additional details.
  • When applying for an Australian visa with multiple valid passports, scan and upload all passports that contain relevant visas or stamps. The electronic visa will be linked to one passport, but providing details of all valid passports is acceptable and may be required in the application form.
  • Visa refusals for subclass 600 often cite clause 600.211 (lack of genuine temporary stay intent). Key factors leading to refusal include a lack of onshore contacts and insufficient proof of prior visa compliance in Australia or countries with similar immigration legislation.
  • If travel insurance is not a mandatory visa requirement, any policy that suits the applicant's needs and budget is acceptable. It is strongly advised not to purchase insurance before the visa is granted to avoid unnecessary expenses in case of visa denial.
  • When booking Australian visa biometrics via VFS, multiple applicants can be added to a single appointment slot. It is recommended to choose 'pay at center' options to avoid potential fee issues with third-party sites. In some locations, walk-in appointments might be available for an additional fee (approx. 3800 RUB), and payment policies vary by local VFS center.
  • Credit card balances are considered in Australian visa applications. While not considered part of liquid assets like savings accounts, they can contribute to demonstrating overall financial capacity. It is advisable to include credit card statements if they reflect responsible credit management.
  • For the Australian 600 visa, document organization is crucial for processing efficiency. Recommend applicants to categorize files: 1) Passport bio page (main scan), 2) Travel history (stamped pages from the last 10 years), 3) Visas (separate file for all past significant visas). Empty pages are unnecessary. Consistency in document layout and clear filenames are advised for visa officer convenience. Do not include domestic identification cards (like internal Russian passports) unless specifically required.
  • After paying for the visa application, a request for biometrics is generated. The applicant must check the messages section in their ImmiAccount and their email. Upon receiving the notification, the applicant must independently book an appointment at the nearest VFS Global center. Biometrics and medical examinations usually have a 14-day completion deadline from the request date.
