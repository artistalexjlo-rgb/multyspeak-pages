Transport in Australia: what actually matters to know

Real experience of people from chats on «Transport» in Australia — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • The Myki youth card for Melbourne public transport can be purchased at 7-Eleven stores or train stations. In the CBD's free tram zone, tapping on/off with Myki is not required, but it is advised to tap on/off when traveling outside this zone to ensure correct fare calculation. The free tram zone boundaries are generally well-marked within the city center.
  • For Australian visa applications, check if the reference number type can be changed within the application form, as there might be a dropdown menu for selection.
  • A transit visa for Australia is intended for people passing through the country to a third destination. Transit passengers do pass through immigration clearance, and exiting the airport for a short period is generally permitted. However, cruising (especially round-trip cruises starting and ending in Australia) does not qualify as transit. For complex itineraries like self-transfers to a cruise, a standard visitor visa is highly recommended to avoid entry denial.
  • While public transport is limited in Tasmania, it is possible to explore the island without a rental car by utilizing one-day organized tours departing from Hobart. Examples include tours to Bruny Island, Mt Wellington (via tourist bus), and Freycinet Park/Wineglass Bay. Although a car offers more freedom, structured day tours provide a viable option for visiting key attractions across Tasmania.
  • Public transport in Brisbane accepts standard bank cards for payment. For Go Card top-ups, service windows are available at Central station. For bank account opening, major banks like ANZ or Suncorp generally do not require specific branch location; walk-ins are often accepted, though checking for appointment requirements is recommended.
  • Australia is a highly cashless society. Foreign cards are often insufficient for certain services like public transport top-ups or specific hotel bookings. Creating a local bank account (e.g., Westpac, ANZ) and linking it to Apple Pay is recommended for tourists. International bank transfers from CIS countries to Australian accounts are possible but can take 2+ days to process.
  • Not all roads in Australia are toll roads. Specifically, there are no toll roads in Western Australia. Users should check regional specifics regarding toll roads when planning travel.
  • When renting a car in Australia, third-party aggregators often allow prepayment with diverse card types, while the final rental contract is settled at the pickup location. Comprehensive insurance ('full coverage') is highly recommended to avoid high costs for minor damages. Essential refueling rule: return the vehicle with a full tank filled within 15 km of the drop-off point to avoid significant surcharges.
  • In Sydney, Australia, parking signs with '1/2P' and specified hours indicate a time limit of 30 minutes. Payment is typically made via a mobile app. If specific days like 'Sunday' are not listed on the sign, parking may be permitted until the next business day. Always verify local parking regulations to avoid fines.
  • When booking domestic flights in Australia, be aware of potential issues with third-party sites like Trip.com, which may show fluctuating prices. Direct booking on airline websites (e.g., Jetstar, Virgin Australia) is often recommended, especially if you have more than just a small carry-on bag. Check baggage allowances carefully, as airlines strictly enforce carry-on weight and size limits, which can lead to unexpected fees. Online check-in is typically available 24 hours before departure on airline websites. For payments and potential refunds, using a reliable card provider is advisable, as some third-party booking agents have a history of slow refund processes.
  • Car rental in Australia requires the primary driver to have a credit or debit card in their own name. Some companies like Hertz accept debit cards; check aggregators for specific options. Additional drivers can be added for a fee. Driving is left-hand traffic with right-hand drive vehicles. Traffic in Melbourne and Victoria is generally calm, while Sydney and New South Wales are busier with rougher roads. Be aware of significant fines for even minor speeding violations; a car with a speed limiter is recommended. Australian road rules regarding intersection priority differ from some other countries: priority is primarily determined by 'Give Way' signs and road markings. The 'give way to the right' rule is applied very selectively at specific uncontrolled intersections, not as a universal principle.
  • Renting a car in Australia with a recently issued driver's license is generally not an issue. While international driving permits are recommended, national licenses are often accepted. Rental companies prioritize having a valid license and a foreign-issued payment card.
  • When renting a car in Australia: 1. **Driver's License:** Ensure your driver's license is valid and meets the minimum age/experience requirements (often 1 year of driving experience). Expired foreign licenses are generally not accepted for driving, though they might be used to demonstrate driving history. Licenses with a clear English translation or an International Driving Permit (IDP) are usually required. 2. **Rental Deposits:** Be prepared for rental car deposits, which can range from AUD $100 to $1,000 or more. These are typically pre-authorized on a credit card and refunded after the vehicle is returned in good condition. Companies like Apex and Europcar are noted for sometimes having no or minimal deposits. Always inquire about deposit policies and the associated refund timelines.
  • Users have successfully rented cars in Australian cities like Melbourne and Canberra using a foreign debit card. While some rental services historically preferred credit cards for deposits, it appears many now accept debit cards. It is advisable to clarify acceptance with the specific rental company when booking, but personal experience indicates it is possible.
  • In Queensland, Australia, public transport fares are significantly subsidized, with most journeys costing only AUD $0.50. This fare applies to buses, trams, and some trains. However, exceptions exist, such as the AirTrain service from Brisbane Airport and certain ferries or island bus routes, which may incur higher standard fares.
  • Driving in Australia requires strict adherence to traffic laws. Exceeding the speed limit, even slightly, can result in substantial fines. Seatbelt use is mandatory for all occupants. Mobile phone use while driving is prohibited. Proper signaling at roundabouts is essential. Be cautious when driving at night, especially in Tasmania, due to wildlife presence.
  • In Australia, parking signage and vehicle registration plates can be confusing. A common issue arises from the similarity between the letter 'I' and the numeral '1'. While 'I' may be used on personalized plates, general issue plates often use '1' to avoid ambiguity. Ensure you correctly identify and input your license plate number, paying close attention to potentially confusing characters, when paying for parking to avoid fines. Fines can be substantial ($70-$100 AUD for minor offenses, potentially more).
  • Car rental in Australia, including Tasmania, is often possible with recently obtained national driver's licenses, even without a separate translation. The primary requirements are a valid license and a foreign-issued payment card. Rental agencies may inquire about the license issue date but are often flexible.
  • When flying with Jetstar or similar carriers with a domestic connection (e.g., Melbourne to Hobart), check the baggage policy at the initial check-in. If both flights are on a single ticket, baggage is often checked through to the final destination, but customs/immigration procedures usually take place at the first point of entry (e.g., Melbourne). It is advisable to allow at least 2 hours for international-to-domestic transfers.
  • Australian tourist visa application timeline: current average processing time is approximately 4-6 weeks from submission. Key points: 1. Application submission date triggers the review process, not the biometric appointment date. 2. Biometrics must be submitted within 14 days of application. 3. Proof of funds: regular salary deposits are more important than large one-time deposits. 4. Supporting documents: marriage certificates are considered 'supporting ID', not 'primary ID'. 5. Car rental: most international agencies require a credit card with the driver's name embossed on it.
  • When renting a car in Australia, consider the following: payment methods (debit vs. credit card), deposit requirements, and insurance options. It is advisable to inquire about these details with the rental company beforehand. For example, one user successfully rented a car in Melbourne through Avis using a Kazakhstan debit card, opting for full insurance and using their driver's license for verification without prior prepayment.
  • For car rentals in Australia, the duration of the driver's license validity is generally not a primary concern for rental companies. The main requirements are a valid license (national or international permits are accepted) and a foreign-issued credit card for payment.
  • Renting a motorhome in Tasmania for 4-5 days can be worthwhile. Parking for motorhomes is available, though specific reserved spots may not always be apparent. For vehicles up to 3.5 tons, a Category B driver's license is typically sufficient. It's advisable to confirm license requirements with the rental company based on the specific motorhome's weight.
  • At Gold Coast Airport (OOL), passengers first use self-service terminals for ticket registration or baggage drop if they haven't checked in online. If already checked in online with only carry-on, print your boarding pass at a self-service machine. After this, proceed directly to security screening. Boarding passes are typically scanned only at the gate in the clean zone, not prior to security by staff or electronic gates.
  • Historically, many car rental services required credit cards, especially for deposit authorization. While this requirement is less common now, and debit cards are increasingly accepted, it is crucial to verify the specific card acceptance policy directly with the rental company when booking, as this information may not always be readily available.
  • In Australia, daily car rental costs for an SUV can range from approximately 240-260 AUD. Sedans are generally cheaper. Advise users to consider these price points when budgeting for car rentals.
  • When renting a car in Australia, advise users that the quoted rental price often does not include additional mandatory costs. These typically include fuel, delivery fees, route charges, mileage fees, and insurance coverage. Users should budget for these extra expenses beyond the base rental rate.
  • In Australian cities, the general speed limit is 50 km/h unless otherwise indicated. School zones typically have a limit of 40 km/h. Outside of urban areas, speed limits range from 70-80 km/h. Exceeding these limits can result in fines of AUD 500-1000. While not resulting in imprisonment for minor speeding offenses, the financial penalties are significant. Adherence to speed limits contributes to safe driving conditions.
