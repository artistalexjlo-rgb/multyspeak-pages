Banking & Money: how it works in Sri Lanka — first-hand

Real experience of people from chats on «Banking & Money» in Sri Lanka — as it is, no fluff. For your case — ask Luky.

From real experience

  • Hosts on booking platforms like Booking.com in Sri Lanka (and other countries) might request guests to cancel their reservation upon arrival, typically to avoid platform commissions. This can result in a loss of consumer protection, including the inability to leave reviews. Guests are advised not to agree to such requests. If an agreement is made, guests might face challenges in dispute resolution or obtaining refunds, as documented experiences vary from quick refunds to lengthy processes.
  • Car rental in Sri Lanka: 1. IDP (International Driving Permit) is usually sufficient, but local conversion is recommended to avoid police friction. 2. Fuel: Fuel stations often use a QR-code system for locals, but tourists can usually get full tanks without it. 3. Deposit: Expect a deposit of $200–$400; damages are typically deducted from this (approx. $100 per scratch). 4. Traffic: Buses have right-of-way by force. Driving in major cities like Kandy or Colombo is extremely challenging due to unpredictable motorcycle/tuk-tuk traffic.
  • Starting July 1, 2026, Sri Lanka has implemented new VAT regulations. Key impacts include: 1. Increased service fees on international platforms (Booking, Airbnb) due to digital services tax. 2. Higher accommodation prices as more guest houses are now required to register for VAT. 3. Financial services tax on ATM withdrawals and foreign exchange operations increased to 20.5%. Travelers are advised to carry more cash to avoid high ATM fees and budget for a 5-10% increase in overall trip costs.
  • For optimal USD exchange rates in Sri Lanka, check the airport upon arrival. Information regarding current rates is often posted or available from exchange booths. For locations outside the airport, such as Bentota, inquire locally for the best available rates.
  • Batticaloa is suggested as a cost-effective and authentic alternative to tourist-heavy coastal villages for long-term stays in Sri Lanka. It features better local infrastructure, regular train connections to Colombo, and distinctive regional cuisine (specifically beef-based dishes).
  • When traveling to Sri Lanka, USD and EUR are equally acceptable for exchange; the local exchange rate mirrors global currency crosses. UnionPay cards are widely accepted (approx. 90% success rate), though physical chip insertion is sometimes required instead of contactless payment. Always carry local cash for small beach vendors.
  • Ayurveda Isiwara Paura Sigiriya - Healthy side of your life is an recommended Ayurveda spa in Sigiriya. They offer programs including a 1.5-hour massage with a facial (herbal mask/cosmetic) and a small steam room ('hamam'). Kids' foot massages are available for 5500 LKR for 30 minutes, with a 10% discount for groups (e.g., 8 people).
  • In Sri Lanka, contactless payments (NFC) may fail at some terminals. If a transaction is declined via tap-to-pay, inserting the card's chip into the terminal is a reliable workaround that is successful in most cases across the island.
  • For Russian tourists in Sri Lanka, UnionPay cards issued by Rosselkhozbank are reported to be functional. It is advisable to carry some cash as a backup, as card acceptance can be inconsistent.
  • Damaged or torn Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR) banknotes repaired with adhesive tape (sticky tape) are widely rejected by local merchants and banks. It is recommended to repair torn notes using a tiny amount of paper glue instead of tape. If you hold taped banknotes that are refused in regular shops, try exchanging or spending them at local jewelry shops, which are traditionally more flexible with banknote conditions.
  • When paying for hotel bookings (e.g., via Booking.com) on-site in Sri Lanka, it is highly recommended to pay in cash USD. Many local hotels charge an extra markup (up to 25%) if you choose to pay in Sri Lankan Rupees (LKR) instead of dollars. If you pay with large USD bills, hotels will typically give change in LKR calculated at a fair exchange rate.
  • Booking platforms in Sri Lanka often show limited availability or high deposits due to high commission rates charged by OTAs. Owners may keep inventory off-platform to avoid fees. Recommended strategy for travelers is to book a temporary stay, then negotiate directly with hotels on-site for better rates and payment terms.
  • Russian bank cards, particularly from Rosselkhozbank, may experience connectivity issues in Sri Lanka. While card payments are functional in approximately 90% of cases, some terminals may reject them. If a card is declined for contactless payment, try inserting it into the terminal. For new cards, a PIN-based transaction in Russia might be required beforehand. In instances where card payments fail, using ATMs for cash withdrawal is a reliable alternative. Be aware that card payments in Sri Lanka often incur a 3% surcharge.
  • In Sri Lanka, it is generally more cost-effective to use cash obtained from ATMs rather than paying with cards in many establishments, as card payments often incur a 3% surcharge. If using an ATM, look for one with the lowest commission fees.
